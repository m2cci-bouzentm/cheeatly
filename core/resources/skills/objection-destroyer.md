## When to Use

- A prospect says "I need to think about it," "I need to talk to my spouse," or "I cannot afford it" and you need a specific overcome
- Conversions are lower than expected and you suspect unaddressed objections are killing deals
- You want to build a reusable objection-handling playbook grounded in the CLOSER framework's Explain step

## Core Framework: There Are Only Three Real Objections

From Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6 (the Explain step of the CLOSER formula, forged from over 4,000 one-on-one closes):

Every sales objection falls into one of three categories:

1. **Stalls** (Circumstance -- "I need to think about it")
2. **Decision-Maker** (Circumstance -- "I need to talk to my husband/wife/partner")
3. **Price** (Real -- "I cannot afford it")

Everything else -- product-specific concerns like "I have a bad back" or "I am not sure this works for my industry" -- are **non-obstacles**. They are questions from people who still want to buy. Answer them casually and move on.

> "If you are constantly battling people, the problem is earlier in the process, not at the close." -- Gym Launch Secrets

The foundational insight: people do not stall because they do not want your product. They stall because they are afraid of making a wrong decision. Not deciding IS a decision -- the decision to stay exactly where they are.

---

## The Universal Pattern: Always Agree, Then Ask a Question

Before using ANY close, apply this pattern from Gym Launch Secrets:

> "Every obstacle overcome should start with 'I totally agree' or 'I totally understand,' then transition to 'Let me ask you a question...' Never say 'but' or 'I disagree.' The prospect needs to conclude on their own that they should proceed -- otherwise they will feel pressured."

**Do this:** "I totally understand it is a lot of money. Let me ask you -- have you ever invested in something that changed your life?"

**Never do this:** "But here is why you are wrong." / "I disagree, let me tell you why."

Stack overcomes like a punching combination -- each one dents the prospect's apprehension. Master 2-3 overcomes for each of the three categories.

---

## The Belief-Breaking Formula

A five-step persuasion framework from Gym Launch Secrets (picked up from Linh Trinh) for shattering a prospect's false beliefs and replacing them with new ones aligned with your solution:

1. **Say what they believe** -- "So you believe cheap front-end offers work?"
2. **Say why it is wrong** -- "This does not provide cash flow to market or acquire customers"
3. **Say what is right** -- "Use a high-ticket front-end acquisition"
4. **Say WHY it is right** -- "It will allow you to spend whatever you need to get people in profitably"
5. **Show proof** -- Third-party stats, testimonials, wall of results behind you

Use this formula within the Overview step of the CLOSER framework when a prospect's objection reveals a deeper false belief about themselves, their market, or their past failures. The formula does not argue -- it replaces the old mental model with a better one backed by evidence. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6)

## Guardrail: Hard Selling Is For Weak Products

Before deploying any of the techniques below, internalize this principle from $100M Money Models:

> "Hard selling is for weak products. If someone doesn't want something, that's OK." -- $100M Money Models

The system works by having the RIGHT offer at the RIGHT time, not by pressuring people. If you find yourself needing aggressive closes on most prospects, the problem is your offer, your targeting, or earlier steps in the CLOSER sequence -- not your closing skills.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Classify the Objection

Ask: which of the three categories does this objection fall into?

| Category | What they say | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| **Stall** | "I need to think about it" / "Not right now" / "Let me sleep on it" | They are afraid of making a mistake, not genuinely undecided |
| **Decision-Maker** | "I need to talk to my husband/wife" / "My business partner decides" | Either a real constraint or a stall disguised as a constraint |
| **Price** | "I cannot afford it" / "It is too expensive" / "I do not have the budget" | Either a real financial constraint or a prioritization issue |

If the objection is a product-specific concern (industry fit, feature question, capability doubt), it is a **non-obstacle**. Answer it directly and return to the close.

### Step 2: Apply the Always-Agree Pattern

Validate their concern before doing anything else:
- "I totally understand..."
- "I totally agree..."
- "That makes complete sense..."

Then transition: "Let me ask you a question..."

### Step 3: Deploy the Right Named Close

#### For Stalls ("I need to think about it")

**The Rocking Chair Close** (Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter):
> "When we say 'I am going to think about it,' we do not go home, sit in our rocking chairs, and stare at the wall, do we? You are going to leave here, then your mind is going to get filled with buying groceries and cooking dinner and doing laundry... and four days later you will catch a glimpse in the mirror, remember why you came, and think 'I am too busy for that right now.' In those two seconds, you will have made the decision."

The principle: "Decisions don't take time, they take information, because thinking is instantaneous." Your job is to help them confront the decision with the right information NOW. (Gym Launch Secrets)

**The Fully Informed Decision Frame** (Gym Launch Secrets):
> "I'm not asking you to decide yes or no today...I'm asking you to make a fully informed decision, that is all." -- Gym Launch Secrets

Reframe the ask from a commitment to an exploration: "The only way to make a fully informed decision is on the inside, not the outside. Get on the inside, see if everything is true, then decide whether to keep it." Combined with a strong guarantee, this eliminates risk entirely.

**The Unicorn Close** (Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter):

Named in the Bonus chapter alongside the Rocking Chair Close for stalls. When a prospect walks in ready to buy but stalls at the last moment, you risk losing them entirely. The Bonus chapter warns against handing someone a pricing sheet and letting them walk around comparing -- citing Josh who did this with a walk-in unicorn who "looked at the clipboard, looked around, set it down, and was never seen again." Instead, maintain the conversational flow and guide them through the decision.

**The What-If Close** (Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter):

Also listed alongside the Rocking Chair Close for stalls in the Bonus chapter. Use this to help prospects envision the positive outcome by asking "What if this actually works?" questions that shift their focus from fear of the wrong decision to excitement about the potential result.

**Three Decision Questions** (Gym Launch Secrets):
Help them through the decision with three simple questions:
1. "Do you think this program will help you?"
2. "Do you have the money or access to it?"
3. "Am I the person you want supporting you?"

If all three are yes, the decision is made. You are not pressuring -- you are providing the framework they need to decide.

#### For Decision-Maker ("I need to talk to my spouse")

From the Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter (Obstacle Overcomes), use **agreement stacking and delayed payment** for decision-maker objections:

**Agreement Stacking:** First determine if this is real or a disguised stall. Apply the Always-Agree pattern, then stack agreements on smaller points until the prospect has built enough momentum to make the decision. Each "I totally understand" followed by a question that gets a "yes" adds to the stack.

**Delayed Payment:** If the decision-maker constraint is genuinely real, offer to start with a delayed payment structure: "Give people a choice: pay full price later (with a satisfaction guarantee) OR pay a discounted price now with bonuses. Get their card on file first." This combines the benefits of delayed payment and satisfaction guarantee to remove all risk from the customer. (Gym Launch Secrets)

If the decision-maker constraint cannot be resolved (business partner, board approval), schedule a follow-up that includes the decision-maker and use the CLOSER formula from the beginning with both present.

#### For Price ("I cannot afford it")

**The Identity Swap Close** (Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter):

> "Right now what we are talking about is you going from a [person] who is unsatisfied with [their situation] to a [person] who IS satisfied. Part of that new identity comes with new priorities for where you spend your money. We only need to vote YES once, so you might as well do it now."

The principle: "We tend to vote with our dollars about the things we care about. Because we can SAY we care about X, Y, and Z, but what REALLY matters at the end of the day is where we spend our money." (Gym Launch Secrets)

**The Best-Case / Worst-Case Close** ($100M Offers):

> "Best case you get [dream outcome] and [additional benefit]. Worst case you tell me I suck, I write you a check, and you get [X weeks/months] of free [service]. Both options are risk free. But the only thing guaranteed not to help you is walking out of here today."

This requires a strong guarantee to work. If you do not have one, build one first (see guarantee design from $100M Offers Ch.15).

**Barter For Advertising** ($100M Money Models):

When a customer has a genuine price objection, trade a discount for advertising value rather than simply cutting the price:

> "I'll knock $100 off if you: 1) Leave reviews on all review sites, 2) Leave a video testimonial, 3) Make public social posts at beginning, middle, and end showing progress, 4) Introduce me to two friends."

> "To me, the advertising is worth more than the $100 discount. To them, the $100 is worth less than the advertising. Win-win." -- $100M Money Models

This preserves the Never Discount principle because you are trading value for value, not giving the same thing for less.

### Step 4: Temperature Check (Diagnostic After Two Rejected Overcomes)

After two consecutive rejected overcomes, use the Temperature Check from $100M Money Models:

> "On a scale from 1 to 10, how bad do you want this?"

- **If 8+:** Keep going. Say: "Awesome. Don't worry. We're gonna figure out a way to make this happen for you." Then move to downsells.
- **If 7 or below:** Ask "Why not a 10?" or "What would a 10 look like?" Then pivot to a different product or reconfigure features.

> "No payment plan will satisfy a customer who doesn't want the thing. So, make sure the person actually wants your thing before putting more effort into selling it." -- $100M Money Models

### Step 5: Downsell for Price (if Temperature is 8+)

Never discount. Change how they pay or what they get.

**Payment Plan Downsell** (7-step process from $100M Money Models):

1. Reward paying in full (offer a discount for prepayment)
2. Suggest 3rd-party financing, credit card, or layaway
3. Offer half now, half later
4. Temperature check (1-10 scale -- need 8+ to continue)
5. Offer three payments
6. Evenly spread payments aligned with paychecks ("When do you get paid?")
7. Free Trial with Penalty (try free if you meet the terms; pay a fee if you skip requirements)

Frame as rewarding prepayment rather than punishing payment plans. Present the payment-plan price as the base price and offer a discount for paying in full. Same math, totally different psychology.

After each offer, ask: "Deal?" or "Fair enough?" -- people rarely say "no, that is not fair" to a customized offer. ($100M Money Models)

**Seesaw Downselling** ($100M Money Models):

A simplified alternative to the full 7-step process, especially useful with less experienced salespeople:

Ask: "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" They say tiny. Then say: "It normally costs XXX. If you prepay today, you get a huge discount and zero monthly payments. That work?" Then: "The more you put down now, the lower your monthly payments."

> "This frames the payment plan as negative and highlights the benefits of prepaying." -- $100M Money Models

**Feature Downsell** ($100M Money Models):

If they say 7 or below on the Temperature Check after payment plan attempts, pivot to Feature Downsells:

1. Remove the highest-value feature and lower the price a little
2. Ask "How about now?"
3. Remove the next feature and lower the price more
4. Temperature check after two feature downsells

The counterintuitive insight: removing a feature people love and lowering the price only a little makes MORE people take the original full-price offer. They realize what they would lose. Removing features customers hate and lowering the price a lot makes more people take the downsell.

Name your feature combinations: First Class, Business Class, Economy. Name the cheapest tier "The Minimum."

Alternate between feature downsells and payment plan downsells for maximum flexibility.

Additional Feature Downsell principle: "Barter discounts for reviews, testimonials, and referrals" -- use the Barter For Advertising technique within the feature downsell flow as well. ($100M Money Models)

### Step 6: The Assumed Close / ID-to-Card Swap (Transition After Objections Are Overcome)

From Gym Launch Secrets, once objections are handled and the prospect is ready, use the Assumed Close to transition smoothly into payment:

> "Do you have your ID on you?" -- this gets them to take out their wallet (a micro-commitment that prevents the "I forgot my wallet" excuse). Start filling out the contract with their ID information. Then say "Awesome, let me trade you your ID for the payment card you want to use" and hand back the ID while gesturing to their wallet.

> "You want to talk to the lead as if you already know they will accept your offer. Many salespeople refer to this as an assumed close." -- $100M Money Models

The close happens almost invisibly -- you transition from checking their ID to filling out forms to collecting payment in a continuous, casual flow. No dramatic "are you ready to buy?" moment.

### Step 7: Layer Bonuses on Remaining Objections

From $100M Offers Ch.14: After the initial ask, if they say no, match bonuses to their specific objection.

> "Ask for the sale first. If they say yes, bonuses create a wow moment. If they say no, identify their specific objection, present a bonus that directly addresses that objection, then ask again."

Example: Prospect says "I am not sure I can stick with it." You say: "I completely understand. That is exactly why we created our Never Fall Off Accountability System -- it is a $1,000 value. We will include that for you. Does that sound fair enough?"

Repeat with additional bonuses matching additional objections. People have a hard time rejecting reciprocity -- each added bonus creates social obligation.

### Step 8: Preemptive Objection Killing with Guarantees

From $100M Offers Ch.15: A strong guarantee eliminates most objections before they arise.

> "Risk is the single greatest objection. Changing the quality of a guarantee alone can 2-4x conversion."

The guarantee math: 130% of sales with 10% refund rate still nets 23% more revenue than 100% of sales with 5% refund rate. Strong guarantees almost always pay off.

Hormozi's favorite: the Service Guarantee -- "We will keep working with you until you get the result." Zero refund risk. Maximum persuasive power. ($100M Offers Ch.15)

## Key Principles

- **Selling Is Serving** -- the act of convincing someone to invest in themselves IS the service. Not selling them is actually failing them, because you know they need help and you let them walk away unchanged. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Selling Is Serving: Three Transfers** -- every sale requires transferring belief in the product, confidence the person can reach their goals, and conviction they can overcome their circumstances. Without all three, you cannot help them. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5)
- **The Conviction Close** -- your conviction must be stronger than their skepticism. This cannot be faked. It comes from delivering results so many times you have absolute certainty. "You must outwork your self-doubt." Hormozi sold $42,000/year programs to gym owners who made $35,000/year because his conviction was built on 33 successful turnarounds. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Hard Selling Is For Weak Products** -- "If someone doesn't want something, that's OK. Do not convince someone against their will. Make offers available at the time your customer has a problem. If they do not want it, find somebody who does. It is a numbers game." This guardrail prevents misuse of objection-handling as high-pressure tactics. ($100M Money Models)
- **Never Discount** -- "Never discount the main offer. It teaches your customers that your prices are negotiable. Adding bonuses to increase value to close the deal is far superior to cutting prices." Change how they pay (payment plan) or what they get (feature downsell), but never give the same thing for less. ($100M Offers Ch.14, $100M Money Models)
- **"People who demand to pay less for the same thing are business terrorists. I don't negotiate with terrorists."** -- if they want to pay less, change what they get. ($100M Money Models)
- **Create a solution for every objection you hear repeatedly** -- build templates, guides, checklists, and systems that preemptively solve problems before the prospect raises them. A single unsolved objection can kill a sale. ($100M Offers Ch.9)

## Output Structure

When this skill is used, produce the following:

```
## Objection Playbook

### 1. Objection Classification
For each objection the user provides or that is inferred from their context:
- The objection verbatim
- Category: Stall / Decision-Maker / Price / Non-Obstacle
- The hidden belief behind the objection

### 2. Named Close Recommendations
Which specific close(s) to use for each objection:
- Rocking Chair Close (for stalls -- paint the picture of what will actually happen)
- Unicorn Close (for stalls -- maintain conversational flow, do not let them walk)
- What-If Close (for stalls -- shift focus from fear to excitement about the result)
- Fully Informed Decision Frame (for stalls -- reframe from commitment to exploration)
- Agreement Stacking + Delayed Payment (for decision-maker)
- Identity Swap Close (for price/priorities)
- Best-Case / Worst-Case Close (for risk/price)
- Barter For Advertising (for genuine price objections -- trade discount for reviews, testimonials, referrals)
- Temperature Check (diagnostic after two rejected overcomes)

### 3. Belief-Breaking Script (if a false belief is identified)
Walk through all five steps within the Overview step of CLOSER:
1. What they believe
2. Why it is wrong
3. What is right
4. Why it is right
5. Proof to show

### 4. Downsell Path (for price objections)
- Seesaw Downselling (simplified: giant payments or tiny ones?)
- Payment Plan Downsell sequence (which of the 7 steps apply)
- Feature Downsell options (what to remove, in what order)
- Named tiers if applicable

### 5. Assumed Close Transition
- How to use the ID-to-Card Swap once objections are resolved
- Casual, continuous flow from paperwork to payment

### 6. Bonus Matching
- Each remaining objection mapped to a specific bonus that neutralizes it
- Bonus name, perceived value, and delivery format

### 7. Guarantee Recommendation
- Which guarantee type best eliminates the top objections
- The math: projected conversion lift vs refund rate

### 8. Ready-to-Use Scripts
- Short version (DM / text)
- Medium version (call / meeting)
- Full version (sales page / FAQ)
```

## Common Mistakes

- **Arguing with the prospect** -- never say "but" or "I disagree." Every counterargument should be framed as a question the prospect answers themselves. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Memorizing 50 closes and throwing them randomly** -- master 2-3 overcomes per category and stack them deliberately. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Treating non-obstacles as real objections** -- product-specific concerns are questions from people who still want to buy. Answer casually and move on. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6)
- **Discounting instead of downselling** -- dropping your price for the same product devalues it, upsets customers who paid full price, and creates ethical problems. Use payment plans, feature downsells, or Barter For Advertising instead. ($100M Money Models)
- **Dumping all bonuses in the initial pitch** -- you lose the psychological leverage of matching specific bonuses to specific objections. Ask first, reveal bonuses after. ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **Letting people leave undecided** -- "the only people who fail the program are ones who had to think about it, because they were not committed enough to make a decision." Not deciding is itself a decision to stay unchanged. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Selling harder instead of fixing the process** -- if you are constantly battling objections at the close, the problem is earlier in the CLOSER sequence (Clarify, Label, Overview, Sell the Vacation), not at the Explain step. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6)
- **Faking conviction with scripts** -- prospects can feel the difference between genuine conviction and performance. Build real proof first. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Pressuring people who genuinely do not want it** -- "Hard selling is for weak products. If someone doesn't want something, that's OK." The system works through the right offer at the right time, not through pressure. ($100M Money Models)
