## When to Use

- When designing a launch, promotion, cohort-based program, or any time-limited offer that needs ethical psychological pressure to drive decisions
- When an offer converts well but prospects stall, delay, or "need to think about it" -- scarcity and urgency eliminate fence-sitting
- When planning price increases, seasonal campaigns, or capacity-based enrollment and you want to maximize conversion during the window

## Core Framework

Scarcity and urgency are two of the four Enhancement Stack levers applied on top of a core offer to increase demand and decrease perceived supply -- without changing the actual offer or fulfillment ($100M Offers Ch.11).

**The Foundational Psychology:**

> "Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain." -- $100M Offers Ch.12

Humans are far more motivated to hoard a scarce resource than to act on something that could help them. This is why scarcity and urgency outperform benefit-driven marketing. Frame what they will lose by not acting.

> "Desire is a contract you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want." -- Naval Ravikant, cited in $100M Offers Ch.11

We only want things we do not have. As soon as we have them, desire disappears. Satisfying all demand kills future desire. The strategic implication: satisfy desire slowly and incompletely to maintain ravenous demand.

**The Hormozi Law:**

> "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off." -- $100M Offers Ch.11

Keep supply under demand. Cut supply and raise prices when demand increases. Demand is fractal (80/20) -- one-fifth of prospects will pay five times the price.

**The Conviction Principle (from Gym Launch Secrets):**

Hormozi discovered that his sales team did 2x the sales in the last 5 days of a 21-day campaign compared to the first 2 weeks combined. The difference was not scripting -- it was conviction. As they neared the deadline, their tones changed because the urgency became REAL to them. The human mind is very good at picking out BS.

> "If someone does not make it into your facility or buy your product today, it really can be their last chance, because the likelihood they reschedule and follow through is only about 10%." -- Gym Launch Secrets

Make the scarcity real to yourself first, and your tone will change naturally. Do not use fake scarcity or read urgency scripts in a monotone voice.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Define Your Real Capacity Constraints

Before designing any scarcity or urgency mechanism, identify your actual constraints. Honest scarcity -- simply defining your actual capacity and advertising it -- is the most ethical and effective form ($100M Offers Ch.12).

Ask the user:
1. How many clients/customers can you realistically serve well in a given period?
2. What is your current capacity utilization? (e.g., "We are at 81% capacity")
3. What happens to quality if you exceed that capacity?
4. Do you have natural time boundaries (cohorts, seasons, quarters)?

> "I always knew what my capacity was per week, and simply chose to let our prospects know how many openings we had left." -- $100M Offers Ch.12

Remember: only you get to draw where "full" is.

### Step 2: Select Scarcity Type(s)

There are three types of scarcity ($100M Offers Ch.12):

**Type 1: Limited Supply of Seats/Slots**

Physical or service capacity limits. For services, use one of three sub-types:

- **Total Business Cap:** "We only work with 20 clients at a time." A hard ceiling on total clients.
- **Growth Rate Cap:** "We only take on 5 new clients per month." Limits the rate of new intake regardless of total capacity.
- **Cohort Cap:** "Each cohort is limited to 12 participants." Groups are capped per round, and the next round has its own enrollment window.

**Type 2: Limited Supply of Bonuses**

The core offer remains available, but specific bonuses disappear after a threshold. "The first 10 people also get [bonus]. After that, you get the program but not this bonus."

**Type 3: Never Available Again**

The offer itself disappears permanently. Use for one-time launches, founding member pricing, or legacy access tiers. Once the window closes, it never reopens.

**Advanced: Extreme Scarcity via Access Tiers ($100M Offers)**

> "If you want to immediately make a lot of money, create a very exclusive service level based on access to you, that you cap at a tiny number. Price it very high. Then, tell people." -- $100M Offers

Create a deliberately unscalable tier -- 5 spots for direct access at $25,000/quarter, or 10 spots for voice memo access at $10,000/month. Cap it, price it high, announce it. These clients tend to be the best clients.

### Step 3: Select Urgency Type(s)

There are four types of urgency ($100M Offers Ch.13):

**Type 1: Cohort-Based Rolling Urgency**

"The next group starts Monday." Fixed start dates create natural deadlines. Miss this cohort and you wait for the next one. Works for courses, programs, group coaching, and onboarding waves.

**Type 2: Rolling Seasonal Urgency**

Rename the same promotion by season with real deadlines. January: "New Year New You Challenge." February: "Valentine's Lovers Promo." March: "Sexy By Spring Special." The actual offer and fulfillment never change -- only the wrapping paper changes ($100M Offers Ch.16). Each seasonal wrapper has a real expiration tied to the season ending.

**Type 3: Pricing/Bonus-Based Urgency**

A promotion or discount they could miss. "Founding member pricing expires Friday." "This bonus package is only available during launch week." The core offer continues to exist, but the enhanced version disappears.

**Type 4: Exploding Opportunity**

An arbitrage or window that decays with time. "This market gap will close as competitors catch on." "This regulatory window shuts in Q2." The opportunity itself has a natural expiration that you are simply communicating honestly.

### Step 4: Design the Launch Sequence (Whisper-Tease-Shout)

Use the Whisper-Tease-Shout launch method from $100M Leads to build anticipation before deploying scarcity and urgency:

**Phase 1 -- Whisper (Callouts):**
- Create curiosity, keep the product mysterious, show behind the scenes
- Start months out: every 4-6 weeks until 60 days out, then every 2-3 weeks until 30 days out
- Goal: make people aware something is coming without revealing what

**Phase 2 -- Tease (Value Elements):**
- Reveal the product, make the launch date public, show value
- Once per week until 14 days out, then twice per week until 3 days out
- Goal: build desire by showing what they will get

**Phase 3 -- Shout (CTA):**
- Specific actions, pound audience with bonuses, scarcity, urgency
- At least twice a day starting 3 days out, every few hours on launch day
- Increase frequency dramatically in the final 3 days
- Goal: drive action with clear deadlines and consequences of missing out

The Hormozi Law applies here directly: the whisper and tease phases are the runway. The longer you build anticipation before the shout, the bigger the conversion event.

### Step 5: Implement Pipeline Cleaning

Before any price increase, use Pipeline Cleaning ($100M Offers):

> "Never raise your prices without letting people know. It shows a position of strength and will give you a nice little influx of cash." -- $100M Offers

Notify your entire pipeline: "The price is going up on [date]. Get in now at the current rate." This converts fence-sitters into buyers, creates urgency, and demonstrates a position of strength.

Example: "Starting January 1st, our program is increasing from $5,000 to $8,000 to match the increased value we are now delivering. If you have been thinking about joining, now is the time to lock in the current rate."

### Step 6: Craft Honest Scarcity Communication

Tell prospects what percentage of capacity you are at. "We are at 81% capacity" implies social proof and urgency simultaneously.

Layer all four Enhancement Stack elements in a single statement:

> "We only take on 5 new clients per quarter (scarcity), and we have 3 spots left this quarter with the current pricing -- it goes up next quarter (urgency). You also get our bonus strategy audit valued at $5,000 (bonus), and if we do not deliver X result in Y time, we keep working for free until we do (guarantee)." -- $100M Offers application example

### Step 7: Plan the Sellout and Announce It

Always sell out. It is better to sell out consistently than to over-supply ($100M Offers Ch.12).

1. Set available slots below what you think you can sell
2. When you sell out, announce it publicly
3. Let unfulfilled demand build
4. Next time, slightly increase capacity while maintaining scarcity

The Chanel example: Chanel sends only 1-2 of each piece to each store. This is not an accident -- it is a compounding scarcity strategy.

## Key Principles

- **Scarcity is a compounding strategy.** Selling out consistently is more valuable than maximizing units sold in any single promotion. When people see you sold out, those who were on the fence develop stronger desire. Next time, they act faster and pay more. This compounds over time. ($100M Offers Ch.12)

- **The last 3% of time creates 50-60% of sales.** Deadlines drive decisions. You will make more money from people who act because of urgency than you will lose from people who miss the deadline -- those people were never going to buy anyway. ($100M Offers Ch.13)

- **Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain.** Frame what prospects will lose by not acting, not just what they will gain by acting. ($100M Offers Ch.12)

- **Conviction, not scripting.** Do not use fake scarcity or read urgency scripts in a monotone voice. Prospects can feel when you are going through the motions versus when you genuinely believe time is running out for them. Make the scarcity real to yourself first. (Gym Launch Secrets)

- **The person who needs the exchange less has the upper hand.** Most people try to manufacture urgency or desperation in the buyer. The real power move is to authentically not need the sale, which paradoxically makes people want to buy more. ($100M Offers)

- **Demand is fractal (80/20).** One-fifth of prospects will pay five times the price. By selling fewer units at higher prices, you make more money, have lower costs, provide more value, and increase desire in the remaining prospect base. ($100M Offers Ch.11)

- **Never discount -- add bonuses instead.** When you need to create urgency around pricing, add disappearing bonuses rather than discounting the core offer. Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable. ($100M Offers Ch.14)

- **Supply and demand are inversely correlated.** Keep supply under demand to maximize profits. The "Delicate Dance of Desire" means satisfying desire slowly and incompletely to maintain ravenous demand. ($100M Offers Ch.11)

## Output Structure

The skill should produce a **Scarcity & Urgency Blueprint** with these sections:

1. **Capacity Analysis:** Real constraints identified, where "full" is drawn, current utilization percentage
2. **Scarcity Mechanism(s):** Which of the three types (with sub-type if applicable), specific numbers and limits, communication language
3. **Urgency Mechanism(s):** Which of the four types, specific deadlines and windows, what disappears and when
4. **Launch Sequence:** Whisper-Tease-Shout timeline with specific dates, content themes per phase, frequency escalation plan
5. **Pipeline Cleaning Plan:** Price increase announcement template, timeline, and target list
6. **Sellout Strategy:** Target capacity, sellout announcement plan, demand building for next round
7. **Communication Scripts:** Honest scarcity statements for sales calls, emails, landing pages, and ads -- each layering scarcity + urgency + bonuses + guarantee
8. **Conviction Notes:** Why the scarcity is real, what the prospect genuinely loses by waiting, the 10% reschedule-and-follow-through statistic

## Common Mistakes

- **Fake scarcity.** Do not use evergreen countdown timers that reset, fabricated "only 3 left" indicators, or artificial waitlists. The human mind is very good at picking out BS. If your scarcity is not real, your tone will betray you. ($100M Offers Ch.12, Gym Launch Secrets)

- **Over-supplying to maximize short-term revenue.** Do not satisfy all demand. Satisfying all demand kills desire and makes future promotions less effective. Set slots below what you think you can sell. ($100M Offers Ch.12)

- **Discounting instead of adding urgency.** Never lower the price to create urgency. Add disappearing bonuses or communicate a coming price increase instead. Discounting puts you in a position of weakness. ($100M Offers Ch.14)

- **Skipping the runway.** Do not shout without whispering and teasing first. The Hormozi Law says the longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. Jumping straight to "BUY NOW" without building anticipation wastes the most powerful conversion lever. ($100M Offers Ch.11, $100M Leads)

- **Raising prices without telling people.** Never raise prices silently. Always announce the increase in advance. The pipeline cleaning step alone can generate a significant influx of cash. ($100M Offers)

- **Treating urgency as a script instead of a conviction.** If you do not genuinely believe the prospect loses something by waiting, your urgency will sound hollow. Internalize the 10% statistic: only about 10% of people who say "I will come back later" actually do. (Gym Launch Secrets)

- **Using scarcity only once.** Scarcity is a compounding strategy, not a one-time tactic. Each sellout makes the next promotion more effective. Plan for multiple rounds, not a single event. ($100M Offers Ch.12)
