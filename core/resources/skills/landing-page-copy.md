## When to Use
- You have an offer or pitch ready and need a sales page or landing page
- Conversions are low and you suspect the copy is the problem
- Messaging feels scattered and you need a structured, persuasion-first page flow

## Core Framework: The 12 Copy Commandments

From Gym Launch Secrets -- the only explicit copywriting framework across all four Hormozi books. Every section of the landing page must be filtered through these rules.

1. **Headline comes first -- curiosity is king.** "When you have written your headline, you have spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar." (David Ogilvy, cited in $100M Leads Ch.10). Five times more people read your headline than anything else.
2. **Say what only you can say.** Generic claims get ignored. State the specific, proprietary thing that differentiates you.
3. **Call out who you are looking for AND who you are NOT.** Use the Cocktail Party Effect: Labels ("Gym Owners"), Yes-Questions ("Do you wake up to pee more than once?"), If-Then Statements ("If you run over $100K/month in ads, we can save you 20%"), Ridiculous Results ("Massage studio books out two years in advance"). ($100M Leads Ch.10)
4. **Reason why -- use the word "because."** People comply when given a reason, even a weak one. Always explain WHY you are making the offer.
5. **Damaging admission -- own your flaws for authenticity.** Admit a real weakness upfront. This paradoxically increases trust and perceived value. The Anti-Guarantee uses this same principle: "Our methods are so powerful and so easy to copy that once you see them, they cannot be unseen. We are exposing the inner workings of our business, and therefore all sales must be final." ($100M Offers Ch.15)
6. **Show, do not tell -- describe emotions and scenarios.** Instead of "make more sales," write: "You will be amazed when you hear customers read their credit card numbers to you over the phone after reading two magic sentences. It will feel like taking orders." (Gym Launch Secrets)
7. **Tie benefits to status.** Frame every benefit in terms of how other people will perceive the prospect's achievement. "Your golf buddies' jaws will drop when they see your ball soar 40 yards past theirs. They will ask you what changed. Only you will know." ($100M Offers Ch.6)
8. **Use urgency and scarcity legitimately.** Three scarcity types: limited seats/slots, limited bonuses, never available again. Four urgency types: Cohort-Based Rolling, Rolling Seasonal, Pricing/Bonus-Based, Exploding Opportunity. "The last 3% of time allotted in a campaign creates 50-60% of sales." ($100M Offers Ch.12-13)
9. **Implied authority -- mention credentials, numbers, notable clients.** Do not brag directly. Let specifics do the work: "After 33 in-person gym turnarounds averaging $42,000 in additional sales in 21 days..."
10. **P.S. = Power Sentence.** The P.S. is the second most read part of any piece of copy after the headline. Use it to restate the single most compelling element of the offer.
11. **Clear next steps -- Step 1, Step 2, Step 3.** Reduce perceived effort by making the path to action obvious and simple.
12. **Third-grade reading level.** "Fancy equals friction. Simple equals sales. I put every ad I have through a free reading-level tool and continue to edit the copy until it is at a third-grade reading level or below." (Gym Launch Secrets)

## Supporting Framework: The Belief-Breaking Formula

From Gym Launch Secrets -- a five-step persuasion structure for shattering false beliefs and replacing them with beliefs aligned with your solution. Highly applicable to the Problem and Solution sections of a landing page.

1. **Say what they believe** (e.g., "So you believe cheap front-end offers work?")
2. **Say why it is wrong** (e.g., "This does not provide cash flow to market or acquire customers")
3. **Say what is right** (e.g., "Use a high-ticket front-end acquisition")
4. **Say WHY it is right** (e.g., "It will allow you to spend whatever you need to get people in profitably")
5. **Show proof** -- third-party stats, testimonials, wall of results

Use this formula in the Problem section to break false beliefs about why past solutions failed, and in the Solution section to establish why your approach works. Each major objection on the page can be addressed with a mini Belief-Breaking sequence.

## Supporting Framework: The CLOSER-Derived Page Flow

The CLOSER sales formula from Gym Launch Secrets (Clarify-Label-Overview-Sell-Explain-Reinforce) maps naturally to landing page section flow. Forged from over 4,000 one-on-one closes.

| CLOSER Step | Page Section | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| **C**larify why they are there | Hero Section | Establish the gap -- what they want that they have not achieved |
| **L**abel the problem | Problem Section | Recap current state vs desired state with specific numbers |
| **O**verview past pains | Problem Section (continued) | Review failed attempts, categorize what was missing |
| **S**ell the vacation | Outcome + Solution Sections | Sell the dream outcome, not the process details |
| **E**xplain away concerns | Objection Handling + Guarantee | Address the three real obstacles: stalls, decision-maker, price |
| **R**einforce the decision | CTA + P.S. + FAQ | Prevent backouts, create choreographed next steps |

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Extract Core Elements

Before writing any copy, identify:
- **Dream outcome** -- what the prospect truly wants (the vacation, not the plane flight). "No one wants your membership, your software, your consulting hours. They want the outcome." ($100M Offers Ch.9)
- **Target avatar** -- who specifically, using Cocktail Party Effect callouts
- **Core pain** -- the specific frustrations, failed attempts, and emotional burden
- **False beliefs** -- what they currently believe about their problem that is wrong (for the Belief-Breaking Formula)
- **Offer structure** -- components, bonuses, delivery vehicles
- **Proof** -- testimonials, case studies, numbers, credentials
- **Objections** -- every reason someone would not buy

### Step 2: Write the Hero Section (Above the Fold)

Apply Commandments 1, 2, 3, 7, and 12. This maps to the **Clarify** step of CLOSER -- establish the gap.

Include:
- **Curiosity headline** -- the single most important element on the page. Test relentlessly. "Hormozi tested 'How to get strangers to want to buy your stuff' against 'Get strangers to want to buy your stuff.' Adding just 'how to' overwhelmingly won." ($100M Leads Ch.10)
- **Avatar callout** -- use Labels or If-Then to filter the right reader in
- **Dream outcome framed as status** -- how others will perceive their achievement
- **Speed/ease signal** -- address the bottom of the Value Equation immediately
- **Primary CTA**

### Step 3: Write the Problem Section

This is the most important section for conversion. Maps to the **Label** and **Overview** steps of CLOSER. Apply the core persuasion principle:

> "The point of good writing is for the reader to understand. The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel understood." ($100M Offers)

> "The pain is the pitch. If you can articulate the pain a prospect is feeling accurately, they will almost always buy what you are offering." ($100M Offers)

The goal is NOT to inform the reader about their problem. The goal is for the reader to feel SEEN. Write as if you have been eavesdropping on their midnight thoughts.

Include:
- Current struggles described with emotional specificity (Commandment 6: show, do not tell)
- Failed attempts and why they did not work
- **Apply the Belief-Breaking Formula here**: State what they currently believe about their problem. Explain why that belief is wrong. Introduce the correct belief. Explain why it is right. Show proof. This transforms the Problem section from a list of pains into a persuasive argument that shatters the assumptions keeping them stuck.
- The logical solutions they have already tried -- "If a problem persists, all the logical solutions have likely been tried because they are obvious. What remains are psychological solutions." The London Underground improved satisfaction more with a cheap dotted map (reducing perceived wait) than with billions on faster trains (reducing actual wait). ($100M Offers Ch.6)
- The emotional weight: frustration, doubt, exhaustion

### Step 4: Write the Outcome Section

Maps to the **Sell the vacation** step of CLOSER. "Sell them the vacation, not the flight. No one wants to hear about TSA lines, baggage claim, delays, layovers, or time changes. They want to hear about Maui." (Gym Launch Secrets, CLOSER formula)

Frame the transformation using status (Commandment 7). Do not just list what changes -- describe how others will react to the change.

Apply the What-Who-When framework from $100M Leads Ch.10:
- **What**: Dream Outcome across all 8 value elements
- **Who**: How the outcome affects the prospect's spouse, kids, colleagues, boss, friends, rivals
- **When**: Contrast past failure, present struggle, and future transformation

Make the outcome visual, concrete, and status-rich.

### Step 5: Write the Solution Section

Introduce the offer. Apply Commandment 2 (say what only you can say).

**Use the second half of the Belief-Breaking Formula here**: Present what is right (your solution), explain why it is right (the mechanism), and show proof (results, data, testimonials). This creates a natural persuasive flow from the Problem section where you broke their old beliefs.

Include:
- What the product/service is -- simply
- How it works -- in 3 steps maximum (Commandment 11)
- Why it works -- the mechanism or proprietary framework
- A damaging admission (Commandment 5) -- own one real limitation to build trust

### Step 6: Write the Value Stack

This is the Hormozi signature section. From $100M Offers Ch.10 and Ch.14:

**Structure each component as:**
- Sexy name (use the M-A-G-I-C formula: Magnetic reason why + Avatar + Goal + Interval + Container word) ($100M Offers Ch.16)
- What it does
- The specific outcome it produces
- Assigned dollar value

**Apply the Irresistible Offer Checklist structure** from Gym Launch Secrets when constructing the stack:
1. Pre-frame with authority and social proof established earlier on the page
2. Price anchor -- drop a higher reference price before revealing the actual price
3. Splinter stack -- unbundle everything you do and list each item separately
4. Layer in scarcity and urgency elements
5. Present the guarantee (covered in Step 9)
6. Add the bribe -- an additional bonus for acting today
7. Make the ongoing price feel like a bargain compared to the front-end investment (downsell your upsell)

**Presentation order:**
1. Core offer with named components
2. Bonuses -- each solving a specific objection. "The value of bonuses should eclipse the core offer value." ($100M Offers Ch.14)
3. Total stacked value
4. Price reveal with contrast

**Key rules:**
- "A single offer is less valuable than the same offer broken into components and stacked as bonuses." ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- "Never discount the main offer -- it teaches customers your prices are negotiable. Adding bonuses puts you in a position of strength and goodwill rather than weakness." ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- Tools and checklists beat additional trainings as bonus formats
- Consider partner bonus arbitrage: get adjacent businesses to provide bonuses for free in exchange for exposure ($100M Offers Ch.14)

### Step 7: Write the Proof Section

Apply Commandment 9 (implied authority). This section supports the **three transfers of sales** from Gym Launch Secrets -- the proof must transfer: (1) belief that the product works, (2) confidence that this specific person can reach their goals, and (3) conviction that they can overcome their circumstances.

Include:
- Testimonials with specific numbers and outcomes -- these transfer **belief in the product**
- Case studies showing the transformation arc from someone similar to the prospect -- these transfer **confidence** ("if someone like me did it, I can too")
- Credentials, client logos, notable results -- these transfer **conviction** through authority
- If no testimonials yet: use logic, demonstrations, or the Conviction Close principle -- "You must outwork your self-doubt. You must be so confident in your delivery, because you have done it so many times, that you know that this person will succeed." ($100M Offers Section V)

### Step 8: Write the Objection Handling Section

Maps to the **Explain** step of CLOSER. Address objections throughout the page, not just in one section. But dedicate a section to the remaining ones.

From Gym Launch Secrets, there are only **three real objections**: stalls ("I need to think about it"), decision-maker ("I need to talk to my spouse"), and price ("I cannot afford it"). Address all three on the page.

For each objection:
- Acknowledge it honestly (never dismiss) -- "Always agree first, then ask a question." (Gym Launch Secrets)
- Apply the Belief-Breaking Formula where appropriate: state what they believe, why it is wrong, what is right, why it is right, show proof
- Apply the damaging admission where appropriate (Commandment 5)
- Reframe using Value Equation logic: is the objection about Dream Outcome, Likelihood, Time Delay, or Effort?

### Step 9: Write the Guarantee Section

From $100M Offers Ch.15 -- four formal guarantee types:

1. **Unconditional**: No questions asked refund. Lowest barrier to entry.
2. **Conditional**: Refund tied to the prospect completing key success actions. Filters out non-implementers.
3. **Anti-Guarantee (Damaging Admission)**: "All sales are final" framed as proof of value. "Since it is so standard to have some sort of guarantee, not having one is attention-worthy." ($100M Offers Ch.15)
4. **Implied**: Performance-based or revenue-share models.

> **Hormozi's personal favorite -- the Service Guarantee**: "Keep working for free until the result is achieved." Zero refund risk with maximum persuasive power. "Since I have been advising businesses to use this particular guarantee, I have yet to have a single person say a client took them up on it." ($100M Offers Ch.15). Present this as a separate, powerful option rather than a fifth formal type -- it is Hormozi's recommended approach for service-based businesses because it eliminates refund exposure entirely while being the most persuasive guarantee possible.

**Stack multiple guarantees when possible.** Give your guarantee a memorable name.

The math: "A strong guarantee that increases sales by 30% while doubling refund rate from 5% to 10% still nets 23% more revenue." ($100M Offers Ch.15)

### Step 10: Write the CTA Section

Maps to the **Reinforce** step of CLOSER. Apply Commandments 8 and 11.

Include:
- Restated dream outcome (status-framed)
- Urgency or scarcity if legitimate
- Clear next steps: Step 1, Step 2, Step 3
- The Fully Informed Decision Frame if appropriate: "I am not asking you to decide yes or no today. I am asking you to make a fully informed decision. The only way to do that is on the inside, not the outside." ($100M Offers)

### Step 11: Write the P.S. (Power Sentence)

The second most read element after the headline (Commandment 10). Use it to:
- Restate the single most compelling benefit, status-framed
- Add a final urgency or scarcity element
- Include a damaging admission that builds last-moment trust

### Step 12: Write the FAQ Section

Address remaining objections using short, direct answers. Each FAQ answer should subtly reinforce a value driver from the Value Equation.

### Step 13: Run the Third-Grade Reading Level Check

Put the entire page through a reading-level tool. Edit until it scores at or below third-grade level. "Fancy equals friction. Simple equals sales." (Gym Launch Secrets)

## Pricing Section Design: The Decoy Offer

When presenting pricing on the landing page, apply the Decoy Offer framework from $100M Money Models:

- **Present a stripped-down option (the decoy) alongside the premium option.** Strip the decoy down as much as reasonable -- make it bare bones. Make the premium option as valuable as possible. The contrast makes the premium the obvious choice.
- Ask the equivalent of "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?" to frame the premium as the serious choice. ($100M Money Models)
- Use the Anchor Upsell principle: present the most expensive option first (5-10x the standard price) to create contrast. "The bigger the gasp, the more they buy." ($100M Money Models)
- Advertise benefits not features -- "sell the transformation, not the workouts and meal plans." ($100M Money Models)

## Key Principles

- **"The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel understood, not for them to understand you."** -- The problem section must make them feel SEEN, not informed. ($100M Offers)
- **"Sell the Vacation, Not the Plane Flight"** -- "No one wants to hear about TSA lines, baggage claim, delays, layovers, or time changes. They want to hear about Maui." Describe the destination (outcome), not the process (features). ($100M Offers Ch.9 / Gym Launch Secrets CLOSER formula)
- **"If a problem persists, all the logical solutions have likely been tried because they are obvious. What remains are psychological solutions."** -- The London Underground improved satisfaction more with a cheap dotted map (reducing perceived wait) than with billions on faster trains. Frame your solution as the psychological breakthrough that the logical approaches missed. ($100M Offers Ch.6)
- **Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)** -- Every section of the page should be moving at least one of these four drivers. ($100M Offers Ch.6)
- **Status is the meta-driver** -- "All dream outcomes are ultimately about status -- perceived increase or decrease in relative standing compared to others." Frame benefits through the lens of social perception. ($100M Offers Ch.6)
- **"People want to buy expensive things -- they just need a reason."** -- The page must provide that reason through stacked value, not discounted price. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **"There is no strategic benefit to being the second cheapest in the marketplace, but there is for being the most expensive."** (Dan Kennedy, cited in $100M Offers Ch.5)
- **Never discount -- add bonuses instead.** Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable. ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **"Selling is serving."** -- Sales is made up of three transfers: belief in the product, confidence the person can reach their goals, and conviction they can overcome circumstances. The landing page must accomplish all three transfers. (Gym Launch Secrets)

## Output Structure

The skill produces a `LANDING_PAGE.md` with these sections:

```
1. Hero Section
   - Curiosity Headline
   - Avatar Callout
   - Status-Framed Outcome
   - Speed/Ease Signal
   - Primary CTA

2. Problem Section
   - Emotional pain (show, do not tell)
   - Failed attempts
   - Belief-Breaking sequence (what they believe, why wrong, what is right, why right, proof)
   - Midnight thoughts
   - "We understand because..." (damaging admission optional)

3. Outcome Section
   - Status-framed transformation
   - What-Who-When angles
   - Visual, concrete future state

4. Solution Section
   - What it is (simple)
   - How it works (3 steps max)
   - Why it works (mechanism / Belief-Breaking proof)
   - Damaging admission

5. Value Stack
   - Core Offer (named components with dollar values)
   - Bonus 1 (name, outcome, value)
   - Bonus 2 (name, outcome, value)
   - Bonus 3+ (name, outcome, value)
   - Total Stacked Value: $X
   - Your Price: $Y
   - Value-to-Price Contrast

6. Proof Section
   - Testimonials with numbers (transfers belief in product)
   - Case studies from similar people (transfers confidence)
   - Credentials / authority signals (transfers conviction)

7. Objection Handling
   - Stall objection -> Reframe
   - Decision-maker objection -> Reframe
   - Price objection -> Reframe
   - Additional objections -> Mapped to Value Equation driver

8. Guarantee Section
   - Named guarantee
   - Type (Unconditional / Conditional / Service / Anti / Stacked)
   - Specific terms

9. Pricing Section (if applicable)
   - Decoy option (stripped down)
   - Premium option (full value, contrasted)
   - Anchor price reference

10. CTA Section
    - Restated outcome (status-framed)
    - Urgency/scarcity
    - Clear next steps (Step 1, 2, 3)
    - Fully Informed Decision Frame (optional)

11. P.S. (Power Sentence)
    - Most compelling benefit restated
    - Final urgency element

12. FAQ
    - Remaining objections as Q&A

13. Readability Check
    - Confirmed third-grade reading level or below
```

## CTA Placement

Place CTAs in at least three locations:
- After the Hero Section
- After the Value Stack (price reveal)
- After the Guarantee Section
- Final CTA at page bottom

## Common Mistakes

- **Writing to inform instead of to persuade.** The prospect should feel understood, not educated. If your problem section reads like a blog post, rewrite it.
- **Listing features instead of status-framed benefits.** Never write "increases drive by 40 yards" when you can write "your golf buddies' jaws will drop."
- **Using clever, complex language.** Third-grade reading level. No exceptions. Fancy equals friction.
- **Skipping the damaging admission.** Perfection is not believable. Own one real flaw to make everything else credible.
- **Presenting bonuses before establishing core value.** The value stack must build: core offer first, then bonuses that eclipse it.
- **Using fake scarcity or urgency.** Honest scarcity compounds over time. Fake scarcity destroys trust permanently. ($100M Offers Ch.12)
- **Discounting instead of adding bonuses.** "Never discount the main offer -- it teaches customers your prices are negotiable." ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **Forgetting the P.S.** It is the second most read element. Leaving it out wastes prime persuasion real estate.
- **Making the CTA vague.** "Get started" is weak. "Step 1: Click the button below. Step 2: Fill out the 30-second form. Step 3: Get your first result within 48 hours" is strong. (Commandment 11)
- **Skipping the Belief-Breaking sequence.** Simply listing pains is not enough. You must break the false beliefs that led to those pains, then replace them with the correct framework. Without this, the prospect leaves thinking "I already knew that" instead of "I finally understand why nothing worked."
- **Treating all objections as equal.** There are only three real objections (stalls, decision-maker, price). Everything else is a question from someone who still wants to buy. Handle the three real ones explicitly. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Not transferring all three elements of conviction.** The page must transfer belief in the product, confidence the prospect can succeed, AND conviction they can overcome their circumstances. Missing any one of these kills conversion. (Gym Launch Secrets)
