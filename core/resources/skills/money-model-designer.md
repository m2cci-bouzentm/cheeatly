## When to Use

- When a business has a single offer and needs to build a complete revenue system around it
- When designing pricing tiers, upsell paths, downsell alternatives, or recurring revenue structures
- When a business is profitable per customer but cash-constrained because the Money Model does not pay back acquisition cost within 30 days

## Core Framework

### The Money Model Framework

A Money Model is a deliberate sequence of offers -- not a single offer. Every opportunity to solve a customer's problem is an opportunity to make an offer. A $100M Money Model makes more profit from one customer than it costs to get and service many customers in the first 30 days.

**Formula:**

```
Money Model = Attraction Offers + Upsell Offers + Downsell Offers + Continuity Offers
```

**The rental car example:** A $19/day car became $100/day through five sequential offers -- vehicle upgrade, late return, premium insurance, minimum insurance downsell, and prepaid gas. The fulfillment barely changed. The offers multiplied revenue 5x.

**The storage unit principle:** A "free month" of storage generated $127 per customer through locks, boxes, tape, labels, moving tools, insurance, and unit upgrades. Free is the beginning of a sequence of paid offers that solve problems the free offer reveals.

> "It is less about having a hundred different products with the same offer, and more about having a hundred different offers for the same product." -- $100M Money Models, Downsell Offers Conclusion

### The Growth Multiplier Formula

```
Growth = (Customer Value Multiplier) x (Customer Count Multiplier) x (Speed Multiplier)

2x each = 8x growth
3x each = 27x growth
```

If you double how much each customer is worth, double how many customers you get, and double how fast they pay, your business grows 8x -- not 6x. The effects compound multiplicatively, not additively. Small improvements in each dimension create outsized results.

($100M Money Models, "The Four Types of Offers That Make Money Models")

### The Pie Equation (Hypothetical Gym Max)

For any recurring business, this formula reveals your ceiling:

```
HGM = Number of new sign-ups per month / Monthly churn rate

Example: 10 sign-ups / 10% churn = 100 max customers
         20 sign-ups / 5% churn  = 400 max customers (4x improvement)
```

Reducing churn from 10% to 5% doubles your maximum -- the same effect as doubling sign-ups. Manipulating both simultaneously creates multiplicative growth. Reducing churn from 9% to 3% is not a 6% improvement -- it is a 300% increase in lifetime value.

(Gym Launch Secrets, Ch. 13-14)

### The Wedding Cake Model

A multi-tiered revenue model where each tier has two components:

- **Icing** = front-end cash flow from new customers
- **Cake** = recurring cash flow from existing customers

Three tiers create six revenue streams. Internal plays (sprinkles) feed all three tiers at 90% margins. The model is driven by two principles:

1. **Accommodate the buying curve** -- 20% of customers will pay 3x more if given the opportunity. Serve them at a higher level.
2. **Maximize buying curves per customer** -- tap into different spending wallets (different product categories) to capture more of their total lifetime value.

Example math from Gym Launch Secrets (Ch. 22-25):
- Tier 1 (Large Group): 30 new/mo x $500 front-end + 243 recurring x $167/mo = ~$55,581/mo
- Tier 2 (Semi-Private): 4 new/mo x $2,500 + 32 recurring x $550/mo = ~$27,600/mo
- Tier 3 (Supplements): 29 buyers/mo x $200 / .23 attrition = 126 recurring x $200 = ~$25,200/mo
- **Total: ~$108,381/month**

### Client-Financed Acquisition

```
If (30-day gross profit per customer) >= (Cost to acquire customer)
Then: Client-Financed Acquisition = unlimited scaling potential
```

Structure your front-end offer so that new customers pay you back within the first 30 days, covering their acquisition cost. This unlocks infinite scaling because you never run out of cash to acquire more customers. Use credit cards for 30-day interest-free float to fund acquisition.

> "Every company I scaled past $1M/month in the first 12 months used this approach." -- $100M Leads, Paid Ads Part II

**LTGP:CAC Ratio** (the health metric):

```
LTGP:CAC = (All revenue from customer over lifetime - All costs to deliver) / (All costs to acquire that customer)
Target: >= 3:1
```

Every struggling portfolio company Hormozi invested in had LTGP:CAC below 3:1. Two levers: lower CAC (better ads) or increase LTGP (better business model). If CAC is within 3x industry average, focus on increasing LTGP instead.

> "The difference between winners and losers is usually how much they make per customer, not how cheaply they acquire them. Costs approach zero but revenue can go to infinity." -- $100M Leads

## Step-by-Step Process

### Stage I: Get Cash (Attraction Offers)

Attraction Offers turn strangers into customers. Everyone wants a great deal. The greater the discount, the better the deal, with free being the greatest discount of all.

> "People look for one thing and buy another by accident all the time. Attraction Offers get them to do it on purpose." -- $100M Money Models

**1. Choose your Attraction Offer type:**

| Type | How It Works | Key Principle |
|------|-------------|---------------|
| **Win Your Money Back** | Customer pays up front, earns money back (as store credit) if they meet criteria | Generated over $1 billion in sales industry-wide. Apply store credit toward a more expensive offer over a longer period. |
| **Giveaways** | Advertise chance to win grand prize, collect contacts, offer everyone else a promotional discount | Grand prize must be the thing you want everyone to buy. Give away two prizes for twice the leads. |
| **Decoy Offer** | Advertise something free/discounted, present premium side by side | Ask "Are you here for free stuff or lasting results?" 80% take rate on premium is achievable. |
| **Buy X Get Y Free** | Customer buys something, gets other stuff free | Raise prices before giving stuff away to preserve profits. "Buy 1 Get 2 Free" drives more interest than "33% off." |
| **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later** | Choice: pay discounted price now with bonuses OR full price later with satisfaction guarantee | Get card on file first. If more than 10% of pay-later people cancel, you promised too much. |

**2. Validate Client-Financed Acquisition math:**

- Calculate your cost to acquire a customer (ad spend / customers acquired)
- Calculate 30-day gross profit per new customer (revenue collected in 30 days minus delivery cost)
- If 30-day gross profit >= acquisition cost, you have CFA and can scale without cash constraints
- If not, add upsells, order bumps, or immediate additional offers to increase 30-day cash collected

**3. Test and raise prices in stages:**

Start cheap to get lots of yeses and customer feedback. Use feedback to improve the product. Then keep raising the price until you cannot make up for the nos with extra cash from the yeses.

> "A Grand Slam Attraction Offer changes your business and life forever." -- $100M Money Models, Attraction Offers Conclusion

### Stage II: Get More Cash (Upsell + Downsell Offers)

Do NOT begin Stage II until Stage I is working reliably. Perfect one offer at a time.

> "The thing you sell the most is not always the thing you make the most profit on. You make profit on the second, third, and fourth offers." -- $100M Money Models, Upsell Offers Introduction

#### Upsell Offers

When an offer solves a problem, another appears. You upsell the solution to the next problem. McDonald's goes from $0.25 profit on a burger to $3.00 profit with fries, drink, and supersize -- an 11.6x increase. Without upsells, there would be no McDonald's.

**4. Implement the four upsell types:**

**Classic Upsell** -- Offer the solution to the customer's next problem the moment they become aware of it. "You can't have X without Y."

- Current customers always have a higher chance of buying than strangers
- When timed right, customers upsell themselves
- **BAMFAM** (Book A Meeting From A Meeting): End every appointment by scheduling the next one. A customer should know the next time they see you, and why, before they leave.

**Menu Upsell** (4 Tactics -- "This took me ten years to learn"):

1. **Unselling** -- Tell customers what they do NOT need. Cross items off the list. This builds enormous goodwill and authority. "I kept products just to cross them out!"
2. **Prescription Upselling** -- Give detailed, personalized instructions on how to use the product as if they already own it. Never ask if they want it; tell them how they will use it.
3. **A/B Upselling** -- Instead of asking "Do you want this?" (yes/no), ask "Do you prefer A or B?" Either answer is a purchase. Add a nudge: "This is my favorite" or "A lot of people love this."
4. **Card On File** -- Ask to use the card already on file. Removes friction and the hidden costs of buying.

> "Instead of asking if customers wanted to buy a product, yes or no, we ask which product they prefer: A or B. Either choice results in an upsell." -- $100M Money Models

**Anchor Upsell** -- Present your most expensive premium offer first (5-10x the price). Get "The Gasp." Come to the rescue by asking if they care about what makes it premium. Present your main offer -- they feel relieved and see a better deal.

- Make the premium offer real -- do not treat it as fake or customers will see through it
- Some customers WILL buy the premium. Be ready to deliver it.
- The bigger the gasp, the more they buy
- Hormozi budgeted $500 for a suit, saw a $16,000 price tag, then happily paid $2,200 for the alternative

**Rollover Upsell** -- Credit some or all of a customer's previous purchases toward your next offer. Price your next offer at least 4x higher than the credit (25% discount max).

Four uses:
1. Re-engage old customers
2. Rescue upset customers (do rollover BEFORE refunding)
3. Steal competitors' upset customers (scrape negative reviews for leads)
4. Upsell current customers

> "I made personalized videos for 200 past customers offering them $4,000 of credit to return. We got about 20% to take the offer. One day of recording videos got us an extra ~$1,900,000 of annual revenue." -- $100M Money Models

#### Downsell Offers

> "They said no to THIS offer, not ALL offers. No means no for this thing, not no for everything." -- $100M Money Models

Never drop your price just to get someone to buy -- that is discounting, not downselling. Downsells are trades: if you give something, get something.

**5. Implement the three downsell types:**

**Payment Plan Downsell** (7-Step Process):

1. Reward paying in full with a discount
2. Offer 3rd-party financing, credit card, or layaway
3. Half now, half later
4. Temperature check: "On a scale from 1 to 10, how bad do you want this?" (Need 8+. If 7 or below, pivot to Feature Downsells.)
5. Split into three payments
6. Evenly spread payments -- ask "When do you get paid?" and align with paychecks
7. Free Trial with Penalty

Key principles:
- Present price with interest included, then offer prepay as the discount (reward prepayment, do not punish payment plans)
- Billing cadence data from 14,000 businesses: Monthly = 10.7% churn, Quarterly = 5%, Annual = 2%
- Start high (fewer bigger payments) and work your way down
- **Seesaw Downselling:** Ask "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" Then frame full payment as the best deal.

**Trial With Penalty** -- Customers try your product free so long as they meet your terms. If they skip requirements, they get charged a fee.

- Always get a credit card before explaining fees
- Always sell staying and paying: "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?"
- Three outcomes: (1) They love it -- set up automatic billing, (2) They hate it -- ask what they would change, get angry at yourself, upsell to higher tier, (3) They did not use it -- waive fees if they meet with you
- Just call it a "Free Trial" externally -- no one wants to hear about penalties upfront

**Feature Downsell** -- Lower prices by changing what customers get, not by discounting. Remove features from highest to lowest value.

- Remove features customers love and lower price a little = more people take the original offer (they see what they would lose)
- Name your feature combinations: First Class, Business Class, Economy -- name cheapest "The Minimum"
- After each downsell ask "Deal?" or "Fair enough?" -- people rarely say "no that is not fair"
- Feature downsell current customers who are not using features BEFORE they cancel
- One business tripled close rate from 25% to 75% by downselling without a guarantee -- and more people bought the full-price option WITH the guarantee

> "People who demand to pay less for the same thing are business terrorists. Do not negotiate with terrorists." -- $100M Money Models

> "After Each Downsell, Ask 'Deal?' Or 'Fair Enough?' This works astonishingly well." -- $100M Money Models

### Stage III: Get The Most Cash (Continuity Offers)

Continuity Offers provide ongoing value for ongoing payments. They boost profit per customer and give one last thing to sell. Make continuity the LAST offer in your sequence -- on its own it leaves you cash-strapped.

> "You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once." -- $100M Money Models, Continuity Offers Introduction

**6. Choose your Continuity Offer type:**

**Continuity Bonus Offer** -- Give the customer an awesome thing if they sign up for continuity today. The bonus should have more value than the first continuity payment. Focus advertising on the bonus, not the membership.

**Continuity Discount Offer** -- Give products or services free if the customer commits to buying more over time. Four ways to apply the discount: up front, at the end, spread over time, or after the first 1-2 payments.

**Waived Fee Offer** -- Charge a startup fee (3-5x monthly rate) for month-to-month. Waive it if they commit to a year. If they cancel early, they pay the fee.

- If more than 5% want to cancel early, the problem is your product, not your pricing
- Larger fee = more commitments, smaller fee = more up-front cash

**7. Set Continuity Pricing using the Continuity Pricing Multiplier:**

```
To get X% to choose continuity, make standalone price Y times the monthly rate:

50% choose continuity = standalone at 1.33x monthly
60% choose continuity = standalone at 1.66x monthly
70% choose continuity = standalone at 2x monthly
80% choose continuity = standalone at 2.33x monthly
90% choose continuity = standalone at 2.66x monthly
```

People pay 33% more to avoid continuity -- so charge 33% more for one-time and half will still buy it.

**8. Apply revenue multiplier tactics:**

- **4-Week Billing Cycle:** Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly. There are 13 four-week cycles in a year vs 12 months -- an 8.3% revenue increase for zero additional work. On 20% margins this increases annual profit by 41%.

> "This tip alone has made me millions in pure profit." -- $100M Money Models

- **Processing Fee:** Add five words: "Plus a 3% processing fee." On a 10% profit business, adding 3% to topline adds 30% to profit. Then offer to waive it if they provide a second form of payment (backup for failed charges).

> "In my life, I've never had anyone not buy because of a processing fee. But 3% added to your topline for no extra work goes straight to your bottom line." -- $100M Money Models

### Stage IV: Revenue Sequencing (Putting It All Together)

**9. Map your complete Money Model:**

For each offer in the sequence, define:
- What problem it solves (every offer solves a problem and reveals the next one)
- What type it is (Attraction, Upsell, Downsell, or Continuity)
- How much revenue it generates
- How it connects to the next offer

**10. Add Internal Plays (Sprinkles):**

Marketing campaigns to existing customers that cost $0 in acquisition, run at 90% margins, and feed all tiers. Run every 6 weeks using themed hooks. Expect 25% take rate from existing customers.

> "Internal plays are some of the major secrets to our success. A lack of these is usually the biggest gaping hole that I come across in businesses." -- Gym Launch Secrets, Ch. 26

**11. Calculate your LTGP:CAC ratio and validate CFA:**

- LTGP:CAC must be >= 3:1
- 30-day gross profit must cover acquisition cost
- If either fails, revisit your offer sequence before spending more on ads

## Key Principles

- **"Simple scales, fancy fails."** Build one stage at a time. Do not try to implement a full Money Model at once -- it will collapse. Perfect one offer at a time. ($100M Money Models, Section VI)

- **"It is less about having a hundred different products with the same offer, and more about having a hundred different offers for the same product."** The temptation when sales stall is to create new products. The correct move is to create new ways to offer existing products. ($100M Money Models, Downsell Conclusion)

- **"We make sales to get customers, not the other way around."** The purpose of a sale is not to extract money -- it is to acquire a customer with ongoing revenue potential. The initial sale is just the beginning. ($100M Money Models)

- **"No means no to this offer, not all offers."** When someone says no, they rejected this specific configuration of price, features, and terms. They did not reject you or your business. Hormozi makes up to nine offers in a single sales conversation. ($100M Money Models)

- **"People who demand to pay less for the same thing are business terrorists. Do not negotiate with terrorists."** Never give the same thing for less. Change what they get (feature downsell) or how they pay (payment plan). Maintain the position of a helpful guide. ($100M Money Models, Feature Downsells)

- **Each stage must pay for the next stage.** Money Models evolve: get customers reliably, then make them pay for themselves, then make them pay for other customers, then maximize long-term value. ($100M Money Models, Section VI)

- **"When your Money Model starts working, your business starts breaking -- that is part of the game."** Scaling problems is not scaling processes. ($100M Money Models)

- **Build it right or build it again.** Measure in quarters, not weeks. Rebuilding -- no matter how fast -- still takes longer than building it right the first time. ($100M Money Models)

- **Affiliate products can fill Money Model gaps without operational headache.** You do not need to build every offer yourself. ($100M Money Models)

## Output Structure

The skill should produce a complete Money Model document with these sections:

### 1. Current State Assessment
- Current offer(s) and pricing
- Current LTGP:CAC ratio (or estimate)
- Current churn rate and Pie Equation ceiling (HGM)
- Which Money Model stage the business is currently at

### 2. Attraction Offer Design
- Selected Attraction Offer type with reasoning
- Pricing and Client-Financed Acquisition math
- 30-day payback calculation

### 3. Upsell Offer Sequence
- Which of the four upsell types to implement and in what order
- Specific offers with pricing for each type
- BAMFAM touchpoints mapped

### 4. Downsell Offer Alternatives
- Payment Plan structure (using the 7-step process)
- Feature Downsell tiers with names
- Trial With Penalty terms (if applicable)

### 5. Continuity Offer Design
- Selected Continuity Offer type
- Continuity Pricing Multiplier calculation (standalone vs recurring ratio)
- 4-week billing cycle implementation
- Processing fee decision

### 6. Revenue Sequence Map
- Visual flow of offers from first contact through continuity
- Revenue projection per customer through the full sequence
- Internal Plays calendar (every 6 weeks)

### 7. Growth Multiplier Projection
- Current customer value, count, and speed
- Target multipliers for each dimension
- Projected growth (value x count x speed)

### 8. Implementation Stages
- Stage I actions (Attraction Offers only)
- Stage II actions (add Upsells and Downsells)
- Stage III actions (add Continuity)
- Metrics to hit before advancing to next stage

## Common Mistakes

- **Trying to implement all four offer types at once.** Build one stage at a time. Perfect one offer before adding the next. "Simple scales, fancy fails." ($100M Money Models, Section VI)

- **Creating new products instead of new offers for the same product.** When sales stall, the instinct is to build something new. The correct move is to create new ways to offer what you already have -- payment plans, bundles, downsells, trials, feature combinations. ($100M Money Models)

- **Discounting instead of downselling.** Never drop your price for the same thing. If they want to pay less, change what they get or how they pay. Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable. ($100M Money Models, Downsell Introduction)

- **Making continuity the first offer.** Continuity on its own leaves you cash-strapped. At $50/month you can get 40 out of 100 to buy vs 10 out of 100 at $1,000 one-time -- but you need upfront cash to fund growth. Continuity must be the LAST offer in sequence. ($100M Money Models, Continuity Introduction)

- **Treating the Anchor Upsell as fake.** If you present an anchor price you do not believe in, the customer will see through it. Actually build a premium offer you want people to buy. Some customers WILL buy the expensive thing. ($100M Money Models, Anchor Upsell)

- **Billing monthly instead of every 4 weeks.** You are leaving 8.3% of revenue on the table (41% of profit on 20% margins) by using calendar months instead of 4-week cycles. ($100M Money Models, Continuity Discount Offers)

- **Ignoring internal plays.** Marketing to existing customers costs $0 in acquisition and runs at 90% margins. Most businesses never market to their existing base. Run themed campaigns every 6 weeks. (Gym Launch Secrets, Ch. 26)

- **Focusing only on acquisition without fixing churn.** The Pie Equation proves that reducing churn has the same impact as increasing sign-ups. Everyone wants more people in the door, but few actively work on keeping them. (Gym Launch Secrets, Ch. 13-14)

- **Having a LTGP:CAC ratio below 3:1 and blaming advertising.** If your ratio is below 3:1, you likely have a business model problem (low LTGP), not an advertising problem. Fix the Money Model before spending more on ads. ($100M Leads)
