## When to Use

- When the user wants to create, sharpen, or restructure a pitch for an offer, product, or service
- When preparing for a sales call, client meeting, interview, or live selling situation and needs a conversational framework
- When an existing pitch feels weak, vague, or is not converting and needs diagnosis and improvement

---

## Core Framework

This skill combines two complementary Hormozi systems:

**1. The Value Equation** (from $100M Offers Ch.6) -- the diagnostic lens for WHY a pitch works or fails:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

Increase the top two. Decrease the bottom two. If the denominator reaches zero, value becomes infinite.

> "Beginner marketers focus on bigger claims (top of equation). Advanced marketers focus on reducing time delay and effort (bottom of equation) -- like Apple, Amazon, Netflix." -- $100M Offers Ch.6

**2. The CLOSER Sales Formula** (from Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6) -- the conversational framework for HOW to deliver a pitch in real time:

- **C - Clarify** why they are there. Establish the GAP between what they want and what they have not achieved.
- **L - Label** the problem. Recap their current state versus desired state with specific numbers.
- **O - Overview** past pains and failures. Categorize each past attempt and show which components were missing.
- **S - Sell the Vacation.** Paint the dream outcome. Nobody wants to hear about TSA lines -- they want Maui.
- **E - Explain** away concerns. Only three real obstacles exist: stalls, decision-maker, and price.
- **R - Reinforce** the decision. Prevent backouts with immediate next steps, a welcome gesture, and a choreographed first impression.

> "Sell them the vacation, not the flight. No one wants to hear about TSA lines, baggage claim, delays, layovers, or time changes. They want to hear about Maui." -- Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Understand the Offer

Extract from the user:
- Who the offer is for (specific avatar)
- What result it promises (dream outcome)
- How it is delivered (format, medium, timeline)
- Current price (if any)
- What feels weak, vague, or generic

Reflect back in simple terms before proceeding.

### Step 2: Diagnose Using the Value Equation

Break the offer into four parts and score each:

**Dream Outcome** -- Is it clear, specific, and desirable? Frame in terms of status: how will others perceive the prospect's achievement?

> "When writing copy, you can make it that much more powerful by talking about how other people will perceive the prospect's achievement." -- $100M Offers Ch.6

**Perceived Likelihood** -- Why should they believe this will work? Is proof visible? Is the path clear? A surgeon's 10,000th patient pays far more than the first because certainty commands premium.

**Time Delay** -- How fast do they get a win? Can this be shortened? Design for a fast emotional win within the first 48 hours.

> "Always try and incorporate short-term, immediate wins for a client. They just need to know they are on the right path and that they made the right decision trusting you." -- $100M Offers Ch.6

**Effort & Sacrifice** -- How hard does it feel? Can steps be removed, simplified, or done for them? In an ideal world, the prospect says yes and the result happens with zero additional effort.

Then explain where the offer is weak and suggest specific improvements.

### Step 3: Apply "The Pain Is The Pitch"

Before writing any pitch copy, articulate the prospect's pain more accurately than they can articulate it themselves. Spend 80% of the pitch setup on pain, 20% on solution.

> "The pain is the pitch. If you can articulate the pain a prospect is feeling accurately, they will almost always buy what you are offering." -- $100M Offers

> "The point of good writing is for the reader to understand. The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel understood." -- $100M Offers

Identify pain at three levels:
- **Surface pain** -- what the prospect says out loud ("I want to lose weight")
- **Deeper pain** -- the emotional and social consequences ("I avoid mirrors, I dread pool parties")
- **Core pain** -- what keeps them up at night, the thing they cannot stop thinking about when everything else is quiet

Use the **Pre-Sell Intake Questionnaire** approach (Gym Launch Secrets) to surface this pain. Before any sales conversation, have prospects answer questions that force them to articulate their pain points, goals, past failures, and commitment level:
- What are you here for?
- What is your dream goal?
- How long have you wanted this?
- Why do you think you will succeed this time?
- How long would it take to accomplish this on your own?

> "Answering these pre-framing questions forces them to slow down, gets them in the right frame of mind to make a purchase decision, and reminds them of their real pains and priorities." -- Gym Launch Secrets

These questions make the prospect sell themselves on why they need you.

### Step 4: Build the Value Stack

List all components of the offer. For each:
- Give it a benefit-driven name (not "Module 3: Nutrition" but "Personalized Lick-Your-Fingers-Good Meal Plan")
- What it does
- Why it matters
- Perceived value (assign a dollar amount)

Separate core offer from bonuses. Stack bonuses in logical order. Calculate total value. Compare with actual price. The value of bonuses should eclipse the core offer value.

Use the **Splinter Stack** technique from the Irresistible Offer Checklist (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.2): unbundle everything you already do and list each item separately to make the offer feel massive. Walk through each component separately rather than quoting one lump number.

> "Never discount the main offer. It teaches your customers that your prices are negotiable. Adding bonuses puts you in a position of strength and goodwill rather than weakness." -- $100M Offers Ch.14

### Step 5: Name the Offer Using M-A-G-I-C

Use the naming formula from $100M Offers Ch.16:

- **M** - Make a Magnetic Reason Why (Free, 88% off, New Year, Grand Opening)
- **A** - Announce Your Avatar (call out exactly who it is for)
- **G** - Give Them A Goal (the dream outcome, specific and tangible)
- **I** - Indicate a Time Interval (sets expectations and creates urgency)
- **C** - Complete With A Container Word (Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Accelerator, Sprint, System)

Use 3-5 components. Shorter and punchier is better. Rhyme and alliterate when possible but do not force it. Generate 5-10 variations.

> "You are not changing the actual offer -- only the wrapping paper. This one concept will get you leads forever." -- $100M Offers Ch.16

### Step 6: Design the Guarantee

Generate 3-4 options from the four types ($100M Offers Ch.15):

1. **Unconditional** -- No questions asked refund within X days
2. **Conditional** -- Refund if they complete specific actions and do not get results
3. **Service Guarantee** -- Keep working for free until the result is achieved (Hormozi's personal favorite -- zero refund risk with maximum persuasive power)
4. **Anti-Guarantee (Damaging Admission)** -- All sales are final, framed as proof of value: "Our methods are so powerful and so easy to copy that once you see them, they cannot be unseen"

Give each guarantee a memorable name. Write exact wording. Recommend the best option.

> "Changing the quality of a guarantee alone can 2-4x conversion." -- $100M Offers Ch.15

The math almost always favors strong guarantees: 130% of sales with 10% refund rate still nets 23% more revenue than 100% of sales with 5% refund rate.

> "Since I have been advising businesses to use this particular guarantee, I have yet to have a single person say a client took them up on it." -- Gym Launch Secrets (on the Service Guarantee)

### Step 7: Add Scarcity and Urgency

Only if it fits. Use honest, real constraints:

**Scarcity (quantity):** Limited seats/slots, limited supply of bonuses, never available again. For services: Total Business Cap, Growth Rate Cap, or Cohort Cap. Always sell out. Let everyone know you sold out.

**Urgency (time):** Cohort-Based Rolling (next group starts Monday), Rolling Seasonal, Pricing/Bonus-Based (promotion they could miss), Exploding Opportunity (arbitrage that decays with time).

> "Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain." -- $100M Offers Ch.12

> "The last 3% of time allotted in a campaign creates 50-60% of sales." -- $100M Offers Ch.13

Apply the **Hormozi Law** when sequencing the pitch: the longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make.

> "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off." -- $100M Offers Ch.11

This means: build value extensively before revealing price. Pre-frame with authority and social proof. Stack the value. Then -- and only then -- present the price.

### Step 8: Build the CLOSER Conversational Script

Write a complete conversational sales script following the CLOSER formula. For each step, provide:
- The specific questions to ask
- Transition language between steps
- What to listen for in responses

**C - Clarify:** "What made you reach out / come in today?" "What is your goal?" "Why now -- what changed?"

**L - Label:** Recap their current state vs desired state with specific numbers. "So right now you are at X, and you want to get to Y. Is that right?"

**O - Overview:** Walk through their past attempts. For each: "What happened? Why did it stop working?" Categorize failures to show which components were missing. Deploy the **Belief-Breaking Formula** here:

1. Say what they believe ("So you believe X approach works?")
2. Say why it is wrong ("The problem with that is...")
3. Say what is right ("What actually works is...")
4. Say WHY it is right ("Because it allows you to...")
5. Show proof -- third-party stats, testimonials, results

> The Belief-Breaking Formula was picked up from Linh Trinh and is applicable to any business. -- Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6

**S - Sell the Vacation:** Describe the destination, not the process. Paint the picture of their life after the transformation. Use status-framing: describe how others will perceive their achievement.

> "Nobody wants to hear about TSA lines -- they want Maui. Sell the dream outcome, not the programming details. Sell in the imaginary realm." -- Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6

**E - Explain away concerns:** Handle the three real objections (see Step 9).

**R - Reinforce:** Immediate next steps, welcome communication, swag or gesture. Create a choreographed first impression.

When delivering the pitch, use the **Prescriptive Assumed Close** -- the most powerful type of sale. Position yourself as an authority who prescribes what the customer needs, rather than presenting options they choose from.

> "The most powerful type of sale is a prescriptive assumed close where a person of authority prescribes what the customer needs and they simply comply." -- Gym Launch Secrets

Do not ask "Would you like to add...?" Instead, prescribe: "For your program, you will need X, Y, and Z. Here is how we set that up."

### Step 9: Handle Objections Using the Three Real Objections

Per Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6 and Bonus chapter, every objection falls into one of three categories:

**1. Stalls** ("I need to think about it")

- Use the **Rocking Chair Close:** "When we say 'I need to think about it,' we don't go home and sit in a rocking chair staring at the wall. You'll leave, your mind fills with groceries and laundry, and four days later you catch a glimpse in the mirror and think 'I'm too busy for that right now.' In those two seconds, you'll have made the decision."
- Use the **Fully Informed Decision Frame:** "I'm not asking you to decide yes or no today. I'm asking you to make a fully informed decision. The only way to do that is on the inside, not the outside. Get on the inside, see if everything is true, then decide whether to keep it."
- Use the **Three Questions Framework** to help them through the decision: "1) Do you think this program will help you? 2) Do you have the money or access to it? 3) Am I the person you want supporting you? If all three are yes, the decision is made."

> "Decisions don't take time, they take information, because thinking is instantaneous." -- Gym Launch Secrets

> "People hate making decisions because they hate making mistakes. Your responsibility is to convince them that the REAL mistake is NOT making a decision." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**2. Decision-Maker** ("I need to talk to my spouse")

The best defense is prevention: for high-ticket sales, use a setter-closer model where the setter confirms the decision-maker will be present and collects a nonrefundable consultation deposit before the meeting.

> "The setter collects a $50-$100 nonrefundable consultation deposit, confirms the decision-maker will be present, and ensures the prospect brings their payment method." -- Gym Launch Secrets

When the objection arises anyway, use **Agreement Stacking** -- a sequence of yes-questions that builds toward the logical conclusion that the decision is already made:

"I totally understand you want to include your [spouse/partner]. Let me ask you a few questions. Do you think [spouse] wants you to [achieve dream outcome -- e.g., be healthier, make more money, feel confident]?" (Yes.) "And would they support you making a positive change in your life?" (Yes.) "So the real question is not whether to do it, but how to share the exciting news. Would it be easier if we got started today and you told them about the great decision you made -- or would you prefer to bring them in so I can walk them through everything too?"

If they still cannot commit, offer a **Delayed Payment Option**: start the program today with the first payment delayed 7-14 days, giving them time to discuss with their partner while already experiencing the value.

> "Every obstacle overcome should start with 'I totally agree' or 'I totally understand,' then transition to 'Let me ask you a question...'" -- Gym Launch Secrets Bonus

**3. Price** ("I can't afford it")

- Use the **Identity Swap Close:** The prospect is going from someone unsatisfied to someone satisfied -- with that new identity comes new priorities for where they spend money. "We tend to vote with our dollars about the things we care about."
- Use the **Best-Case / Worst-Case Close:** "Best case you get [dream outcome]. Worst case you tell me I suck, I write you a check, and you get [X weeks] of free [service]. Both options are risk free. But the only thing guaranteed not to help you is walking out of here today."

> "Right now what we are talking about is you going from a [person] who is unsatisfied with [situation] to a [person] who IS satisfied. Part of that new identity comes with new priorities for where you spend your money. We only need to vote YES once, so you might as well do it now." -- Gym Launch Secrets Bonus

**Universal pattern:** Always agree first, then ask a question. Never say "but" or "I disagree."

> "Every obstacle overcome should start with 'I totally agree' or 'I totally understand,' then transition to 'Let me ask you a question...'" -- Gym Launch Secrets Bonus

Stack overcomes like punching combinations -- each one dents the prospect's apprehension.

### Step 10: Write the Pitch Versions

Apply the **Twelve Copy Commandments** (Gym Launch Secrets) when writing all pitch copy:

1. **Headline comes first** -- curiosity is king
2. **Say what only you can say** -- unique proof and perspective
3. **Call out who you are looking for AND who you are NOT** -- repel the wrong prospects
4. **Reason why** -- use the word "because"
5. **Damaging admission** -- own your flaws for authenticity
6. **Show, do not tell** -- describe emotions and scenarios, not features
7. **Tie benefits to status** -- frame how others will perceive their achievement
8. **Use urgency and scarcity legitimately** -- real constraints only
9. **Implied authority** -- mention credentials, numbers, notable clients
10. **P.S. = Power Sentence** -- the second most read part after the headline
11. **Clear next steps** -- Step 1, Step 2, Step 3
12. **Third-grade reading level** -- put every ad through a reading-level tool

> "Fancy equals friction. Simple equals sales. I put every ad I have through a free reading-level tool and continue to edit the copy until it is at a third-grade reading level or below." -- Gym Launch Secrets

> Instead of "make more sales," write: "You will be amazed when you hear customers read their credit card numbers to you over the phone after reading two magic sentences. It will feel like taking orders." Note the description and the emotions. -- Gym Launch Secrets

#### Short Version (for ads, bios, hooks)
- 1-2 lines
- Clear outcome + specific audience + time or mechanism
- Apply Commandments 1, 3, 7

#### Medium Version (for landing page or email)
- Pain articulation (make them feel understood)
- Dream outcome (sell the vacation)
- What they get (value stack summary)
- Why it works (proof)
- CTA
- Apply Commandments 1-8, 11

#### Long Version (full pitch)
- Callout to audience (Commandment 3)
- Pain amplification -- 80% of the setup (Commandment 6: show, do not tell)
- Desired outcome with status-framing (Commandment 7)
- Solution explanation (sell the vacation, not the flight)
- Value stack with assigned dollar values (Splinter Stack)
- Price vs value contrast (Price Anchor)
- Guarantee (named, with exact wording)
- Scarcity and urgency (Commandment 8)
- CTA (Commandment 11)
- P.S. = Power Sentence (Commandment 10)
- Damaging admission somewhere in the body (Commandment 5)

## Key Principles

### The Three Transfers of Sales
Every sale requires three transfers (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5):
1. **Belief** in the product -- that it actually works
2. **Confidence** that the person can reach their goals with it
3. **Conviction** that they can overcome their circumstances to use it

Without transferring all three, you cannot help people make the decision to change.

> "If you don't like selling, it means you don't like helping people make decisions to help themselves." -- Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5

### The Conviction Close
Your conviction must be stronger than the prospect's skepticism. This conviction cannot be faked -- it comes from delivering results enough times that you have absolute certainty.

> "You must outwork your self-doubt. You must be so confident in your delivery, because you have done it so many times, that you know that this person will succeed." -- $100M Offers

Hormozi sold $42,000/year programs to gym owners making $35,000/year -- as a kid in his twenties. His conviction was built on 33 in-person gym turnarounds averaging $42,000 in additional sales in 21 days.

> "Look at yourself in the mirror at night, alone, and if your conviction remains unshakable, you are ready." -- $100M Offers

### Sell the Vacation, Not the Plane Flight
No one wants your membership, your software, your consulting hours. They want the outcome -- the weight loss, the revenue growth, the peace of mind. Stop selling the vehicle and start selling the destination.

> "No one wants to hear about TSA lines, baggage claim, delays, layovers, or time changes. They want to hear about Maui." -- Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6

### The Pain Is The Pitch
If you can articulate the pain a prospect is feeling more accurately than they can, they will almost always buy. Spend most of the pitch articulating the problem so deeply that they feel truly seen.

> "The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel understood, not for them to understand you." -- $100M Offers

### Status Is The Meta-Driver
All dream outcomes are ultimately about status -- perceived increase or decrease in relative standing. Even rejecting a Lamborghini for a minivan is a status play. Frame every benefit in terms of how others will perceive the prospect's achievement.

> "Your golf buddies' jaws will drop when they see your ball soar 40 yards past theirs. They will ask you what changed. Only you will know." -- $100M Offers Ch.6

### Never Discount -- Add Bonuses Instead
Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable and puts you in a position of weakness. Adding bonuses increases perceived value while maintaining price integrity.

> "Adding bonuses to increase value to close the deal is far superior to cutting prices." -- $100M Offers Ch.14

### The Hormozi Law
The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. Build value extensively before revealing price. Pre-frame, stack proof, articulate pain, paint the dream -- then present the price.

> "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make. The longer the runway, the bigger the plane that can take off." -- $100M Offers Ch.11

### The Irresistible Offer Checklist
When constructing the full pitch, ensure these eight elements are present (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.2):

1. **Pre-Frame** -- position authority and social proof before the prospect arrives
2. **Price Anchor** -- drop a higher price point before presenting the actual price
3. **Splinter Stack** -- unbundle everything and list each item separately
4. **Scarcity** -- limited spots or availability using real constraints
5. **Urgency** -- time-based pressure with real deadlines
6. **Crazy Guarantee** -- remove all risk from the prospect
7. **Bribe** -- an irresistible immediate incentive for acting now
8. **Downsell Your Upsell** -- charge the most at the start when excitement is highest, then make ongoing service feel like a bargain

## Common Mistakes

1. **Explaining the process instead of painting the outcome.** "We use a proprietary 12-step method" means nothing. "Your phone will ring with qualified leads within 48 hours" means everything. Sell the vacation, not the flight.

2. **Spending all the pitch on the solution and none on the pain.** The prospect must feel understood before they will listen to your solution. Spend 80% of setup on articulating their pain.

3. **Discounting when they push back on price.** Never lower the price -- add bonuses, stack guarantees, or change terms. Discounting destroys pricing power permanently.

4. **Listing features instead of status-framed benefits.** "Includes 24/7 support" is a feature. "Your team will wonder how you suddenly have answers to everything" is a status-framed benefit. Apply Copy Commandment 7.

5. **Skipping the guarantee or making it weak.** A strong guarantee almost always pays for itself. The math favors bold guarantees. The Service Guarantee (keep working until result achieved) has zero refund risk.

6. **Using fake scarcity.** Honest scarcity -- simply defining your actual capacity and advertising it -- is the most ethical and effective form. Fake countdown timers erode trust.

7. **Arguing with objections instead of agreeing first.** Every objection response must start with "I totally understand" then transition to a question. Never say "but" or "I disagree."

8. **Lacking conviction.** If you do not genuinely believe your product works, no script will save you. Build conviction through repetition of results, not through affirmations.

> "You don't become confident by shouting affirmations in the mirror. You become confident by giving yourself a stack of undeniable proof that you are who you say you are. Outwork your self-doubt." -- $100M Money Models

9. **Presenting options instead of prescribing.** The most powerful sale is a prescriptive assumed close where you tell the customer what they need and they comply. Do not ask "Would you like to add...?" -- prescribe what is included in their plan.

10. **Writing above a third-grade reading level.** Fancy equals friction. Simple equals sales. Run every piece of pitch copy through a reading-level tool and edit until it passes.
