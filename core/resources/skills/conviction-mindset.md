## When to Use

- When the user hesitates to charge premium prices, feels imposter syndrome, or fears rejection in sales conversations, interviews, or client calls
- When the user needs to internalize why premium pricing is a moral obligation and how conviction transfers through tone and certainty
- When the user is considering giving up on a market, offer, or business and needs the persistence frameworks to keep going

## Core Framework

### The Conviction Equation

Conviction is the invisible multiplier that makes every other Hormozi framework effective. Without it, the best offer, the strongest guarantee, and the most compelling pitch all fall flat. Conviction is not faked -- it is built through repeated delivery of results.

**The equation:** Your conviction must be stronger than the prospect's skepticism. The gap between your certainty and their doubt determines whether the sale happens.

> "You must outwork your self-doubt. You must be so confident in your delivery, because you have done it so many times, that you know that this person will succeed."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers

**The test:** Look at yourself in the mirror at night, alone, and if your conviction remains unshakable, you are ready. If it wavers, you need more reps delivering results before you can ethically sell at premium prices.

**The proof:** Hormozi sold $42,000/year programs to gym owners who made $35,000/year -- as a kid in his twenties telling grown men he would help them make more money. His conviction was built on 33 in-person gym turnarounds averaging $42,000 in additional sales in 21 days. That stack of evidence made his certainty unshakable.

### The Three Transfers of Sales (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5)

At its heart, every sale is made up of three transfers:

1. **Belief in the product** -- the prospect must believe your solution actually works
2. **Confidence in themselves** -- the prospect must believe THEY can reach their goals using it
3. **Conviction about circumstances** -- the prospect must believe they can overcome their specific obstacles

> "If you do not like selling, it means you do not like helping people make decisions to help themselves. Sales is made up of three transfers: belief in the product, confidence the person can reach their goals, and conviction they can overcome circumstances. Without transferring these three things, you cannot help people."
> -- Alex Hormozi, Gym Launch Secrets

**Critical insight:** Before every sales conversation, check your OWN belief, confidence, and conviction. If you do not genuinely believe your product works, that the prospect can succeed, and that their circumstances are surmountable, fix that first. Then transfer those three things through stories, proof, and direct experience.

**What NOT to do:** Do not just present features and prices and hope the prospect convinces themselves. If you are not actively transferring belief, confidence, and conviction, you are not selling -- you are presenting.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Phase 1: Diagnose Your Conviction State

1. **Audit your belief:** Do you genuinely believe your product/service works? If not, what evidence would you need? List every successful outcome you have delivered. If the list is short, go deliver more results before selling at premium.
2. **Audit your confidence in the prospect:** Do you believe this specific person can succeed? If you do not believe in your prospects, you will communicate that through your tone -- and they will feel it.
3. **Audit your conviction about circumstances:** Do you believe circumstances can be overcome? List the three hardest situations your past clients faced and how they succeeded anyway. These become your conviction stories.

### Phase 2: Build Your Evidence Stack

4. **Stack undeniable proof:** Collect every result, testimonial, case study, and metric from your delivery history. This is not for the prospect -- it is for YOU.

> "You don't become confident by shouting affirmations in the mirror. You become confident by giving yourself a stack of undeniable proof that you are who you say you are. Outwork your self-doubt."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Money Models (Final Thoughts)

5. **If your evidence stack is thin:** Over-deliver first. Use the Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum from $100M Offers Ch.10: "Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction." Over-deliver like crazy at first to create cash flow, testimonials, and case studies. The initial over-delivery builds the evidence stack that funds your conviction.

6. **Price conviction test:** Practice saying your price out loud until you can do it with complete confidence and zero flinching.

> "Charge as high a price as you can say out loud without cracking a smile."
> -- Dan Kennedy, quoted in $100M Offers Ch.5

### Phase 3: Internalize the Moral Argument for Premium Pricing

7. **Understand the Virtuous Cycle of Price** ($100M Offers Ch.5): Higher price creates higher emotional investment from clients, higher perceived value, better adherence, better results, better clients who are easier to satisfy, and more margin to reinvest in excellence. Lowering prices creates the opposite death spiral.

8. **Internalize "Those who pay the most, pay the most attention"** ($100M Offers Ch.5): If clients must do something to achieve results, getting them maximally invested through pricing ensures adherence. Charging premium is not greedy -- it is the mechanism that drives client success.

9. **Understand the wine study proof:** Higher price literally equals higher perceived value. The same wine was rated better when labeled expensive. People want to buy expensive things -- they just need a reason. Your job is to provide that reason through your offer, not to lower the price.

10. **Apply the moral reframe:** Charging less is not more ethical. It is less ethical because it produces worse client outcomes. Lower prices attract worse clients, reduce their commitment, and strip you of the margin needed to deliver excellence.

> "There is no strategic benefit to being the second cheapest in the marketplace, but there is for being the most expensive."
> -- Dan Kennedy, quoted in $100M Offers Ch.5

### Phase 4: Deploy Conviction Frameworks in Sales

11. **Use the Fully Informed Decision Frame** ($100M Offers) when a prospect says "I need to think about it":

> "I'm not asking you to decide yes or no today... I'm asking you to make a fully informed decision, that is all."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers

Tell them: "I am not asking you to decide yes or no today. I am asking you to make a fully informed decision. The only way to do that is from the inside, not the outside. Get on the inside, see if everything is true, then decide whether to keep it." Combined with a strong guarantee, this eliminates the psychological weight of commitment.

12. **Never discount -- add bonuses instead** ($100M Offers Ch.14): When a prospect pushes back on price, do not lower the price. Increase the value. Add bonuses, stack guarantees, but never discount. Discounting trains clients that your prices are negotiable and puts you in a position of weakness.

13. **Remember that scarcity and urgency must be REAL -- conviction, not scripting** (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.4): Hormozi discovered that his sales team did 2x the sales in the last 5 days of a 21-day campaign compared to the first 2 weeks combined. The difference was not scripting -- it was conviction. As they neared the deadline, their TONES changed because the urgency became REAL to them.

> "The human mind is very good at picking out BS, so you need to be convinced that the scarcity and urgency are true."
> -- Alex Hormozi, Gym Launch Secrets

### Phase 5: Apply Persistence Frameworks

14. **The Many-Sided Die** ($100M Leads, final chapter): Everyone gets a die with a different number of sides, but only one side is green. Each time you roll green, another red side turns green. The more you roll, the more greens you get. You cannot lose if you do not quit. The die you are given does not matter if you roll enough times.

There are only two guarantees: the more you roll the better you get, and if you quit you lose.

> "You cannot lose if you do not quit."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Leads

15. **Separate identity from offer performance** ($100M Offers, Section V):

> "If your offer doesn't work, it doesn't mean you suck. It means your offer sucks. Big difference. You only suck if you stop trying."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers

If you try one hundred offers, you will succeed. Stop personalizing failure -- it is not about you, it is about the offer.

16. **Apply the Niche Slap Principle** ($100M Offers Ch.4): Entrepreneurs hop from niche to niche thinking the market is the problem, when the real problem is they have not committed long enough to find their Grand Slam Offer. All markets have unpleasant characteristics. The grass is never greener. Pick one and commit until you figure it out.

17. **Use the "Right vs Rich" mental model** ($100M Offers Ch.6): Stop arguing about how people "ought" to behave and start capitalizing on how they actually behave. People pay $200 for supplements but will not pay $29/month for a gym membership -- because the perceived value (low effort, fast results) is higher. The product with better perceived value wins, not the objectively better product.

## Key Principles

### On Conviction

- **Conviction cannot be faked.** Prospects can feel the difference between genuine conviction and performance. Build real proof first. ($100M Offers)
- **Conviction is built through repetition.** Do the work enough times that you know it works. Then, when someone challenges your price, your unshakable certainty will be felt. ($100M Offers)
- **Selling is serving.** The act of convincing someone to invest in themselves IS the service. Not selling them is actually failing them, because you know they need help and you let them walk away unchanged. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5)
- **Scarcity conviction beats scripted scarcity.** Your tone changes naturally when you genuinely believe time is running out for the prospect. If someone does not buy today, the likelihood they reschedule and follow through is only about 10%. Make the urgency real to yourself first. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.4)

### On Premium Pricing as Moral Obligation

- **Those who pay the most, pay the most attention.** Higher investment equals higher commitment equals better outcomes. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **Charging more to help more.** Low prices mean low emotional investment, low perceived value, worse results, more demanding clients, and less money to reinvest in service. The cheapest customers ask for the most. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **The Virtuous Cycle of Price.** Raising prices creates a self-reinforcing positive cycle. The goal is not to be slightly above market -- it is to be so much higher that the consumer thinks "there must be something entirely different going on here." ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **Price is a signal.** When your pricing does not make people question whether what you are doing is legal, you are probably not charging enough. Hormozi's father asked "Is it legal what you're doing?" when he learned 500+ gym owners each paid $42,000/year. ($100M Offers)

### On Persistence and Self-Doubt

- **Confidence is evidence, not affirmation.** You do not become confident by shouting affirmations in the mirror. You become confident by giving yourself a stack of undeniable proof that you are who you say you are. ($100M Money Models)
- **The Many-Sided Die.** Most people watch others roll green and assume they have a better die. In reality, they just rolled more times. ($100M Leads)
- **Yeses give opportunity, nos give feedback.** Failure is a requisite for success. When someone says no, ask why. Their answers give you ammo to get the next person to say yes. Rack up failures as fast as you can to pay down your "no tax." ($100M Leads)
- **You either win or you learn.** A failed campaign is an investment in knowledge, not a loss. ($100M Leads)
- **Patience is still the fastest way.** You either build it right or you build it again. And again. And again. ($100M Money Models)

### On Over-Delivery as Conviction Fuel

- **Over-deliver first, optimize later.** The Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum: create flow, monetize flow, then add friction. Generate demand first, get people saying yes, then optimize operations. ($100M Offers Ch.10)
- **Over-delivery builds your evidence stack.** In your first 90 days with any client, take on extra projects, document processes, create systems. Once you have proven value, you can optimize. The initial over-delivery builds trust, reputation, and the conviction you need to charge premium. ($100M Offers)
- **Abundant exchange.** What clients come in for initially is what they give you money to fix. But what they come back for is everything else you gave them that they did not pay for -- community, attention, solving problems they did not know they had. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **The second worst thing is they say no. The worst thing is if they would have said yes but you never asked.** The cost of not asking is always higher than the cost of hearing no. ($100M Money Models)

## Output Structure

When applying this skill, produce the following:

### 1. Conviction Audit
- Current belief score (1-10) in product/service
- Current confidence score (1-10) in prospect's ability to succeed
- Current conviction score (1-10) that circumstances are surmountable
- Evidence stack: list of delivered results, testimonials, metrics
- Gap analysis: what evidence is missing, and how to get it

### 2. Premium Pricing Justification
- The moral argument: why charging more produces better client outcomes
- The Virtuous Cycle applied: how higher price improves each link in the chain
- The conviction test: can you say your price without flinching?
- Price positioning: framed as category-of-one, not second-cheapest

### 3. Conviction Transfer Plan
- For each of the Three Transfers (belief, confidence, conviction): specific stories, proof points, and techniques to deploy
- The Fully Informed Decision Frame scripted for the user's context
- Scarcity/urgency statements that are TRUE and that the user genuinely believes

### 4. Persistence Framework
- Many-Sided Die application: how many rolls has the user made, and what green sides have appeared?
- Identity separation: distinguish "my offer sucks" from "I suck"
- Niche Slap check: is the user hopping niches or committed to improving the offer?
- Next actions: specific rolls to make this week

## Common Mistakes

- **Faking conviction with scripts or bravado.** Prospects can feel the difference between genuine conviction and performance. If you do not have real evidence, go deliver results before selling at premium. Do not try to shortcut this. ($100M Offers)
- **Discounting instead of adding value.** Never lower the price -- it trains customers your prices are negotiable. Add bonuses instead. Discounting puts you in a position of weakness; bonuses put you in a position of goodwill. ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **Using fake scarcity or reading urgency scripts in monotone.** The human mind is very good at picking out BS. If you do not genuinely believe the urgency, your tone will betray you. Make it real to yourself first. (Gym Launch Secrets Ch.4)
- **Personalizing failure.** A failed offer does not mean you are a failure. It means the offer needs work. Separate your identity from your offer's performance. ($100M Offers Section V)
- **Switching niches when the offer is not working.** The instinct is to change markets. The correct move is usually to change the offer within the same market. Commit to your niche long enough to find the Grand Slam Offer. ($100M Offers Ch.4)
- **Thinking motivation replaces evidence.** Affirmations and positive thinking do not build conviction. Undeniable proof through action does. ($100M Money Models)
- **Letting prospects leave undecided.** Decisions do not take time -- they take information. When someone says "I need to think about it," they will not go home and sit in a rocking chair staring at the wall. They will get busy with life and never come back. Helping them decide NOW is an act of service, not pressure. (Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Arguing with objections instead of agreeing first.** Every obstacle overcome should start with "I totally agree" or "I totally understand," then transition to "Let me ask you a question..." Never say "but" or "I disagree." (Gym Launch Secrets)
