## When to Use

- When you have one offer and want to build a full revenue system around it (upsells, downsells, continuity)
- When you want to increase lifetime value, average order value, or speed of payment per customer
- When converting a service into a productized offer ecosystem with multiple price points and entry paths

## Core Framework: The Money Model

A Money Model is a deliberate sequence of offers designed to increase how many customers you get, how much they pay, and how fast they pay it. It combines four offer types in a specific order.

> "It is less about having a hundred different products with the same offer, and more about having a hundred different offers for the same product. Simple scales, fancy fails." -- $100M Money Models

**The Money Model Formula:**

```
Money Model = Attraction Offers + Upsell Offers + Downsell Offers + Continuity Offers
```

**Growth Multiplier Formula:**

```
Growth = (Customer Value Multiplier) x (Customer Count Multiplier) x (Speed Multiplier)
2x each = 8x growth. 3x each = 27x growth.
```

**Build in stages -- never all at once:**

- Stage I: Get Cash (Attraction Offers)
- Stage II: Get More Cash (Upsell + Downsell Offers)
- Stage III: Get The Most Cash (Continuity Offers)

> "Patience is still the fastest way to get to your goal. You either build it right or you build it again. And again. And again." -- $100M Money Models, Section VI

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Map Your Current Offer

Identify what you sell today and how it is delivered. Determine which of the four offer types it is (most businesses only have one -- usually an Attraction Offer or a standalone core offer with no sequence around it).

Ask the user:
- What do you sell right now?
- What happens after someone buys? Is there a next step?
- What problems does your product solve, and what NEW problems does it create?

> "Every offer you make solves a problem and simultaneously creates a new one. The new problem is your next upsell opportunity." -- $100M Money Models

### Step 2: Design the Attraction Offer

The Attraction Offer turns strangers into customers. It should generate enough cash to cover acquisition cost within 30 days (Client-Financed Acquisition).

Five proven Attraction Offer types from the book:
1. **Win Your Money Back** -- customer pays, earns money back as store credit if they meet criteria
2. **Giveaways** -- advertise a grand prize, offer everyone else a promotional discount
3. **Decoy Offer** -- free or cheap version side-by-side with premium (80% take rate on premium achievable)
4. **Buy X Get Y Free** -- raise prices first, then give stuff away to preserve margins
5. **Pay Less Now or Pay More Later** -- get card on file, choice of discounted now vs full later

Key principle: "We don't get customers to make a sale, we make sales to get customers."

### Step 3: Design Delivery Vehicles Using the Delivery Cube

Before building upsells and downsells, map HOW you deliver value across six dimensions. This determines what you can offer at each price point.

**The Delivery Cube (6 Dimensions):**

| Dimension | Options |
|---|---|
| 1. Personal attention | One-on-one, small group, one-to-many |
| 2. Client effort level | DIY (figure it out), DWY (you teach them), DFY (you do it for them) |
| 3. Live delivery medium | In-person, phone, email, text, Zoom, chat |
| 4. Recording/consumption format | Audio, video, written |
| 5. Response speed | 24/7, 9-5, within 5 min, within 1 hour, within 24 hours |
| 6. The 10x/1-10th test | See below |

**The 10x / 1-10th Stretch Test:**

Ask two questions to stretch your thinking:
1. If my customers paid me 10x my price (or $100,000), what would I provide?
2. If they paid me 1/10th the price and I had to make my product even MORE valuable, how would I do that?

> "At 10x: I would fly to their location, live with them, and do everything for them. At 1/10th: I would create automated tools, pre-built templates, and self-service systems. The 1/10th version often reveals scalable solutions that are high value and low cost." -- $100M Offers Ch.10

Key insight: High-value one-to-many solutions (tools, templates, recorded content) have high one-time creation cost but infinitely low ongoing cost.

### Step 4: Design Upsell Offers

Upsells get existing customers to spend more. There are four distinct types:

**4a. Classic Upsell**
Offer the solution to the customer's next problem the moment they become aware of it. Use the "You can't have X without Y" structure.

Always apply BAMFAM: Book A Meeting From A Meeting. End every appointment by scheduling the next one.

> "The thing you sell the most isn't always the thing you make the most profit on." -- $100M Money Models

**4b. Menu Upsell (4 Tactics)**
This system took Hormozi ten years to learn:

1. **Unselling** -- tell customers what they do NOT need. This builds trust. Keep products specifically to cross them out.
2. **Prescription Upselling** -- give detailed, personalized instructions on how to use the product as if they already own it. Never ask "do you want it?" -- tell them how they will use it.
3. **A/B Upselling** -- ask which option they prefer rather than whether they want to buy. "Chocolate or vanilla?" Either answer is a purchase.
4. **Card On File** -- ask "Want to use the card on file?" to lower friction.

> "Instead of asking if customers wanted to buy a product, yes or no, we ask which product they prefer: A or B. Either choice results in an upsell." -- $100M Money Models

**4c. Anchor Upsell**
Present your most expensive premium offer first (5-10x the price). Get "The Gasp." Come to the rescue with your main offer.

Steps: Present anchor (5-10x price) -> Get The Gasp -> Ask if they care about premium features -> Present main offer -> Ask for payment.

> "If you treat the anchor like a fake, the customer will too. Actually make a premium offer you want people to buy. Some customers WILL buy the super expensive thing." -- $100M Money Models

**4d. Rollover Upsell**
Credit past purchases toward a new, more expensive offer. Works for re-engaging old customers, rescuing upset customers, and stealing competitors' upset customers.

Formula: Next offer price should be at least 4x the rollover credit (25% discount max).

> "I made personalized videos for 200 past customers offering them $4,000 of credit to return. We got about 20% to take the offer. One day of recording videos got us an extra ~$1,900,000 of annual revenue." -- $100M Money Models

### Step 5: Design Downsell Offers

Downsells turn nos into yeses by changing how they pay or what they get. Never drop your price for the same product -- that is discounting, not downselling.

> "People who demand to pay less for the same thing are business terrorists. I don't negotiate with terrorists." -- $100M Money Models

**5a. Payment Plan Downsell (7-Step Process)**

1. Reward paying in full with a discount
2. Offer 3rd-party financing, credit card, or layaway
3. Half now, half later
4. Temperature check (1-10 scale -- need 8+ to continue; 7 or below, pivot to Feature Downsells)
5. Split into three payments
6. Evenly spread payments (ask "When do you get paid?" and align)
7. Free Trial with Penalty

Key technique -- Seesaw Downselling: Ask "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" Frame full payment as the best deal.

Present price with interest included, then offer prepay as the discount. Same math, but the customer feels rewarded rather than penalized.

Billing cadence data from 14,000 businesses: Monthly = 10.7% churn. Quarterly = 5%. Annual = 2%.

**5b. Feature Downsell**
Lower prices by changing what customers get, not by discounting. Remove features from highest to lowest value.

Formula: Take something away + lower the price + ask "How about now?"

- Remove features customers love and lower price a little = more people take the original offer
- Remove features customers hate and lower price a lot = more people take the downsell
- Name your feature combinations: First Class, Business Class, Economy -- name cheapest "The Minimum"
- After each downsell ask "Deal?" or "Fair enough?" -- people rarely say that is not fair

> "People only see the value of something after you threaten to take it away." -- $100M Money Models

**5c. Trial With Penalty**
Customers try your product free if they meet your terms. If they skip requirements, they pay a fee.

- Always get a credit card before explaining fees
- Always sell staying and paying: "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?"
- Break fees up per missed criteria rather than one lump sum
- Three outcomes: (1) They love it -- upsell to longer term. (2) They hate it -- get angry at yourself, upsell to better fit. (3) They did not use it -- waive fees if they meet with you.

### Step 6: Design Continuity Offers

Continuity Offers provide ongoing value for ongoing payments. Make this the LAST offer in your sequence -- continuity alone leaves you cash-strapped.

> "You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once." -- $100M Money Models

**Three Continuity Offer types:**

**6a. Continuity Bonus Offer**
Give an awesome bonus if they sign up for continuity today. The bonus should have more value than the first continuity payment. Advertise the bonus, not the membership.

**6b. Continuity Discount Offer**
Give products or services free if the customer commits to buying more over time. Apply discount up front, at the end, spread over time, or after the first 1-2 payments.

**6c. Waived Fee Offer**
Charge a startup fee (3-5x monthly rate) for month-to-month. Waive the fee if they commit to a year. If they cancel early, the fee applies.

> "If more than 5% of people want to cancel early, the problem is your product, not your pricing." -- $100M Money Models

**Continuity Pricing Multiplier (data-driven ratios):**

| Standalone price vs monthly | % who choose continuity |
|---|---|
| 1.33x | 50% |
| 1.66x | 60% |
| 2x | 70% |
| 2.33x | 80% |
| 2.66x | 90% |

> "People pay 33% more to avoid continuity -- so charge 33% more for one-time and half will still buy it." -- $100M Money Models

### Step 7: Apply Revenue Multiplier Hacks

After the core Money Model is designed, apply these profit levers:

**4-Week Billing Cycle:** Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly. 13 cycles per year vs 12 = 8.3% more revenue for zero extra work. On 20% margins, this increases annual profit by 41%.

**Processing Fee:** Add "plus a 3% processing fee." On a 10% profit business, 3% added to topline adds 30% to profit.

> "In my life, I've never had anyone not buy because of a processing fee." -- $100M Money Models

**Internal Plays (Sprinkles):** Every 6 weeks, create a themed campaign for existing customers. Price at $150-$199. Expect 25% take rate. Run at 90% margin. Use the consultation to upsell further.

> "Internal plays are some of the major secrets to our success. Maybe 5% of businesses I know actually do this right." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Downsell Your Upsell:** Charge the highest price at the beginning when excitement is highest (e.g., $100/week for 12 weeks), then offer ongoing service at a significantly lower rate (e.g., $50/week). The ongoing rate feels like a bargain.

> "No one wants a membership. They want a result and a deadline. People will pay two to four times the price for something if it is NOT on continuity." -- Gym Launch Secrets

### Step 8: Apply the Sales-to-Fulfillment Continuum

Once the Money Model is designed, decide how to deliver it.

**The mantra: "Create flow. Monetize flow. Then add friction."**

1. Over-deliver first to create cash flow and testimonials
2. Use cash flow to fix operations and create efficiency
3. You may not need to change what you offer -- just create systems that deliver the same value at lower cost

> "Create flow, monetize flow, then add friction. Generate demand first, get people saying yes, then optimize operations." -- $100M Offers Ch.10

## Key Principles

- **A Money Model is a sequence of offers, not a single offer.** Every offer solves a problem and reveals the next problem. Each problem is a revenue opportunity.
- **Build one stage at a time.** Perfect your Attraction Offer before adding upsells. Perfect upsells before adding continuity. Each stage must pay for the next.
- **100 offers for the same product, not 100 products.** When sales stall, the temptation is to create new products. The correct move is new ways to offer existing products.
- **Never drop price without changing the offer.** Downsells are trades, not concessions. If you give something, get something back.
- **Present higher prices first, always.** Anchor effect makes every subsequent offer feel like a better deal.
- **Upsell as many times as it makes sense to.** "The second worst thing that happens is they say no. The worst thing is if they would've said yes but you never asked."
- **Timing is everything.** Offer the right thing when customers realize they need it. You can make as many offers as you like if the timing is right.
- **Make it easy to buy.** Card on file, saved payment methods, simplified checkout, immediate access.
- **Integrate upsells into delivery.** When your meal plans mention supplements, people ask about supplements. Reference upsell products naturally in core delivery.
- **Affiliate products can fill Money Model gaps** without operational headache.
- **When your Money Model starts working, your business starts breaking** -- that is part of the game.

## Common Mistakes

- **Trying to build the full Money Model at once.** Build Stage I first, perfect it, then add Stage II. Gym Launch went from $0 to $4,400,000/month in 20 months by following the stages in order.
- **Creating new products instead of new offers.** "Simple scales, fancy fails." Find 100 ways to offer what you already have.
- **Discounting instead of downselling.** Dropping your price devalues your product. Change how they pay (payment plan) or what they get (feature downsell) instead.
- **Making continuity the first offer.** Continuity alone leaves you cash-strapped. It must be the LAST offer in the sequence, after attraction and upsells have generated cash.
- **Treating the anchor as a fake.** If you do not genuinely sell the premium offer, customers see through it. Some customers WILL buy the expensive thing -- be ready to deliver it.
- **Stopping after one upsell.** McDonald's goes from $0.25 profit on a burger to $3.00 with fries, drink, and supersize -- an 11.6x increase. Without upsells, there would be no McDonald's.
- **Ignoring the billing cycle.** Switching from monthly to 4-week billing is 8.3% more revenue for zero work. On 20% margins this is a 41% profit increase. Most businesses never think about it.
- **Neglecting Internal Plays.** Marketing to existing customers runs at 90% margins with zero acquisition cost. Only 5% of businesses do this right.
