## When to Use
- When pricing feels random, commoditized, or based on competitor matching rather than value delivered
- When you need to raise prices but lack conviction or a framework to justify premium positioning
- When designing payment structures, continuity pricing, billing cycles, or downsell sequences for an existing offer

## Core Framework: The Virtuous Cycle of Price

The single most important pricing concept from Hormozi. Pricing is not a number you set -- it is a cycle you enter. You choose the virtuous cycle or the vicious cycle. There is no middle ground.

**The Virtuous Cycle (raise prices):**
Higher price --> higher client emotional investment --> higher perceived value --> better client adherence --> better results --> attracts best clients (least demanding) --> more margin to reinvest in service --> even better delivery --> justifies even higher price --> repeat

**The Vicious Cycle (lower prices):**
Lower price --> lower client emotional investment --> lower perceived value --> worse adherence --> worse results --> attracts worst clients (most demanding) --> thinner margins --> worse delivery --> forced to lower price further --> death spiral

Source: $100M Offers Ch.5, Gym Launch Secrets Ch.7-11

### The Moral Argument for Premium Pricing

> "Those who pay the most, pay the most attention."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers Ch.5

Higher prices are more ethical, not less, because:
- Clients who invest more follow through more, producing better results
- Higher margins let you invest in better service and support
- Your sales team's conviction increases when they can actually deliver on premium promises
- People will not give you their time until they give you their money -- charging more makes them prioritize your service

### The Blind Wine Study

Higher price literally equals higher perceived value. The same wine, labeled as expensive, was rated as tasting better by participants. Price is not just a number -- it changes the actual experience of the product in the buyer's mind.

> "People want to buy expensive things. They just need a reason."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers Ch.5

Source: $100M Offers Ch.5

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Establish Your Pricing Philosophy

Ask the user these diagnostic questions:
1. What is the dream outcome your offer delivers? What is it worth in dollars, time, or status?
2. Are you currently priced based on value delivered, competitor matching, or gut feeling?
3. Can you say your current price out loud without flinching?

Apply the conviction test:

> "Charge as high a price as you can say out loud without cracking a smile."
> -- Dan Kennedy, quoted in $100M Offers Ch.5

If you cannot say your price with complete conviction, either (a) your price is too high for your current proof, or (b) you need more reps delivering results to build conviction. Hormozi sold $42,000/year programs to gym owners earning $35,000/year -- his conviction was built on 33 in-person turnarounds averaging $42,000 in additional sales in 21 days.

Source: $100M Offers Ch.5

### Step 2: Apply Niche Pricing Escalation

The same product can be priced 100x higher through specificity. Each level of niching allows a price increase because the prospect thinks "this was made exactly for me."

| Specificity Level | Example | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Generic | Time Management | $19 |
| Industry | Time Management for Sales Professionals | $99 |
| Sub-niche | Time Management for B2B Outbound Sales Reps | $499 |
| Hyper-niche | Time Management for B2B Outbound Power Tools Sales Reps | $2,000 |

> "You want to be 'the guy' who services 'this type of person' or solves 'this type of problem.' And even more niched: 'I solve this type of problem for this specific type of person in this unique counter-intuitive way that reverses their deepest fear.'"
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers Ch.4

Walk the user through niching their offer down at least 2-3 levels from where they currently sit. The core content can be nearly identical -- the specificity commands the premium.

Source: $100M Offers Ch.4

### Step 3: Set the Price Target

The goal is not to be slightly above market:

> "There is no strategic benefit to being the second cheapest in the marketplace, but there is for being the most expensive."
> -- Dan Kennedy, quoted in $100M Offers Ch.5

The target is to be so much higher that the consumer thinks "there must be something entirely different going on here." That creates a category of one with monopoly profits. Price at 3-10x the highest competitor and use the price difference itself as a signal of superior value.

Apply the Value Equation to justify the premium:

**Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)**

For each variable, document how the offer performs:
- Dream Outcome: What specific result do they get? Frame in terms of status -- how will others perceive their achievement?
- Perceived Likelihood: What proof exists (case studies, track record, guarantees)?
- Time Delay: How fast do they see results? Fast beats free.
- Effort & Sacrifice: How much work must they do? DFY approaches zero effort and command the highest prices.

Source: $100M Offers Ch.5-6

### Step 4: Design Payment Architecture

#### Payment Plan Downsell Process (7 Steps)

When a prospect wants the offer but resists the price, walk down this sequence. Never just drop the price -- that is discounting, not downselling. Downsells are trades.

1. **Reward paying in full** -- offer a discount for prepayment. Present the price with interest included, then frame prepay as the discount (same math, feels better)
2. **3rd-party financing** -- credit card, financing partner, layaway
3. **Half now, half later** -- split into two payments
4. **Temperature check** -- "On a scale of 1-10, how interested are you?" If 7 or below, pivot to Feature Downsells instead
5. **Three payments** -- spread across three installments
6. **Evenly spread payments** -- ask "When do you get paid?" and align with paychecks. Use Seesaw Downselling: "Giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" then frame full payment as the best deal
7. **Free Trial with Penalty** -- they try free if they meet your terms; if they skip requirements, they get charged

Billing cadence data from 14,000 businesses:
- Monthly billing = 10.7% monthly churn
- Quarterly billing = 5% monthly churn
- Annual billing = 2% monthly churn

Always start high (fewer, bigger payments) and work down.

Source: $100M Money Models, Payment Plan Downsells chapter

#### The 4-Week Billing Cycle Hack

> Bill every 4 weeks instead of monthly. There are 13 four-week cycles in a year vs 12 months -- an 8.3% revenue increase for no extra work. On 20% margins this skyrockets annual profit by 41%.

Hormozi states this tip alone has made him millions in pure profit. Additionally, lower more frequent billing debits are preferable -- a weekly $39 charge barely registers on a bank statement while a monthly $168 charge is noticed and reconsidered.

Source: $100M Money Models, Continuity Discount Offers; Gym Launch Secrets Ch.7-11

#### The Processing Fee Hack

> "Add five words: 'Plus a 3% processing fee.' In my life, I've never had anyone not buy because of a processing fee. But 3% added to your topline for no extra work goes straight to your bottom line."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Money Models

On a 10% profit margin business, adding 3% to topline adds 30% to profit. Then offer to waive it if they provide a second form of payment (backup for failed charges), which reduces involuntary churn.

Source: $100M Money Models

### Step 5: Design Continuity Pricing

Use the Continuity Pricing Multiplier to control what percentage of customers choose recurring vs one-time:

| Standalone Price (as multiple of monthly) | % Who Choose Continuity |
|---|---|
| 1.33x | 50% |
| 1.66x | 60% |
| 2.00x | 70% |
| 2.33x | 80% |
| 2.66x | 90% |

Key principle: People pay 33% more to avoid continuity. So charge 33% more for one-time and half will still buy it.

Continuity should be the LAST offer in your sequence. At $50/month you can get 40 out of 100 to buy vs 10 out of 100 at $1,000 one-time -- but continuity on its own leaves you cash-strapped.

> "You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Money Models

Source: $100M Money Models, Continuity Bonus Offers and Continuity Discount Offers chapters

### Step 6: Build the Pricing Announcement Strategy

#### Pipeline Cleaning via Price Increase

Before raising prices, notify your entire pipeline:

> "Never raise your prices without letting people know. It shows a position of strength and will give you a nice little influx of cash."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers

Template: "Starting [date], our [offer] is increasing from [current] to [new price] to match the increased value we are now delivering. If you have been thinking about joining, now is the time to lock in the current rate."

This converts fence-sitters, creates genuine urgency, and signals market strength.

Source: $100M Offers Ch.5

### Step 7: Apply the Never-Discount Rule

> "Never discount the main offer. It teaches your customers that your prices are negotiable. Adding bonuses to increase value to close the deal is far superior to cutting prices."
> -- Alex Hormozi, $100M Offers Ch.14

When facing price resistance:
- Add bonuses instead of discounting (each bonus matched to a specific objection)
- Use Feature Downsells (remove features from highest to lowest value -- people see the value in what was removed)
- Use Payment Plan Downsells (change how they pay, not what they get)
- Never say: "I can give you 20% off if you sign today." This trains every future customer to negotiate and erodes pricing power permanently.

> "Any fool can sell a product by offering it for a discount. It takes great marketing to sell the same product for a premium."
> -- Rory Sutherland (CMO of Ogilvy), quoted in $100M Offers Ch.6

Source: $100M Offers Ch.14, $100M Money Models

## Key Principles

- **The Virtuous Cycle of Price is the foundation.** Higher price = better clients = better results = more margin = better service = higher price. Enter this cycle deliberately. ($100M Offers Ch.5, Gym Launch Secrets Ch.7-11)
- **"Those who pay the most, pay the most attention."** Premium pricing is the moral choice because it drives better adherence and better outcomes. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **"There is no strategic benefit to being the second cheapest in the marketplace, but there is for being the most expensive."** Cheap signals low value. Expensive creates its own category. ($100M Offers Ch.5, Dan Kennedy)
- **Price changes perception, not just economics.** The blind wine study proves higher price literally equals higher perceived quality. People want to buy expensive things -- they just need a reason. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **Niche Pricing Escalation: same product, 100x price.** Each level of specificity allows a price increase because the prospect thinks "this was made for me." ($100M Offers Ch.4)
- **Demand is Fractal (80/20).** One-fifth of prospects will pay five times the price. Selling to fewer people at higher prices is more profitable and creates more demand. ($100M Offers Ch.11)
- **Never discount -- add bonuses or downsell.** Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable. Bonuses put you in a position of strength. ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **Bill every 4 weeks, not monthly.** 13 cycles vs 12 months = 8.3% more revenue. On 20% margins, 41% more profit. ($100M Money Models)
- **Add a 3% processing fee.** No one has ever not bought because of a processing fee. On 10% margins, it adds 30% to profit. ($100M Money Models)
- **The person who needs the exchange less has the upper hand.** True pricing power comes from genuinely not needing the sale. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **"Charge as high a price as you can say out loud without cracking a smile."** Practice saying your price until you can do it with complete confidence. (Dan Kennedy, quoted in $100M Offers Ch.5)

## Output Structure

Produce the following for the user:

```
PRICING STRATEGY

1. PRICING PHILOSOPHY
   - Current pricing diagnosis (competitor-based, cost-based, or value-based)
   - Virtuous Cycle assessment (which cycle are they in?)
   - Conviction test result (can they say the price without flinching?)

2. NICHE PRICING ESCALATION
   - Current niche level
   - 2-3 deeper niche levels with projected price at each
   - Recommended niche + price point

3. PREMIUM PRICE TARGET
   - Value Equation analysis (all four drivers)
   - Price target (3-10x highest competitor)
   - Price justification narrative

4. PAYMENT ARCHITECTURE
   - Full-pay price (with prepay discount)
   - Payment plan options (using 7-step downsell)
   - Billing cycle recommendation (4-week vs monthly vs quarterly vs annual)
   - Processing fee implementation
   - Continuity pricing (using multiplier table)

5. PRICE INCREASE STRATEGY
   - Pipeline cleaning announcement template
   - Timeline for implementation
   - Bonus stack for overcoming resistance (never discount)

6. CONVICTION PLAN
   - Proof points needed to say the price without cracking a smile
   - Moral argument for the premium (how higher price helps clients)
```

## Common Mistakes

- **Competing on price instead of value.** If prospects can compare your offer to alternatives and think "these are the same, I will buy the cheaper one," you are commoditized. A Grand Slam Offer differentiates so you sell on value, not price. ($100M Offers Ch.3)
- **Discounting instead of adding bonuses or downselling.** Discounting trains customers that prices are negotiable. Use bonuses (position of strength) or feature/payment downsells (change what they get or how they pay). ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **Setting continuity pricing without the multiplier ratios.** Random continuity pricing leaves money on the table. Use the data: 2x standalone-to-monthly ratio gets 70% choosing continuity. ($100M Money Models)
- **Monthly billing when 4-week billing is available.** The 8.3% revenue increase requires zero additional work or value delivery. ($100M Money Models)
- **Lowering prices when sales are slow.** This enters the vicious cycle. Instead, increase value, add proof, improve conviction, or niche further. ($100M Offers Ch.5, Gym Launch Secrets)
- **Raising prices without telling your pipeline first.** Always announce price increases -- it converts fence-sitters and shows strength. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **Feeling guilty about premium pricing.** This is the opposite of ethical. Higher prices drive better client results. Those who pay the most, pay the most attention. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
- **Being the second cheapest.** There is a strategic benefit to being the most expensive. There is zero strategic benefit to being second cheapest. You are in no-man's land. ($100M Offers Ch.5)
