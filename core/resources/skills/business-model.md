## When to Use

- The user has a product or service but no deliberate sequence of offers around it -- they sell one thing at one price and wonder why growth stalls
- The user needs to figure out how to make customer acquisition self-funding (Client-Financed Acquisition) so cash never limits scaling
- The user wants to increase revenue per customer without creating new products -- "it is less about having 100 products and more about having 100 offers for the same product" ($100M Money Models, Section VI)

## Core Framework

### The Money Model Framework

A Money Model is a deliberate sequence of offers designed to increase how many customers you get, how much they pay, and how fast they pay it. A good Money Model makes more profit from one customer than it costs to get and service many customers in the first 30 days, removing cash as a limiter to scaling.

> "Simple scales. Fancy fails." -- $100M Money Models

**The fundamental equation:**

```
Money Model = Attraction Offers + Upsell Offers + Downsell Offers + Continuity Offers
```

**The four offer types:**

1. **Attraction Offers** turn strangers into customers using free or discounted entry points. "People look for one thing and buy another by accident all the time. Attraction Offers get them to do it on purpose." ($100M Money Models, Attraction Offers Introduction)
2. **Upsell Offers** get existing customers to spend more -- more, better, or different solutions. "The thing you sell the most isn't always the thing you make the most profit on." McDonald's goes from $0.25 profit on a burger to $3.00 with fries, drink, and supersize -- an 11.6x increase. ($100M Money Models, Upsell Offers Introduction)
3. **Downsell Offers** turn nos into yeses by changing how they pay or what they get. "They said no to THIS offer, not ALL offers." ($100M Money Models, Downsell Offers Introduction)
4. **Continuity Offers** keep people buying with ongoing value for ongoing payments. "You can shear a sheep for a lifetime, but you can only skin it once." ($100M Money Models, Continuity Offers Introduction)

### The Growth Multiplier Formula

```
Growth = (Customer Value Multiplier) x (Customer Count Multiplier) x (Speed Multiplier)
```

- 2x each = 8x growth
- 3x each = 27x growth

### Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA)

```
If (30-day gross profit per customer) >= (Cost to acquire customer)
Then: unlimited scaling potential
```

### LTGP:CAC Ratio

```
LTGP:CAC = (All revenue from customer over lifetime - All costs to deliver) / (All costs to acquire that customer)
Target: >= 3:1
```

### The Pie Equation

```
Hypothetical Max Members = New sign-ups per month / Monthly churn rate
```

### The Wedding Cake Model

Every tier has Icing (front-end cash) and Cake (recurring revenue). Driven by two principles: accommodate the buying curve (let high-spenders spend more) and maximize buying curves per customer (tap into different spending wallets).

### Internal Plays (Sprinkles)

Marketing campaigns to existing customers costing $0 in acquisition, running at 90% margins.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Stage I: Get Cash (Attraction Offers)
1. Identify core offer
2. Design an Attraction Offer (5 proven types: Win Your Money Back, Giveaways, Decoy, Buy X Get Y Free, Pay Less Now or Pay More Later)
3. Calculate CFA math
4. Perfect before moving to Stage II

### Stage II: Get More Cash (Upsell + Downsell)
5-8. Design Upsell Offers (Classic, Menu with 4 tactics, Anchor, Rollover)
9-11. Design Downsell Offers (Payment Plan 7-step, Feature Downsell, Trial With Penalty)

### Stage III: Get The Most Cash (Continuity)
12. Continuity Bonus Offer with Pricing Multiplier
13. Continuity Discount Offer
14. Waived Fee Offer
15. 4-Week Billing Cycle (8.3% revenue increase, 41% profit increase on 20% margins)
