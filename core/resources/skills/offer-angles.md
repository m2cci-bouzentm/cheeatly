## When to Use

- Your ads or content are fatiguing and response rates are declining, but the core offer still works.
- You need multiple advertising angles for the same product to test across platforms, audiences, or seasons.
- You want to systematically generate angles instead of relying on creative inspiration.

## Core Frameworks

### 1. What-Who-When Angle Matrix ($100M Leads, Ch. 10)

Every ad angle is a combination of three dimensions. Combining all three produces dozens of unique angles from a single offer.

**WHAT -- 8 Value Elements (4 pairs of opposites):**

| Positive | Negative (opposite) |
|---|---|
| Dream Outcome | Nightmare (what happens if they don't act) |
| Perceived Likelihood | Risk (what could go wrong) |
| Speed | Time Delay (how long pain continues) |
| Ease | Effort & Sacrifice (what they must endure) |

**WHO -- Stakeholder Perspectives:**

The customer themselves PLUS everyone whose opinion affects their status: spouse, kids, parents, colleagues, boss, friends, rivals, competitors.

> "People are primarily status driven -- show how your product changes how others treat your customer." -- Hormozi

**WHEN -- Three Time Horizons:**

- **Past**: What has already gone wrong? What have they already tried and failed?
- **Present**: What are they struggling with right now?
- **Future**: What will happen (good or bad) depending on their decision?

**How to generate angles:** Pick one WHAT element, one WHO perspective, one WHEN horizon. That is one angle. A single offer with 8 value elements x 8 stakeholders x 3 time horizons = 192 possible angles.

**Example -- Weight loss offer:**
- Dream Outcome + Spouse + Future = "Imagine your partner's face when you walk out in that dress this summer"
- Nightmare + Kids + Present = "Your kids see you out of breath climbing the stairs every single day"
- Ease + Boss + Past = "Remember dreading the company retreat because nothing fit? Never again"

### 2. Seasonal Offer Wrapping ($100M Offers, Ch. 16)

The core offer and fulfillment never change. Only the wrapping paper changes.

> "We are not changing the actual offer. We are only changing the wrapping paper." -- Hormozi

**The principle:** Rename the same offer with a seasonal or event-based wrapper to make it feel fresh. Each wrapper gets new creative, new copy, and a real deadline.

**Example -- Same 6-week fitness challenge, 12 months of angles:**

| Month | Wrapper Name |
|---|---|
| January | New Year New You 6-Week Challenge |
| February | Valentines Lovers Promo |
| March | Sexy By Spring Special |
| April | Fools in Love Promo |
| May | Summer Shred Kickoff |
| June | Beach Body Bootcamp |
| July | Independence Day Reset |
| August | Back-to-School Body Blitz |
| September | Fall Into Fitness |
| October | Scary Strong Challenge |
| November | Gratitude Gains Program |
| December | Holiday Hustle Sprint |

**Use the M-A-G-I-C formula to name each wrapper:**
- **M**agnetic reason why (the seasonal hook)
- **A**vatar (who it is for)
- **G**oal (dream outcome)
- **I**nterval (time duration)
- **C**ontainer word (Challenge, Blueprint, Bootcamp, Sprint, Intensive, Accelerator)

> "Use 3-5 components. The shorter and punchier the better. Rhyme and alliterate when possible but do not force it." -- Hormozi

### 3. Fatigue Change Order ($100M Offers, Ch. 16)

When response rates drop, change elements in this exact order. Each level is cheaper and less disruptive than the next.

| Priority | What to Change | Example |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Creative / images | New photos, new video hooks, new thumbnails |
| 2 | Body copy | Rewrite the supporting text, different proof points |
| 3 | Headline / wrapper | New name using M-A-G-I-C, seasonal rebrand |
| 4 | Duration / interval | 6-week to 21-day, 90-day to quarterly |
| 5 | Enhancer / discount | Add a bonus, change the incentive structure |
| 6 | Monetization structure | Payment plans, pricing model (LAST RESORT) |

> "This one concept will get you leads forever. The same core offer with a new name can get 2x, 3x, or 10x the response rate." -- Hormozi

**Rule:** Never jump to changing the offer itself. Exhaust levels 1-5 first. The actual product and fulfillment stay identical.

### 4. Cocktail Party Effect Call Outs ($100M Leads, Ch. 10)

Your ad must get the right person to notice it before anything else matters. Use the cocktail party effect -- people hear their own name in a noisy room.

> "If they never notice your ad, nothing else matters." -- Hormozi

**Four types of verbal call outs:**

1. **Labels**: Directly name the audience. "Clark County Moms." "Gym Owners." "SaaS Founders doing $50K MRR."
2. **Yes-Questions**: Ask something only your target answers yes to. "Do you wake up to pee more than once a night?" "Spending more than $10K/month on ads?"
3. **If-Then Statements**: "If you run over $100K/month in ads, we can save you 20%." "If your team is over 50 people, this will cut onboarding time in half."
4. **Ridiculous Results**: Lead with an outcome so specific it demands attention. "Massage studio books out two years in advance." "Dentist adds $40K/month without a single new patient."

**Nonverbal call outs:** Contrast (pattern interrupt), likeness (show someone who looks like the avatar), scene-setting (show the environment they live in).

> "LOCAL AREA + TYPE OF PERSON is still one of my all-time favorite ways to get attention. Chino Hills Residents who want to lose weight." -- Hormozi

### 5. How I vs How To Content Framing ($100M Leads)

The same angle framed two ways has dramatically different reception.

> "When you tell your experience, you are sharing value. When you tell a stranger what to do, it's hard to avoid coming off preachy or arrogant." -- Hormozi

| How To (preachy) | How I (sharing) |
|---|---|
| How To Build a 7-Figure Agency | How I Built My 7-Figure Agency |
| This is the best way to generate leads | My favorite way to generate leads |
| 5 Steps To Close More Deals | 5 Things I Changed That Doubled My Close Rate |
| How To Write Better Ads | How I Went From 0.5% to 4.2% CTR |

**Rule:** "How I" is bulletproof -- no one can question your experience. "How To" invites criticism from everyone who disagrees. Default to "How I" especially when you are early in building authority.

### 6. More Better New Optimization ($100M Leads, "Core Four on Steroids")

Before generating new angles, exhaust three levers in order.

> "Most businesses think they have saturated their market when they have only saturated their effort on one method, one platform, in one way." -- Hormozi

**Always in this order:**

1. **More**: Are you doing enough volume? "What is stopping you from doing 10x what you are currently doing?" Double inputs to double outputs. Most people dramatically underestimate required volume.
2. **Better**: Find the constraint (biggest drop-off point in your funnel). Test one variable per week per platform. Do not test multiple things simultaneously.
3. **New**: Only after exhausting More and Better. Expand in this sub-order:
   - New placements on current platform
   - New platforms entirely
   - New Core Four activities (add cold outreach if you only do content, etc.)

### 7. Niche-Specific Angles via Starving Crowd Criteria ($100M Offers, Ch. 4)

The same offer niched down commands radically different pricing and response rates. Each niche level is a new angle.

> "You want to be 'the guy' who services 'this type of person' or solves 'this type of problem.' And even more niched: 'I solve this type of problem for this specific type of person in this unique counter-intuitive way that reverses their deepest fear.'" -- Hormozi

**Niche Pricing Escalation example:**

| Specificity Level | Offer | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Generic | Time Management Course | $19 |
| Industry | Time Management for Sales Professionals | $99 |
| Sub-industry | Time Management for B2B Outbound Sales Reps | $499 |
| Micro-niche | Time Management for B2B Outbound Power Tools Sales Reps | $2,000 |

**Before niching, validate four Starving Crowd indicators:**
1. **Massive Pain** -- They desperately need what you offer. The degree of pain = the price you can charge.
2. **Purchasing Power** -- They can afford what you charge.
3. **Easy to Target** -- Associations, groups, lists, channels where they gather.
4. **Growing** -- The market is expanding, not contracting.

> "A starving crowd beats everything. Pick a market with pain, money, targeting ease, and growth." -- Hormozi

### 8. Category of One Positioning ($100M Offers, Ch. 2-3)

The ultimate angle is one that cannot be compared to any competitor.

> "A Grand Slam Offer is one that cannot be compared to any other product or service... It lets you sell in a 'category of one' where the purchasing decision is between your product and nothing." -- Hormozi

**How to achieve Category of One:**
- Combine offer elements so uniquely that no competitor has the same stack.
- Price so far above the market that prospects think "there must be something entirely different going on here."
- Use the anti-comparison as the angle itself: "This is not a [commodity category]. This is [unique framing]."

> "The same business doing the same fulfillment work can appear to have a totally different product simply by using a Grand Slam Offer instead of a commodity offer." -- Hormozi

**The math:** 2.5x more responses x 2.5x more conversions x 4x higher price = 22.4x more cash collected from the same market with the same fulfillment.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Audit the Current Offer

Before generating angles, confirm the offer itself is solid:
- What is the dream outcome?
- What is the core mechanism (how does it work)?
- What proof exists (case studies, numbers, testimonials)?
- What is the current positioning?
- Which angles have already been tested and fatigued?

### Step 2: Run the What-Who-When Matrix

Generate at least 24 angles (pick 3 WHAT elements x 4 WHO perspectives x 2 WHEN horizons):

For each combination, write one headline and one opening line.

### Step 3: Apply Cocktail Party Call Outs

For each top angle from Step 2, write four versions:
1. Label version ("Attention [specific avatar]...")
2. Yes-Question version ("Do you [specific pain]?")
3. If-Then version ("If you [qualifier], then [promise]...")
4. Ridiculous Result version ("[Specific client] achieved [specific result]...")

### Step 4: Frame as How I (not How To)

Convert the best angles into experience-based framing. Replace prescriptive language with personal narrative.

### Step 5: Create Seasonal Wrappers

Map the top 4-6 angles to upcoming calendar events, seasons, or industry milestones. Name each using M-A-G-I-C.

### Step 6: Niche Down the Winners

Take the 3 best-performing angles and create niche-specific versions at progressively deeper specificity levels.

### Step 7: Build the Testing Queue Using Fatigue Change Order

Organize all generated angles into a testing queue following the fatigue change order:
1. New creative for existing winning angles first
2. New body copy variations second
3. New headline/wrapper variations third
4. Only change duration, enhancers, or monetization if 1-3 are exhausted

## Key Principles

- **"We are not changing the actual offer. We are only changing the wrapping paper."** The fulfillment stays the same. Only the front-end presentation rotates. ($100M Offers, Ch. 16)
- **"If they never notice your ad, nothing else matters."** Call outs beat clever copy. Get the right person to stop scrolling before you try to persuade them. ($100M Leads, Ch. 10)
- **"People are primarily status driven -- show how your product changes how others treat your customer."** Frame benefits through the eyes of the people whose opinion matters to the buyer. ($100M Leads, Ch. 10)
- **"The pain is the pitch."** You do not need to explain your solution if you articulate their pain more accurately than they can themselves. ($100M Offers, Ch. 4)
- **"Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain."** Negative angles (nightmare, risk, time delay, effort) often outperform positive angles (dream outcome, likelihood, speed, ease). ($100M Offers, Ch. 12)
- **"When you tell your experience, you are sharing value. When you tell a stranger what to do, it's hard to avoid coming off preachy or arrogant."** Default to "How I" framing. ($100M Leads)
- **"Most businesses have NOT saturated their market -- they have saturated their effort."** Before creating new angles, verify you have done enough volume with existing ones. ($100M Leads)

## Output Structure

When this skill runs, produce:

1. **Offer Audit Summary** -- Current positioning, dream outcome, proof, and fatigued angles.
2. **What-Who-When Angle Grid** -- Minimum 24 unique angles with headlines and opening lines, organized by value element.
3. **Call Out Variations** -- Top 6 angles rewritten as Labels, Yes-Questions, If-Then, and Ridiculous Results (24 variations).
4. **How I Reframes** -- Top angles converted from prescriptive to experience-based framing.
5. **Seasonal Wrapper Calendar** -- Next 6-12 months of wrapper names using M-A-G-I-C formula with real deadlines.
6. **Niche Escalation Ladder** -- Top 3 angles niched down through 3-4 specificity levels with corresponding price points.
7. **Testing Queue** -- All angles prioritized using the fatigue change order, with clear instructions on what to test first.

## Common Mistakes

- **Changing the offer when the angle is the problem.** Exhaust the fatigue change order (creative -> body -> headline -> duration -> enhancer -> monetization) before touching the core offer. Most people skip straight to restructuring.
- **Writing "How To" when you should write "How I."** Prescriptive framing invites criticism. Experience framing is bulletproof. Especially dangerous when you lack established authority.
- **Only using positive angles.** Negative angles (nightmare, risk, time delay, effort/sacrifice) often outperform positive ones. Fear of loss is stronger than desire for gain. Test both sides.
- **Generic call outs.** "Attention business owners" calls out nobody. "Phoenix-area dental practices doing $80K+/month" stops the right person mid-scroll.
- **Testing multiple variables at once.** Test one thing per week per platform. If you change the headline AND the creative simultaneously, you learn nothing.
- **Skipping the volume check.** Before generating new angles, ask: "What is stopping me from doing 10x what I am currently doing?" The answer is usually nothing. More volume with existing angles beats clever new angles at low volume.
- **Niching too late.** A generic offer at $19 and a micro-niche offer at $2,000 can have identical fulfillment. The angle IS the niche. Do not wait until the product is different -- the framing alone justifies the premium.
