# Skill: CLOSER Sales Conversation Framework

## Purpose
Guide the user through a complete sales conversation using Hormozi's CLOSER formula -- the 6-step framework forged from over 4,000 one-on-one closes. This skill covers the full arc from opening a conversation to reinforcing the decision, including objection handling with specific named closes.

The skill must:
- adapt the CLOSER framework to the user's context (sales call, job interview, freelance pitch, client discovery)
- walk through each step sequentially, gathering information before moving to the next
- help the user craft specific language for each step
- prepare objection responses using Hormozi's actual techniques
- output a complete conversation playbook the user can rehearse and use

The skill should feel like a sales coach preparing you for a specific conversation, not a generic script generator.

---

## When to Use
- Preparing for a sales call, demo, or closing conversation
- Preparing for a job interview where you need to "sell" yourself
- Structuring a freelance discovery call or client pitch
- Handling objections in any high-stakes conversation
- Re-engaging a prospect who went cold or wants to cancel (exit interview re-sell)

## Foundation: The Three Transfers of Sales
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5

Before any sales conversation, you must check your own readiness. Every sale requires three transfers:

1. **Transference of belief in the product** -- you genuinely believe your product/service works
2. **Transference of confidence** -- you believe the prospect can reach their goals
3. **Transference of conviction** -- you believe the prospect can overcome their circumstances

> "If you do not like selling, it means you do not like helping people make decisions to help themselves." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**The Conviction Close:** Your conviction must be stronger than the prospect's skepticism. This cannot be faked -- it comes from having delivered results enough times that you have absolute certainty.

> "You must outwork your self-doubt. You must be so confident in your delivery, because you have done it so many times, that you know that this person will succeed." -- Gym Launch Secrets

Hormozi sold $42,000/year programs to gym owners who made $35,000/year -- as a kid in his twenties telling grown men he would help them make more money. His conviction was built on 33 successful in-person gym turnarounds averaging $42,000 in additional sales in 21 days.

**Self-check before the conversation:**
- Do I genuinely believe this will work for them? If not, fix your product first.
- Do I believe they specifically can succeed? If not, identify why and address it.
- Do I believe their circumstances are surmountable? If not, you will unconsciously agree with their objections.

## Pre-Conversation Setup: Sales Environment Checklist
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5

A sales system creates leverage and scale. Script your entire environment so that when you are not there, the chips are stacked in your team's favor.

> "When every prospect goes through the same environment and the same conversation, you get consistent, predictable outcomes." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Environment checklist:**
- **Greet within 10 seconds by name** -- immediate personal recognition
- **Stacked sign-in sheets** -- previous sign-in sheets visible so the prospect sees how many others have signed up (social proof)
- **Testimonial wall in waiting area** -- before/after photos, reviews, success stories visible while they wait
- **Pre-sell questionnaire** -- intake form that gets the prospect thinking about their problem and goals before you sit down
- **Shoulder-to-shoulder seating** -- sit next to the prospect, never across a table (eliminates adversarial positioning)
- **Visible stack of signed contracts** -- casually placed where the prospect can see that others have already committed
- **Extra chairs** -- signal that this is a busy, in-demand operation

> "Do not wing it. Do not let each salesperson develop their own style. Do not sit across a table from the prospect. Do not have an empty, sterile sales area with no social proof." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Handling early price questions:**

When a prospect asks "How much does it cost?" before the CLOSER process begins, redirect:

> "The answer to 'How much does it cost?' is 'It depends on your goal.'" -- Gym Launch Secrets

Most people think all solutions in your category are interchangeable. It is the salesperson's job to educate and break those beliefs before presenting price.

> "Do not hand someone a pricing sheet and let them walk around comparing. Josh did this with a walk-in unicorn who looked at the clipboard, looked around, set it down, and was never seen again." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Template:** "Great question -- it depends on your goal. Let me understand what you are trying to accomplish first, and then I can tell you exactly what it would take to get you there."

## High-Ticket Note: The Setter-Closer Model
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5 -- "The Longer the Runway, the Bigger the Takeoff"

For high-ticket sales ($2,000+), use a two-step sales process:

1. **Setter** -- qualifies the prospect over the phone and collects a $50-$100 nonrefundable consultation deposit
2. **Closer** -- meets the prospect in person for the full CLOSER conversation

> "The more time and effort invested in selling before the prospect arrives, the bigger the potential payoff." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Setter requirements:**
- Confirm the decision-maker will be present at the in-person meeting
- Ensure the prospect brings their payment method
- Edify the closer throughout -- position them as a demigod gatekeeper
- Use the same CLOSER script -- the only difference is how you present the price at the end

> "If you do not close the deposit, there is a 90% chance you will not close in person." -- Gym Launch Secrets

This model is not required for lower-ticket sales, but the principle applies: the longer the runway (qualification, pre-sell, deposit), the bigger the takeoff (close rate on premium offers).

## Core Framework: The C-L-O-S-E-R Formula
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5-6 and Bonus chapter

### Step 1: C -- Clarify Why They Are There

Establish the GAP -- what they want that they have not achieved.

**Key questions:**
- What made you come in / reach out / take this call today?
- What is your goal?
- Why did you sign up / apply / respond?

**What you are doing:** Understanding the gap between where they are and where they want to be. You need specific, quantifiable answers -- not vague aspirations.

**Application by context:**
- **Sales call:** "What specifically prompted you to book this call today?"
- **Job interview:** "What does this role need to accomplish that it is not accomplishing today?"
- **Freelance pitch:** "What is the specific outcome you are looking for from this project?"
- **Client discovery:** "What brought you to look at solutions right now -- what changed?"

---

### Step 2: L -- Label Them With the Problem

Recap their current state versus their desired state with specific numbers. Get their stats and confirm the gap.

**What you are doing:** Making the problem concrete, measurable, and undeniable. When you label the problem accurately, the prospect feels understood.

> "The pain is the pitch. If you can articulate the pain a prospect is feeling accurately, they will almost always buy what you are offering." -- $100M Offers

> "The point of good writing is for the reader to understand. The point of good persuasion is for the prospect to feel understood." -- $100M Offers

**Template:** "So right now you are at [current state] and you want to be at [desired state]. Is that right?"

**Application by context:**
- **Sales call:** "So right now you are doing $X/month and you want to get to $Y/month. That is a gap of $Z. Is that accurate?"
- **Job interview:** "So the team currently handles X but the goal is Y, and that gap is what this role needs to close?"
- **Freelance pitch:** "Your current conversion rate is X% and you need it at Y% -- that difference represents $Z in revenue?"
- **Client discovery:** "You are currently spending X hours/week on this process and you want it down to Y?"

Spend time here. Most salespeople rush past the problem to get to the solution. The highest-converting approach is spending most of the time articulating the prospect's problem so deeply that they feel truly seen.

---

### Step 3: O -- Overview Their Past Pain and Attempts

Review what they have tried before and why it failed. Categorize each attempt and show which components were missing.

**Key questions:**
- What have you tried before to solve this?
- How did that go?
- What worked and what did not?
- Why do you think it did not work?

**What you are doing:** Two things simultaneously:
1. Building empathy by showing you understand their history of frustration
2. Setting up the Belief-Breaking Formula (see below) to explain why past approaches failed and why yours is different

**The Belief-Breaking Formula:**
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6 -- picked up from Linh Trinh

A five-step persuasion framework for shattering false beliefs about why they failed:

1. **Say what they believe:** "So you believe [their current approach] is the right way to do this?"
2. **Say why it is wrong:** "The challenge with that approach is [specific reason it fails]."
3. **Say what is right:** "What actually works is [your approach]."
4. **Say WHY it is right:** "Because it [specific mechanism that produces results]."
5. **Show proof:** Third-party stats, testimonials, case studies, wall of results.

**Example (from the book):**
1. "So you believe cheap front-end offers work?"
2. "This does not provide cash flow to market or acquire customers."
3. "Use a high-ticket front-end acquisition."
4. "It will allow you to spend whatever you need to get people in profitably."
5. Show proof from similar businesses that made the switch.

**Application by context:**
- **Sales call:** Walk through their history of tools/services/approaches, identify the missing component in each
- **Job interview:** "What approaches has the team tried before? What was the gap?" Then position your skillset as the missing component
- **Freelance pitch:** "You mentioned you tried [agency/DIY/in-house]. What specifically fell short?" Then break the belief about why those failed
- **Client discovery:** Map their attempts to a framework showing which critical elements were absent each time

---

### Step 4: S -- Sell Them the Vacation (Not the Plane Flight)

> "Sell them the vacation, not the flight. No one wants to hear about TSA lines, baggage claim, delays, layovers, or time changes. They want to hear about Maui." -- Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6

**What you are doing:** Painting the picture of the destination -- the dream outcome -- not describing your process, features, or methodology. Sell in the imaginary realm.

> "No one wants your membership, your software, your consulting hours. They want the outcome -- the weight loss, the revenue growth, the peace of mind. Stop selling the vehicle and start selling the destination." -- $100M Offers

**Frame benefits in terms of status change:**
> "When writing copy, you can make it that much more powerful by talking about how other people will perceive the prospect's achievement." -- $100M Offers

Do not just state the benefit -- paint a picture of the social reaction they will receive. Connect the dots by describing the status change others will witness.

**Use the Value Equation to structure the vacation:**
> Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)

- **Dream Outcome:** Paint the specific result they will have
- **Perceived Likelihood:** Show proof it will work for THEM specifically (case studies, track record)
- **Time Delay:** Show how quickly they will see results ("You will see the first results within [timeframe]")
- **Effort & Sacrifice:** Show how easy it is ("All you need to do is [minimal action]")

**Application by context:**
- **Sales call:** "Imagine six months from now, your pipeline is full, your team is closing at 30%, and you are turning away clients because you are at capacity."
- **Job interview:** "In six months, this team will be shipping twice as fast, the backlog will be clear, and your VP will be asking what changed."
- **Freelance pitch:** "When this project is done, your landing page will convert at 8%, your cost per lead will drop by half, and you will have a system you can replicate."
- **Client discovery:** "Picture your Monday morning: instead of scrambling through spreadsheets, you open one dashboard and everything is there."

### Step 5: E -- Explain Away Their Concerns

> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6 and Bonus chapter (Obstacle Overcomes)

**The Universal Pattern: Always Agree, Then Ask a Question**

> "Every obstacle overcome should start with 'I totally agree' or 'I totally understand,' then transition to 'Let me ask you a question...' Never say 'but' or 'I disagree.' The prospect needs to conclude on their own that they should proceed." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**There are only THREE real objection categories:**
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.6

1. **Stalls** -- "I need to think about it"
2. **Decision-Maker** -- "I need to talk to my [spouse/partner/boss]"
3. **Price** -- "I cannot afford it"

Everything else (technical questions, fitness concerns, implementation worries) is NOT a real objection -- these are questions from people who still want to buy. Answer them casually and move on.

> "If you are constantly battling people, the problem is earlier in the process, not at the close." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Master 2-3 overcomes for each category. Stack them like punching combinations -- each one dents the prospect's apprehension.**

---

#### General Decision-Helping Framework
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5

These three questions can be used across ANY objection category to help a prospect through their decision-making process. The books present them as a general framework for all stalls, not just one category:

> "People hate making decisions because they hate making mistakes. Your responsibility is to convince them that the REAL mistake is NOT making a decision." -- Gym Launch Secrets

1. Do you think this will help you achieve [goal]?
2. Do you have the money or access to it?
3. Am I the person you want supporting you through this?

> "If all three are yes, the decision is made. You are not pressuring -- you are providing the framework they need to decide." -- Gym Launch Secrets

Use these three questions whenever a prospect is stuck -- regardless of whether the surface objection is a stall, a decision-maker deferral, or a price concern. If all three answers are yes, the only remaining barrier is fear of deciding.

---

#### Objection Category 1: STALLS ("I need to think about it")

**Close 1: The Rocking Chair Close**
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter

> "When we say 'I am going to think about it,' we do not go home, sit in our rocking chairs, and stare at the wall, do we? You are going to leave here, then your mind is going to get filled with buying groceries and cooking dinner and doing laundry... and four days later you will catch a glimpse in the mirror, remember why you came, and think 'I am too busy for that right now.' In those two seconds, you will have made the decision."

**Template:** "I totally understand wanting to think it over. But let me ask you -- when you leave here, are you going to go home and sit in a rocking chair staring at the wall thinking about this? Or is life going to get busy, and four days from now you will catch yourself thinking about it for two seconds before getting pulled away? That two-second moment IS the decision. So let us make it right now while you have all the information."

> "The honest truth is that decisions don't take time, they take information, because thinking is instantaneous." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Close 2: The Fully Informed Decision Frame**
> Source: $100M Offers

> "I'm not asking you to decide yes or no today... I'm asking you to make a fully informed decision, that is all." -- $100M Offers

**Template:** "I am not asking you to decide yes or no right now. I am asking you to make a fully informed decision. The only way to do that is on the inside, not the outside. Get on the inside, see if everything is true, then decide whether to keep it. Whether it is 29 minutes or 29 days from now, if you are not happy, I am not happy."

---

#### Objection Category 2: DECISION-MAKER ("I need to talk to my spouse/boss")

**Pattern:** Agree, then use agreement stacking.

**Template:** "I totally understand. And [spouse/boss] sounds like a smart person. Let me help you present this to them clearly."

Then use the three decision-helping questions from the general framework above to walk them through their own position. If they answer yes to all three, the conversation with their decision-maker becomes much simpler -- they are presenting a decision they have already made, not asking for permission.

**Close:** "If all three are yes, then the only question for [spouse/boss] is whether they support you investing in yourself -- and from what you have told me about them, they absolutely do."

---

#### Objection Category 3: PRICE ("I cannot afford it")

**Close 1: The Identity Swap Close**
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Bonus chapter

Tell the story of identity transition -- when a person changes who they are, their spending priorities change with them.

> "Right now what we are talking about is you going from a [person] who is unsatisfied with [situation] to a [person] who IS satisfied. Part of that new identity comes with new priorities for where you spend your money. We only need to vote YES once, so you might as well do it now." -- Gym Launch Secrets

> "We tend to vote with our dollars about the things we care about. Because we can SAY we care about X, Y, and Z, but what REALLY matters at the end of the day is where we spend our money." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**Close 2: The Best-Case / Worst-Case Close**
> Source: $100M Offers

**Template:** "Best case, you get [dream outcome] and [additional benefit]. Worst case, you tell me I suck, I write you a check, and you get [X weeks/months] of free [service]. Both options are risk-free. But the only thing guaranteed not to help you is walking out of here today."

> "The only thing guaranteed not to help you is walking out of here today." -- $100M Offers

**Close 3: The A/B Close**
> Source: $100M Money Models

Instead of asking "Do you want this?" (yes/no), ask "Do you prefer A or B?" Either answer is a purchase.

> "Instead of asking if customers wanted to buy a product, yes or no, we ask which product they prefer: A or B. Either choice results in an upsell." -- $100M Money Models

**Examples:** "Chocolate or vanilla?" "Start tomorrow or Monday?" "Morning or afternoon?" "Cash or card?"

Add a nudge: "I like the chocolate" or "Most people love Tuesday sessions."

> Hormozi asked "Do you like chocolate or vanilla?" instead of "Do you want protein powder?" and sold 20 customers in a row after previously selling zero.

Use the A/B Close after price resistance has been addressed -- once they are leaning in, give them a choice between two purchase options rather than a choice between buying and not buying.

**Close 4: Payment Plan Downsell (if genuinely cannot afford full price)**

Do NOT discount. Add bonuses or change terms.

> "Never discount the main offer. It teaches your customers that your prices are negotiable. Adding bonuses to increase value to close the deal is far superior to cutting prices." -- $100M Offers

If they genuinely cannot pay in full, use the 7-step Payment Plan Downsell from $100M Money Models:
1. Reward paying in full with a discount
2. Offer 3rd-party financing, credit card, or layaway
3. Half now, half later
4. Temperature Check (1-10 scale, need 8+)
5. Split into three payments
6. Evenly spread payments
7. Free Trial with Penalty

> "No payment plan will satisfy a customer who doesn't want the thing. So, make sure the person actually wants your thing before putting more effort into selling it." -- $100M Money Models

**Simplified Alternative: Seesaw Downselling**
> Source: $100M Money Models

A simpler downsell process, especially useful for less experienced salespeople:

> "This frames the payment plan as negative and highlights the benefits of prepaying." -- $100M Money Models

**Template:** "Would you rather have giant monthly payments or tiny ones?" (They say tiny.) "It normally costs XXX. If you prepay today, you get a huge discount and zero monthly payments. That work?" Then: "The more you put down now, the lower your monthly payments."

Use this as a faster alternative to the full 7-step process when the salesperson is less experienced or the deal size does not warrant the full downsell sequence.

#### The Temperature Check (Diagnostic Tool)
> Source: $100M Money Models

Use after two consecutive rejected offers or downsells.

**Template:** "On a scale from 1 to 10, how bad do you want this?"

- **If 8+:** "Awesome. Don't worry. We're gonna figure out a way to make this happen for you." Keep downselling.
- **If 7 or below:** "Why not a 10?" or "What would a 10 look like?" Then pivot to a different product or reconfigure features.

---

#### Bonus Stacking on Objections
> Source: $100M Offers Ch.14

Ask for the sale first. If they say yes, reveal bonuses as a wow moment. If they say no, match each bonus to their specific objection.

**Template:** "I totally understand [their objection]. That is exactly why we created [Bonus Name] -- it is a [$X] value that [solves their specific concern]. I will include that for you. Does that sound fair enough?"

> "Never discount the main offer. Adding bonuses puts you in a position of strength and goodwill rather than weakness." -- $100M Offers

After each bonus or downsell, ask **"Deal?"** or **"Fair enough?"**

> "After Each Downsell, Ask 'Deal?' Or 'Fair Enough?' This works astonishingly well. Fewer people will see you change the offer for them and then say 'No that's not fair.'" -- $100M Money Models

---

#### The Prescriptive Assumed Close
> Source: Gym Launch Secrets Ch.5

The books call this "the most powerful type of sale." Instead of asking if the prospect wants your solution, prescribe it as a person of authority.

> "The most powerful type of sale is a prescriptive assumed close -- where a person of authority prescribes what the customer needs and they simply comply. This is how pharmaceutical companies have operated since the dawn of time: a doctor prescribes pills and you buy them. Price does not matter." -- Gym Launch Secrets

**How to use it:** Position your recommendation as what is included in their plan, not as an optional add-on. Do not ask "Would you like to add...?" Instead, prescribe: "For your program, you will need X, Y, and Z. Here is how we set that up."

> "Do not present supplements, add-ons, or upsells as optional extras on a menu. Instead, prescribe: 'For your program, you will need X, Y, and Z. Here is how we set that up.'" -- Gym Launch Secrets

**When to use:** After the prospect has agreed to the core offer and you are layering in additional components. The Prescriptive Assumed Close works best from a position of established authority -- after you have demonstrated expertise through the CLOSER process.

**Template:** "Based on everything you have told me, here is what your program looks like: [core offer], plus [component A] and [component B]. Let me get your [ID/card] so we can get you started."

---

### Step 6: R -- Reinforce the Decision

Prevent buyer's remorse and backouts with immediate reinforcement.

**What you are doing:** Making them feel like they made the best decision of their life, right now, before doubt creeps in.

**Reinforcement actions:**
- Handwritten invitation or welcome note
- Personal welcome call from the owner/founder/senior person
- Welcome swag or gift
- Immediate next step scheduled (BAMFAM -- Book A Meeting From A Meeting)
- Fast emotional win within 48 hours

> "Always try and incorporate short-term, immediate wins for a client. They just need to know they are on the right path and that they made the right decision trusting you." -- $100M Offers

> "Never leave a customer in no man's land. They should always know what happens next." -- $100M Money Models

> "A customer should know the next time they see you -- and why -- before they leave." -- $100M Money Models (via Sharran)

**Application by context:**
- **Sales call:** Send a personalized welcome video, schedule onboarding for tomorrow, deliver a quick win in the first session
- **Job interview:** Send a thank-you note with a specific 30-day action plan, reference something personal from the conversation
- **Freelance pitch:** Send the signed proposal with a welcome packet, schedule the kickoff call for the next business day, deliver a quick audit or insight within 24 hours
- **Client discovery:** Summarize the conversation in writing, confirm next steps, provide one immediate piece of value they can use today

## Step-by-Step Process

When a user asks for help with a sales conversation, walk through these steps:

1. **Identify the context:** What type of conversation? (Sales call, interview, pitch, discovery, exit interview)
2. **Check the Three Transfers:** Does the user have genuine belief, confidence, and conviction? If not, help them build it with evidence.
3. **Determine ticket size:** If $2,000+ and in-person is feasible, recommend the Setter-Closer Model.
4. **Gather information for each CLOSER step:**
   - C: What is the prospect's situation? Why are they engaging now?
   - L: What are the specific numbers (current state vs. desired state)?
   - O: What has the prospect tried before? What failed?
   - S: What is the dream outcome in vivid, status-framed language?
   - E: What are the likely objections? Prepare 2-3 overcomes per category.
   - R: What reinforcement actions will happen immediately after the close?
5. **Craft specific language** for each step tailored to the user's product/service and prospect
6. **Prepare objection overcomes** using the named closes above
7. **Build the conversation playbook** as a complete document

## Key Principles
> All sourced from Gym Launch Secrets and $100M Offers

- **Selling is serving.** If you do not sell them, you are failing them -- you know they need help and you let them walk away unchanged.
- **The pain is the pitch.** Spend 80% of the conversation articulating their pain, 20% presenting your solution.
- **Sell the vacation, not the plane flight.** Describe the destination, never the process.
- **Always agree first, then ask a question.** Never say "but" or "I disagree."
- **Decisions do not take time, they take information.** Thinking is instantaneous.
- **There are only three real objections.** Stalls, decision-maker, and price. Everything else is a question, not an objection.
- **Stack closes like punching combinations.** Each one dents apprehension.
- **Scarcity and urgency must be REAL.** Your conviction must be genuine -- the human mind is very good at picking out BS.
- **Never discount -- add bonuses instead.** Discounting teaches customers your prices are negotiable.
- **People hate making decisions because they hate making mistakes.** Your job is to show that NOT deciding is the bigger mistake.
- **The set is the close.** If you close the small commitment (deposit, meeting, application), you will close the big one.
- **Conviction beats skepticism.** "You must outwork your self-doubt."
- **Those who pay the most, pay the most attention.** Higher price equals higher commitment equals better outcomes.
- **"It depends on your goal."** Never answer a price question with a number before establishing the gap.
- **Prescribe, do not offer.** The most powerful sale is when authority prescribes and the customer complies.

## Output Structure

When this skill completes, produce a **CLOSER Playbook** document containing:

1. **Context** -- type of conversation, who the prospect is, what you are selling
2. **Three Transfers Self-Check** -- belief, confidence, conviction assessment
3. **Pre-Conversation Setup** -- environment checklist: greeting protocol, sign-in sheet social proof, testimonial wall, pre-sell questionnaire, shoulder-to-shoulder seating, visible signed contracts, extra chairs, and the "it depends on your goal" redirect for early price questions
4. **High-Ticket Note** -- if applicable, Setter-Closer Model with deposit collection and decision-maker confirmation
5. **CLOSER Script** -- specific language for each of the 6 steps:
   - Clarify questions
   - Label statement (current vs. desired with numbers)
   - Overview questions and Belief-Breaking sequence
   - Vacation pitch (vivid outcome description with status framing)
   - Objection overcomes (2-3 per category with specific close names)
   - Reinforcement plan (immediate actions post-close)
6. **Named Closes Ready** -- Rocking Chair, Fully Informed Decision, Identity Swap, Best-Case/Worst-Case, A/B Close, Prescriptive Assumed Close, and decision-helping questions prepared for the specific context
7. **Bonus Stack** -- bonuses matched to likely objections
8. **Temperature Check Plan** -- what to do at each score level
9. **Downsell Path** -- 7-step Payment Plan Downsell or simplified Seesaw Downsell depending on salesperson experience
10. **Follow-Up Sequence** -- if they do not close today

## Common Mistakes

> Based on what Hormozi explicitly warns against in Gym Launch Secrets and $100M Offers

- **Explaining your process instead of painting the outcome.** Nobody cares about your methodology. They care about Maui.
- **Arguing with objections.** Never say "but" or debate. Agree, then ask a question.
- **Treating questions as objections.** Technical concerns ("will this work with my setup?") are buying signals, not resistance. Answer casually and move on.
- **Dumping all bonuses in the initial pitch.** Save them for after the first ask -- use them to match specific objections.
- **Discounting instead of adding value.** "It teaches your customers that your prices are negotiable and puts you in a position of weakness."
- **Letting prospects leave undecided.** Not deciding IS a decision -- the decision to stay unchanged.
- **Faking scarcity or urgency.** Prospects can feel the difference. Make it real to yourself first.
- **Skipping the Overview step.** Without understanding past failures, you cannot break beliefs or position your solution as different.
- **Rushing to the close.** "The longer you delay the ask, the bigger the ask you can make."
- **Sitting across a table from the prospect.** Sit shoulder-to-shoulder. Never create an adversarial physical setup.
- **Handing someone a pricing sheet and walking away.** The price must be presented in context of the value and the gap.
- **Winging it.** "Make all conversations exactly the same for consistent outcomes." Script your environment and your framework.
- **Asking "Do you want this?" instead of "A or B?"** A yes/no question invites rejection. A choice between two options invites a purchase.
- **Presenting add-ons as optional extras.** Prescribe what they need from a position of authority. Do not ask "Would you like to add...?"
- **Trying to close $2,000+ on a cold walk-in.** Use the setter-closer model -- collect a deposit first, then close in person.
