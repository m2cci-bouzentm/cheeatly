## When to Use

- When a user needs to generate leads for a business, freelance practice, job search, or any offering and does not know where to start or how to scale
- When a user has an offer built (via hormozi-offer or similar) but zero or insufficient leads seeing it
- When a user wants to diagnose why their current advertising is underperforming, identify which channels to add, or increase volume on existing channels

## Core Framework

### The Core Four Advertising Methods

There are exactly four ways any person can let other people know about the stuff they sell. These combine two audience types (warm/cold) with two communication types (1-to-1/1-to-many):

```
                    1-to-1 (Private)         1-to-many (Public)
Warm Audience       Warm Outreach            Post Free Content
Cold Audience       Cold Outreach            Run Paid Ads
```

> "It's hard to be poor with leads bangin' down your door." -- $100M Leads, opening line

**Warm audiences** are people who gave you permission to contact them: friends, followers, past customers.
**Cold audiences** are strangers who have not given you permission.

Start with warm outreach, add content, then cold outreach, then paid ads. All four compound together -- each method makes the others more effective.

> "Advertising lets you have a terrible product and still make money. It lets you be terrible at sales and still make money. It lets you make a ton of mistakes and still make money." -- $100M Leads, Section I

### The Value Equation (Applied to Lead Generation)

Every offer, lead magnet, and advertisement should be evaluated through the Value Equation from $100M Offers:

```
Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)
```

Maximize the top two. Minimize the bottom two. If the denominator reaches zero, value becomes infinite. This applies to free offers as much as paid ones.

> "Grand Slam Offers work for free stuff as much or better than paid stuff." -- $100M Leads, Lead Magnets chapter

### Engaged Leads, Not Just Leads

A lead is just a person you can contact. An **engaged lead** is a person who has shown interest in the stuff you sell. Engaged leads are the true output of advertising. The entire purpose of the Core Four is to generate engaged leads.

> "Words matter because they affect how we think and what we do. Pursuing engaged leads (not just leads) changes your entire strategy." -- $100M Leads, Section I

### LTGP:CAC Ratio

The fundamental efficiency metric for all advertising:

```
LTGP:CAC = (All revenue from customer over lifetime - All costs to deliver) / (All costs to acquire that customer)

Target: >= 3:1
```

Every struggling portfolio company Hormozi invested in had LTGP:CAC below 3:1. Two levers exist: lower CAC (better ads) or increase LTGP (better business model). Costs can only approach zero but revenue can go to infinity -- eventually focus on making more per customer.

> "The difference between winners and losers is usually how much they make per customer, not how cheaply they acquire them." -- $100M Leads, Paid Ads Part II

### Client-Financed Acquisition (CFA)

Structure offers so new customers pay you back within 30 days, covering acquisition cost. This unlocks infinite scaling because you never run out of cash.

```
If (30-day gross profit per customer) >= (Cost to acquire customer)
Then: Client-Financed Acquisition = unlimited scaling potential
```

> "Every company I scaled past $1M/month in the first 12 months used this approach." -- $100M Leads, Paid Ads Part II

Use credit cards for 30-day interest-free float. Add upsells, order bumps, and immediate offers to increase 30-day cash collected.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Identify Your Starting Position

Ask the user:

1. Do you have more **time** than money, or more **money** than time?
   - More time: Start with warm outreach and content creation
   - More money: Start with cold outreach or paid ads
2. Do you already have a warm audience (contacts, followers, past customers)?
   - Yes: Begin with warm outreach immediately
   - No: Build one through content while doing cold outreach
3. What is your current LTGP:CAC ratio?
   - Below 3:1: Fix the business model (increase LTGP) before scaling ads
   - Above 3:1: Scale advertising spend
   - Unknown: Calculate it before proceeding

### Step 2: Create a Lead Magnet (7 Steps)

A lead magnet is a complete solution to a narrow problem that, once solved, reveals another problem solved by the core offer.

1. **Pick a narrow problem** to solve for the right customer
2. **Choose how to solve it**: Reveal Problems, Samples/Trials, or One Step of a Multi-Step Process
3. **Choose delivery method**: Software, Information, Services, or Physical Products
4. **Test the name** (headline, image, subheadline) -- the packaging matters more than anything
5. **Make it easy to consume** -- multiple formats (video, text, audio)
6. **Make it darn good** -- give away the secrets, sell the implementation
7. **Clear CTA** with reasons to act now (scarcity, urgency, or any reason at all)

> "Give away the secrets. Sell the implementation." -- $100M Leads, Lead Magnets chapter

> "If you are scared of giving away secrets, the alternative is worse: people who might have become customers think you only have sucky fluff and buy from someone else." -- $100M Leads

### Step 3: Activate Core Four Method #1 -- Warm Outreach

**Volume target**: 100 reach outs per day.

**Who to contact**: Compile all contacts from phone, email, and social media.

**How to reach out**: Use the ACA Framework (Acknowledge-Compliment-Ask):
- **Acknowledge**: Restate what they said in your own words to show active listening
- **Compliment**: Tie what they shared to a positive character trait
- **Ask**: Ask another question that leads closer to your offer topic
- Repeat 3-4 exchanges before making any offer

**The ask**: Do not ask if they want to buy. Ask if they know anyone.

> "Do you know anybody who is [struggling with X] looking to [dream outcome] in [time period]? I am taking on five case studies for free because that is all I can handle." -- $100M Leads, Warm Outreach chapter

**First Five Free Strategy**: Offer first five customers the product for free in exchange for feedback, testimonials, and referrals. Then increase price by 20% every five customers.

> "You probably suck (for now). People are far more forgiving when you haven't charged anything." -- $100M Leads

**Re-engagement**: Use Dean Jackson's 9-Word Email for dormant contacts: "Are you still looking to [4 word desire]?"

> "This message is money for getting leads to engage. And it is among the first things I do when I invest in a new business." -- $100M Leads

**Follow-up**: Contact each person up to 3 times across different platforms. Email first, then call, then DM, then text. Wait 3-6 months and repeat -- circumstances change.

**Math**: 500 reach outs per week at 1% conversion with a $400 product = $104,000 annually.

### Step 4: Activate Core Four Method #2 -- Post Free Content

**Volume target**: 100 minutes per day making content. Release at least one piece daily.

**The Content Unit (Hook-Retain-Reward)**:

Every piece of content must follow this atomic structure:

- **Hook**: Get them to notice. Use interesting topics, strong headlines, and platform-matched formatting. Seven headline attention triggers: recency, relevancy, celebrity, proximity, conflict, unusual, ongoing.
- **Retain**: Keep them consuming. Use lists, steps, and stories to embed unresolved questions. String together multiple content units for longer formats.
- **Reward**: Satisfy the reason they consumed it. Match or exceed expectations set by the hook.

> "There is no such thing as too long, only too boring." -- $100M Leads, Content chapter

> "When you have written your headline, you have spent 80 cents of your advertising dollar." -- David Ogilvy, quoted in $100M Leads

**Positioning -- "How I" vs "How To"**: When starting, talk about what you have done (sharing) instead of what people should do (preachy). "How I Built My Agency" beats "How To Build An Agency."

> "When you tell your experience, you are sharing value. When you tell a stranger what to do, it's hard to avoid coming off preachy or arrogant." -- $100M Leads

**Puddles-Ponds-Lakes-Oceans**: Start with the narrowest possible topic (puddle) where you can be the undisputed expert. Expand as authority grows. Do not compete with people who have better track records in broad topics.

**Monetization -- Give Until They Ask**:

> "The moment you start asking for money is the moment you decide to slow down growth." -- $100M Leads

Two monetization strategies:
- **Integrated offers**: Ads within content
- **Intermittent offers**: Occasional ask posts

Give in public, ask in private. Let the audience self-select when ready. When you give enough, people start asking YOU.

> "People are always waiting for you to ask for money -- when you do not, they trust you more." -- $100M Leads

**The compounding asset**: Content disappears in days, but the audience you build keeps growing. Volume of output directly correlates with audience growth.

### Step 5: Activate Core Four Method #3 -- Cold Outreach

**Volume target**: 100 reach outs per day.

**Three problems to solve**:

1. **Build a targeted list**: Scrape, buy, or manually compile lists of prospects who match your avatar
2. **Personalize to look warm**: Research 1-3 personal facts about each prospect before reaching out. Reference their recent content, business, or achievements.
3. **Increase volume**: Use automation for list-building and follow-up sequencing, but keep messages personal

> "People like people who like them. Even if someone doesn't know you, they'll give you more time if you know something about them." -- $100M Leads

**Big Fast Value**: Instead of offering a "game planning session" (code for sales call), offer something people would normally pay for, completely free. The goal is to blow their minds in under 30 seconds.

> "I didn't say so good they should pay for it. I said stuff they actually pay for. Big difference." -- $100M Leads

**Follow-up sequence**: Email first (most will not respond). Then call. Then DM. Then text. Then wait 3-6 months and repeat.

> "Quantity has a quality all of its own." -- Napoleon, quoted in $100M Leads opening of cold outreach chapter

### Step 6: Activate Core Four Method #4 -- Run Paid Ads

**Volume target**: 100 minutes per day making ads, 100 days straight running them.

**Ad Structure -- Three Components**:

1. **Call Outs** (get noticed): Use the Cocktail Party Effect. Four verbal callout types:
   - **Labels**: "Clark County Moms," "Gym Owners"
   - **Yes-Questions**: "Do you wake up to pee more than once?"
   - **If-Then Statements**: "If you run over $100K/month in ads, we can save you 20%"
   - **Ridiculous Results**: "Massage studio books out two years in advance"

   > "If they never notice your ad, nothing else matters." -- $100M Leads

2. **Value Elements** (get interest): Use the What-Who-When Framework:
   - **WHAT** (8 elements): Dream Outcome, Nightmare (opposite), Perceived Likelihood, Risk (opposite), Speed, Time Delay (opposite), Ease, Effort/Sacrifice (opposite)
   - **WHO** (stakeholder perspectives): Customer + spouse, kids, parents, colleagues, bosses, friends, rivals, competitors
   - **WHEN** (3 time horizons): Past, present, and future for each perspective
   - Combining all three creates dozens of unique advertising angles

   > "People are primarily status driven -- show how your product changes how others treat your customer." -- $100M Leads

3. **CTAs** (tell them what to do): Be clear, not clever. Spell out exactly what to do next.

**Three Phases of Scaling**:

- **Phase 1 -- Track Money**: Set up tracking before spending a dollar. If you do not track, you get cleaned out.
- **Phase 2 -- Lose Money**: Budget 2x the 30-day cash collected per customer to test each ad. Expect to lose 9 out of 10 times.
- **Phase 3 -- Print Money**: When ads are profitable, spend as much as you can. Reverse budget from sales goals, not arbitrary amounts. Pad by 20% because ads get less efficient as they scale.

> "When you find a winner, 100x down on it. The number of times you lose is high but the amount is low; wins are few but huge." -- $100M Leads

**Your best free content makes the best paid ads**: If a content piece performs well organically, nine times out of ten it will make a great paid ad.

### Step 7: Apply the Rule of 100

Commit to 100 primary advertising actions every day for 100 days straight. This is the minimum volume commitment that guarantees results.

| Method | Rule of 100 |
|---|---|
| Warm Outreach | 100 reach outs per day |
| Post Content | 100 minutes per day making content |
| Cold Outreach | 100 reach outs per day |
| Paid Ads | 100 minutes per day making ads |

> "Reach. Out. To. One. Hundred. People. Every. Day." -- $100M Leads

**Advanced version -- Open To Goal**: Work until you hit a specific number of outcomes, no matter how long it takes.

> "Give up the idea of doing your best. Instead, do what is required." -- $100M Leads

### Step 8: Scale with More Better New

When you want more leads from any method, there are only three levers. Always exhaust them in this order:

1. **More**: Double your inputs to double your outputs. Most people dramatically underestimate required volume.
2. **Better**: Find the constraint (biggest drop-off point). Test one thing per week per platform.
3. **New**: New placements on current platform, then new platforms, then new Core Four activities.

> "Most businesses have NOT saturated their market -- they have saturated their effort." -- $100M Leads

> "What is stopping you from doing 10x what you are currently doing? Answer that question first." -- $100M Leads

### Step 9: Activate Lead Getters (Scaling Beyond Yourself)

Four types of people who advertise on your behalf, creating leverage:

1. **Customer Referrals**: Lowest cost, exponential growth potential.
   - Build goodwill using the Goodwill Equation: **Goodwill = Value Delivered - Price Charged**
   - Six ways to increase goodwill: (1) sell better customers, (2) set better expectations, (3) get more people better results, (4) make faster wins, (5) keep making stuff better, (6) tell them what to buy next
   - Seven ways to ask for referrals: one-sided benefit, two-sided benefit (Dropbox model), ask right when they buy, use referrals as negotiation chip, run referral events, ongoing referral program, unlockable referral bonuses
   - If referrals exceed churn, your business compounds without other advertising

   > "Do you know why businesses have so few referrals? They never ask for them." -- $100M Leads

   **The One-Customer Thought Experiment**: Imagine you lost all customers but one. All future customers must come from this one person's referrals. How would you treat them? Do that for every customer.

   > "Start acting like the advertising gods will revoke your core four privileges at any moment." -- $100M Leads

2. **Employees**: Train using Document-Demonstrate-Duplicate (3Ds).
   - Document: Write a checklist. Record yourself. Test: can you do an A+ job following only the checklist?
   - Demonstrate: Walk them through step by step.
   - Duplicate: They do it in front of you following the checklist. Fix the checklist, not the person.

   > "If they follow your directions exactly and get the wrong result, still praise them for following directions. Then fix the checklist on the spot." -- $100M Leads

3. **Agencies**: Use them to LEARN new methods, not as permanent outsourced advertising.
   - Set 6-month terms upfront. Pay extra for them to explain their decisions. Train your team alongside them. Switch to consulting once your team beats theirs.

   > "I want to do what you do in my business, but I don't know how. I'd like to work with you for 6 months so I can learn how you do it." -- $100M Leads

4. **Affiliates**: Independent businesses that tell their audience to buy your stuff.
   - Three-tier payout: Tier 1 (25% CAC) for sign-up, Tier 2 (50% CAC) for activation, Tier 3 (100% CAC) for sustained performance. Blended payout averages well below max CAC.
   - Launch using Whisper-Tease-Shout:
     - **Whisper** (Callouts): Create curiosity, keep product mysterious, show behind the scenes. Every 4-6 weeks until 60 days out.
     - **Tease** (Value Elements): Reveal product, make launch date public, show value. Weekly until 14 days out.
     - **Shout** (CTA): Specific actions, pound audience with bonuses, scarcity, urgency. At least twice daily starting 3 days out, every few hours on launch day.

### Step 10: Diagnose Problems Correctly

Three distinct problem types require three distinct solutions:

- **Advertising Problem**: Not enough engaged leads seeing your offer. Solution: increase Core Four volume and variety.
- **Sales Problem**: Engaged leads have the problem and money but are not buying. Solution: fix the sales process (see CLOSER framework).
- **Business Model Problem**: CAC is reasonable but profit is insufficient. Solution: increase LTGP through upsells, retention, and pricing (see Money Model).

> "If your engaged leads have the problem you solve and the money to spend, but they are not buying, you have a sales problem, not an advertising problem." -- $100M Leads

> "The cost to acquire customers between competitors is much closer than you would think -- the difference is how much they make per customer." -- $100M Leads

## Key Principles

- **All advertising problems are volume problems.** Most people do not do half or a third of the required volume. They do 1/17th or 1/1500th. Effort should never be the reason something does not work.

  > "Most people do not do half or a third of the required volume. They do dramatically less." -- $100M Leads, Advertising in Real Life chapter

- **The Size of the Pie Fallacy.** Businesses look at their tiny slice of one platform, see 3-4 competitors, and conclude they saturated their market. In reality, they saturated their effort on one method, one platform, in one way.

- **Give away the secrets, sell the implementation.** Your free stuff should be so good people feel obligated to pay you. The marketplace judges everything you offer, free or not.

- **Content is not the asset, the audience is.** Individual pieces disappear in days, but the audience keeps growing. Volume of output directly correlates with audience growth.

- **People do not have shorter attention spans, they have higher standards.** There is no such thing as too long, only too boring. Streaming proves people binge for hours.

- **Yeses give opportunity, nos give feedback.** Every no is valuable market research that directly improves the next pitch. Rack up failures as fast as you can.

  > "Yeses give me opportunity. Nos give me feedback. Either way, I win." -- $100M Leads

- **Force first wins in 48 hours.** Customers form lasting impressions within the first 48 hours after buying. Deliver progress updates frequently. Break big deliverables into small milestones.

  > "Fast beats free." -- $100M Offers

- **BAMFAM -- Book A Meeting From A Meeting.** Never leave a customer without booking the next appointment. They should always know when they will hear from you next and what happens next.

  > "Never leave a customer in no man's land. They should always know what happens next." -- $100M Leads

- **I am compensated tomorrow for the value I provide today.** Building goodwill is an investment with delayed returns. Play long-term games with long-term people.

- **Everyone is replaceable, especially me.** A business that only works with you in it is a high-paying job, not a valuable asset. The moment others can run it, it becomes worth $10M+.

## Output Structure

The skill should produce a complete lead generation plan with the following sections:

1. **Situation Assessment**: Current channels, volume, LTGP:CAC ratio, and whether the user has an advertising problem, sales problem, or business model problem
2. **Lead Magnet Design**: A specific lead magnet following the 7-step framework, with a tested name, clear delivery method, and CTA
3. **Core Four Activation Plan**: Which of the four methods to start with, specific daily volume targets per method, and the Rule of 100 commitment
4. **Content Strategy** (if applicable): Topics using Puddles-Ponds-Lakes-Oceans positioning, Content Unit structure, and monetization approach (integrated vs intermittent offers, or Give Until They Ask)
5. **Paid Ads Blueprint** (if applicable): Callout types, What-Who-When ad angle matrix, budget allocation across the three phases (Track-Lose-Print)
6. **More Better New Diagnosis**: For each active channel -- are they doing enough volume (More)? Are they testing weekly (Better)? Do they need new placements or platforms (New)?
7. **Lead Getter Roadmap**: Which of the four lead getters to activate first (customers, employees, agencies, affiliates), with specific referral system design using the Goodwill Equation
8. **CFA Check**: Does the current offer structure support Client-Financed Acquisition? If not, what changes are needed to break even within 30 days?
9. **Scaling Timeline**: A phased plan from first leads through all four Core Four methods and lead getters, following the seven levels from $100M Leads roadmap

## Common Mistakes

- **Doing 1/100th the required volume and concluding the method does not work.** Hormozi tested 300 flyers when the mentor tested with 5,000 and deployed 5,000 per day. The right action in the wrong amount still fails.

- **Trying all four methods at once instead of mastering one first.** Start with warm outreach, add content, then cold outreach, then paid ads. Sequence matters.

- **Confusing advertising problems with sales problems or business model problems.** If engaged leads are not buying, fix sales. If CAC is fine but margins are bad, fix the business model. Do not fire the wrong person.

- **Running paid ads without tracking.** Phase 1 is always Track Money. If you do not track, you get cleaned out.

- **Asking people to buy instead of asking if they know anyone.** In warm outreach, ask if they know someone who is struggling with X. Most who say yes actually mean themselves.

- **Making free content that is thinly veiled sales pitches.** Give away so much value people feel obligated to pay. If your free stuff is not good enough, the alternative is worse: prospects think your paid stuff is sucky.

  > "If you struggle to give your stuff away for free, the problem is not price -- it is the hidden costs. The time, effort, and sacrifice required to benefit from it are too high." -- $100M Leads

- **Using agencies as permanent outsourced advertising.** Use them to learn, not to delegate forever. Set 6-month deadlines and train your team alongside them.

- **Neglecting referrals.** Most businesses have few referrals because they never ask. If your product were exceptional, people would already know about it.

  > "If your product were exceptional, people would already know about it. Ask yourself: why are my customers too embarrassed to tell everyone they know about my product?" -- $100M Leads

- **Spending more on ads before fixing LTGP:CAC.** If the ratio is below 3:1, focus on increasing LTGP (better business model) rather than lowering CAC (better ads). Revenue can go to infinity; costs can only approach zero.

- **Giving up before rolling the die enough times.**

  > "You cannot lose if you do not quit." -- $100M Leads, final line
