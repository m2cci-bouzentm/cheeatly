# Skill: Guarantee Designer

## Purpose
Design guarantees that eliminate the prospect's perceived risk -- the single greatest objection in any sale. A guarantee change alone can 2-4x conversion without touching anything else in the offer.

This skill walks through the four guarantee types from $100M Offers Ch.15, proves the math behind why strong guarantees almost always pay off, covers guarantee-adjacent risk reversal frameworks from $100M Money Models and Gym Launch Secrets, and produces a named, stackable guarantee strategy.

---

## When to Use
Activate this skill when:
- An offer exists but conversions are low due to perceived risk
- The user needs to reverse risk for a product, service, or freelance engagement
- Price resistance is high and the user wants to increase perceived likelihood of achievement without lowering price
- The user wants to stack multiple guarantees for maximum persuasive power
- The user is afraid of offering guarantees because of refund fears
- The user needs a trial or earn-back structure that functions as a guarantee

## Core Framework: The Four Guarantee Types

From $100M Offers Ch.15, there are exactly four guarantee types. Each serves a different strategic purpose.

### Type 1: Unconditional Guarantee
No questions asked. Full refund within a specified period.

**How it works:** The customer can request a refund for any reason within the guarantee window. No conditions, no hoops.

**Best for:** Products, digital goods, courses, anything where the cost of fulfillment has already been incurred and a refund does not create additional cost.

**Example:** "Try it for 30 days. If you do not love it for any reason, we will give you every penny back. No questions asked."

**Risk profile:** Highest refund exposure, but highest conversion lift. The math almost always favors it (see Guarantee Math below).

### Type 2: Conditional Guarantee
Refund or continued service tied to the customer completing specific success actions.

**How it works:** The customer gets the guarantee only if they follow the program, complete the steps, attend the calls, or meet other conditions tied to the key actions that produce results.

**Best for:** Services, coaching, programs, consulting -- anywhere client effort determines outcomes.

**Example from the books:** "You will get 20 clients in your first 30 days. If you don't, we keep working with you -- at no additional charge -- until you do. All we ask is that you follow our system, attend the calls, and implement within 48 hours."

**Risk profile:** Lower refund exposure because conditions filter out non-committed buyers. Conditions should be tied to the actions that actually produce results -- this way, anyone who qualifies for the refund has already done the work and gotten results (making refund requests rare).

### Type 3: Anti-Guarantee (Damaging Admission)
All sales are final -- stated explicitly with a compelling reason why.

**How it works:** You announce that there is no guarantee, and you frame the lack of guarantee as proof of the offer's value. The reasoning: the methods are so powerful that once revealed, they cannot be unseen.

**Hormozi quote:** "Since it is so standard to have some sort of guarantee, not having one is attention-worthy."

**Example from the books:** "We are going to show you our proprietary lead generation process -- our exact funnels, ads, and metrics. We are exposing the inner workings of our business. As a result, all sales are final. If you need a guarantee before taking a jump, you are not the type of person we want to work with."

**Best for:** Intellectual property, proprietary methods, mastermind access, anything where the value is in the knowledge itself and cannot be "returned."

**Risk profile:** Zero refund exposure. Paradoxically increases perceived value by signaling extreme confidence.

### Type 4: Implied Guarantee (Performance / Revenue Share)
No explicit guarantee is stated. Instead, the business model itself reverses risk through performance-based pricing or track record.

**How it works:** The business only gets paid when the client gets results (revshare, performance fees, pay-per-result). Alternatively, the track record is so overwhelming that the guarantee is implied by social proof.

**Best for:** Agencies, consultants, service providers willing to bet on their own results. Also works when you have massive social proof (thousands of case studies, public track record).

**Example:** "We only get paid when you get paid. 20% of revenue generated from our campaigns. If we generate nothing, you pay nothing."

**Risk profile:** Revenue risk shifts entirely to the provider. Only viable when you have proven systems and high confidence in results.

## The Guarantee Math Proof

This is the most important section. Most people avoid strong guarantees because they fear refunds. The fear is emotional, not mathematical.

### The Calculation ($100M Offers Ch.15)

**Without strong guarantee:**
- 100 sales
- 5% refund rate = 5 refunds
- Net sales = 95

**With strong guarantee (increases sales 30%, doubles refund rate):**
- 130 sales
- 10% refund rate = 13 refunds
- Net sales = 117

**Result: 23% more net revenue with the stronger guarantee.**

### The Principle
"For the guarantee to not be worth it, the absolute increase in refund percentage would have to fully offset the absolute increase in sales percentage, which almost never happens."

### The Rule of Thumb
A strong guarantee that increases conversions by 30% can afford to more than double the refund rate and still come out ahead. The conversion lift almost always exceeds the refund increase.

### When the Math Breaks
The guarantee math only fails when:
- The product genuinely does not deliver results
- There is no friction or conditions to filter non-serious buyers
- Pay-later cancellation rates exceed 10% -- from $100M Money Models, Pay Less Now or Pay More Later chapter: "If more than 10% of pay-later people cancel, you promised too much, the guarantee conditions are too low, or the price is too high." This threshold applies specifically to pay-later structures, not all refund rates generally.

**Hormozi's personal refund policy:** "If a customer asks for their money back -- give it back. Avoid the headache." Focus energy on the next customer, not fighting refunds.

## The Service Guarantee (Hormozi's Personal Favorite)

This is the guarantee type Hormozi recommends most because it carries zero refund risk while being the most persuasive guarantee possible.

**How it works:** Guarantee the outcome, not a refund. Promise to keep working for the client free of charge until the result is achieved. Add conditions tied to key success actions.

**Hormozi quote:** "Since I have been advising businesses to use this particular guarantee, I have yet to have a single person say a client took them up on it."

**Why it works:**
1. Zero refund risk -- you never give money back
2. Maximum persuasive power -- you are betting your own time on their success
3. Almost nobody takes you up on it -- because the conditions ensure they do the work, and the work produces results
4. It filters for serious buyers -- non-committed people self-select out

**Structure:**
- Specific measurable outcome: "You will get [X result] in [Y timeframe]"
- Conditions tied to success actions: "All we ask is that you [do the things that produce results]"
- Continued service commitment: "If you don't hit that number, we keep working with you at no additional charge until you do"

**Example from the books:** "You will get 20 clients in your first 30 days. If you don't, we keep working with you -- at no additional charge -- until you do. All we ask is that you follow our system, attend the calls, and implement within 48 hours."

## Guarantee Stacking

From $100M Offers Ch.15: "You can stack guarantees." Combining multiple guarantee types addresses different layers of risk and compounds persuasive effect.

**How to apply:** Layer guarantees so each one addresses a different concern. For example, a conditional guarantee addresses "will it work?" while a short-window unconditional guarantee addresses "what if I just don't like it?" The extractions confirm the stacking concept but do not prescribe a rigid methodology -- adapt to your specific offer and the risks your prospect perceives.

**Example from the Offer Enhancement Stack:** "Guarantee specific outcomes like 'double your conversion rate in 90 days or we work free until you do'" combined with scarcity, urgency, and bonuses to create a complete risk-reversal package.

## Guarantee-Adjacent Risk Reversal Frameworks

These frameworks from $100M Money Models and Gym Launch Secrets function as guarantees by reversing customer risk, even though they are structured differently than the four core guarantee types.

### Trial With Penalty ($100M Money Models, Trial With Penalty chapter)

**How it works:** Customers try your product for free so long as they meet your terms. If they skip requirements, they get charged a fee. This is not "see if you like it" -- it is "get it free if you do the stuff that makes you a great customer."

**Key principles from the book:**
- Always get a credit card before explaining fees
- Always sell staying and paying before the trial begins: "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?"
- Make check-ins required as upsell opportunities
- Break fees up per missed criteria rather than one lump sum
- Explain fees AFTER getting card for higher take rate

**Three outcomes from trials:**
1. They like it -- set up automatic billing
2. They hate it -- ask what they would change, get angry at yourself, upsell to higher tier
3. They did not use it -- offer to waive fees if they meet with you

**Why it matters for guarantee design:** Trial With Penalty is a conditional guarantee variant where the "guarantee" is free access contingent on behavior. It filters for serious customers while eliminating all perceived risk for committed buyers.

### Win Your Money Back ($100M Money Models, Win Your Money Back chapter)

**How it works:** Set a goal for the customer and tell them how to reach it. If they reach it, they get their money back or store credit. Generated over $1 billion in sales industry-wide.

**Key principles from the book:**
- Criteria must be easy to track, get customers results, and advertise the business
- Apply store credit over a longer period or toward a more expensive offer (e.g., $600 credit over 12 months = $50/mo discount)
- Make everyone a winner in private -- lower anxiety and increase retention
- The real money comes from people who succeed and have something else to buy
- All meetings and check-ins are opportunities to make more offers

**Hormozi quote:** "We don't get customers to make a sale, we make sales to get customers."

**Why it matters for guarantee design:** This is a guarantee that pays for itself. Customers who earn back their money are proven, engaged buyers who then purchase more. The "guarantee" becomes a customer acquisition and retention engine.

### The Bribe (Gym Launch Secrets, Irresistible Offer Checklist)

**How it works:** Offer the entire front-end program FREE if the customer signs up for a membership today (assumed close). This removes all risk from the immediate purchase decision.

**From the Irresistible Offer Checklist:** "Bribe: Offer the entire front-end program FREE if they sign up for a membership today (assumed close)."

**Why it matters for guarantee design:** The Bribe is a risk reversal tactic that complements the Crazy Guarantee. While the guarantee says "if it doesn't work, no risk," the Bribe says "the thing you're buying right now is free." Together they eliminate both outcome risk and financial risk.

### Pay Less Now or Pay More Later ($100M Money Models, Pay Less Now or Pay More Later chapter)

**How it works:** Give people a choice: pay full price later (with a satisfaction guarantee) OR pay a discounted price now with bonuses. Get their card on file first. This combines delayed payment with satisfaction guarantee to remove all risk from the customer.

**The 10% cancellation threshold:** "If more than 10% of pay-later people cancel, you promised too much, the guarantee conditions are too low, or the price is too high."

**Why it matters for guarantee design:** This structure layers a satisfaction guarantee on top of a delayed payment option, creating a double risk reversal that makes the purchase decision feel completely safe.

### Sell Staying And Paying Before The Trial Begins ($100M Money Models)

**The principle:** "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?" This question must precede any trial or guarantee offer.

**From the book:** "For trial offers, get the commitment to stay long-term BEFORE the trial starts. If they will not agree to stay if results are good, there is no point giving them a trial."

**Why it matters for guarantee design:** Any trial, guarantee, or risk reversal structure should be preceded by this commitment question. Without it, you are giving away free access to people who never intended to become customers. A trial without commitment is just free stuff.

## The Guarantee as a Feature Downsell Lever

From $100M Money Models, Feature Downsells chapter, and the "Remove Value To Show Value" mental model:

The guarantee itself can be used as a negotiation lever. When a prospect objects to price, you can remove the guarantee as a feature downsell -- and this often makes them want the guarantee back.

**How it works:** "If you are okay without the guarantee, I can knock $400 off. If you want the guarantee back, the full package is actually the better deal. Which would you prefer?"

**Why it works:** "People only see the value of something after you threaten to take it away. Removing a guarantee as a feature downsell makes people realize how much they wanted the guarantee -- and they buy the full-price option to keep it."

**Result from the books:** One business tripled close rate from 25% to 75% by downselling without a guarantee -- and more people bought the full-price option with the guarantee.

## Naming Your Guarantee

From $100M Offers Ch.16 (M-A-G-I-C naming) and the principle that every component of your offer should have a benefit-communicating name:

**Hormozi quote:** "Every component of your offer -- the main product, each bonus, each guarantee -- should have a name that communicates the benefit in the title itself. A named component with a dollar value assigned is perceived as dramatically more valuable than the same thing unnamed."

**Naming guidance from the books:**
- Make it memorable -- rhyme or alliterate when you can (from the M-A-G-I-C naming formula)
- The name should communicate who it is for and what it does
- Instead of generic names like "Money-back guarantee," use names that describe the specific outcome

**Examples:**
- Instead of "Meal Plan" --> "Personalized Lick-Your-Fingers-Good Meal Plan" (the same naming principle applies to guarantees)
- Instead of "Money-back guarantee" --> "Triple-Your-Traffic Guarantee"
- Instead of "Satisfaction guaranteed" --> "20-Clients-In-30-Days-Or-We-Work-Free Guarantee"
- Instead of "Risk-free trial" --> "Zero-Risk Test Drive"

## The Best-Case / Worst-Case Close

When presenting guarantees in a sales conversation, use this closing framework from $100M Offers Ch.15:

"Best case you get [dream outcome] and [additional benefit]. Worst case you tell me I suck, I write you a check, and you get [X weeks/months] of free [service]. Both options are risk free. But the only thing guaranteed not to help you is walking out of here today."

**Hormozi quote:** "The only thing guaranteed not to help you is walking out of here today."

**Application examples:**
- **Selling:** "Best case your revenue doubles. Worst case you tell me I suck, I write you a check, and you got six months of free consulting. The only thing that is guaranteed not to help you is doing nothing."
- **Freelance:** "Best case your traffic increases 50%. Worst case you get a full refund and keep all the work I did for you."

## The Crazy Guarantee (Gym Launch Secrets)

From the Irresistible Offer Checklist in Gym Launch Secrets: "Personal satisfaction guarantee removes all risk. Best case you change their life; worst case they worked out for free."

This is the gym-specific application of the guarantee principle. The Crazy Guarantee is one of eight components of the Irresistible Offer Checklist (alongside Pre-Frame, Price Anchor, Splinter Stack, Scarcity, Urgency, Bribe, and Downsell Your Upsell). In the gym context, the satisfaction guarantee eliminates risk because the worst-case scenario -- free workouts -- still has value for the customer.

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Identify the Core Risk
Ask the user:
- What is the single biggest reason prospects say no?
- What outcome are they afraid of not achieving?
- What have they tried before that failed (creating skepticism)?
- What is their worst-case fear about buying?

The answer determines which guarantee type to lead with.

### Step 2: Calculate the Guarantee Math
Walk through the math with the user's actual numbers:
- Current conversion rate (or estimated)
- Current refund rate
- Projected conversion lift with a stronger guarantee (conservative: 20-30%)
- Projected refund rate increase (conservative: doubling)
- Net revenue comparison

Show them that the math almost always favors the stronger guarantee.

### Step 3: Select the Primary Guarantee Type
Based on the business model:
- **Product / digital / course:** Start with Unconditional (easiest, highest conversion lift)
- **Service / coaching / consulting:** Start with Service Guarantee (zero refund risk, maximum persuasion)
- **Intellectual property / proprietary methods:** Consider Anti-Guarantee (frame no-refund as proof of value)
- **Agency / performance work:** Consider Implied/Performance (revshare or pay-per-result)
- **Trial-based / subscription:** Consider Trial With Penalty (free access contingent on behavior)
- **Goal-oriented programs:** Consider Win Your Money Back (earn-back structure that drives retention)

### Step 4: Add Conditions (if applicable)
If using a Conditional or Service Guarantee:
- Tie conditions to the key success actions that actually produce results
- Conditions should be things the prospect would want to do anyway
- Conditions filter non-serious buyers without feeling restrictive
- Example conditions: attend all calls, complete implementation within 48 hours, follow the system for 30 days

### Step 5: Sell Staying And Paying First
Before presenting any trial or guarantee, ask: "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?" Frame the guarantee as the beginning of a relationship, not a one-off test. A trial without commitment is just free stuff.

### Step 6: Design the Stack
Layer guarantees to address different risk layers. The books confirm you can stack guarantees but do not prescribe a rigid formula -- design the stack based on what risks your specific prospects perceive:
- Combine a conditional or service guarantee (outcome risk) with a short-window unconditional guarantee (buyer's remorse risk)
- Add a Win Your Money Back component for goal-oriented offers
- Use the Bribe (front-end program free) to eliminate immediate financial risk
- Each guarantee in the stack addresses a different layer of risk

### Step 7: Name Each Guarantee
Apply the naming principles from Ch.16:
- Outcome-based name that communicates the benefit
- Make it memorable -- rhyme or alliterate
- Distinct from generic "money-back guarantee"

### Step 8: Write the Guarantee Script
Produce the exact language for:
- Sales page / landing page copy
- Sales conversation (using the Best-Case / Worst-Case Close)
- Email / proposal language
- FAQ entry addressing "what if it doesn't work?"

### Step 9: Set the Refund Policy
Define the operational side:
- Refund window (if unconditional)
- Condition verification process (if conditional)
- Continued service terms (if service guarantee)
- Trial penalty structure (if Trial With Penalty)
- Earn-back criteria and credit structure (if Win Your Money Back)
- Follow Hormozi's personal rule: "If a customer asks for their money back -- give it back. Avoid the headache."

## Key Principles

- **Risk is the single greatest objection.** Changing the quality of a guarantee alone can 2-4x conversion. ($100M Offers Ch.15)
- **The guarantee math almost always works in your favor.** 130% of sales with 10% refund rate still nets 23% more revenue than 100% of sales with 5% refund rate. ($100M Offers Ch.15)
- **Service guarantees carry zero refund risk.** Promise to keep working until the result is achieved. "I have yet to have a single person say a client took them up on it." ($100M Offers Ch.15)
- **The anti-guarantee paradoxically increases perceived value.** "Since it is so standard to have some sort of guarantee, not having one is attention-worthy." ($100M Offers Ch.15)
- **Guarantees increase Perceived Likelihood of Achievement** -- the second variable in the Value Equation. "People pay for certainty. A surgeon's 10,000th patient pays far more than the first. Increase through proof, track record, guarantees." ($100M Offers Ch.6)
- **Never discount -- add guarantees and bonuses instead.** Discounting trains clients that your prices are negotiable. ($100M Offers Ch.14)
- **Name your guarantee something memorable.** A named guarantee with a specific outcome is perceived as dramatically more valuable than "money-back guarantee." ($100M Offers Ch.16)
- **Removing the guarantee as a feature downsell makes people want it back.** Use the guarantee as a negotiation lever -- people who see the price without the guarantee often choose to pay more to keep it. ($100M Money Models, Feature Downsells chapter)
- **The 10% pay-later cancellation threshold.** Specifically for pay-later structures: "If more than 10% of pay-later people cancel, you promised too much, the guarantee conditions are too low, or the price is too high." ($100M Money Models, Pay Less Now or Pay More Later chapter)
- **Win Your Money Back generated over $1 billion in sales industry-wide.** Set a goal, let them earn their money back as store credit. The real money comes from people who succeed and have something else to buy. ($100M Money Models, Win Your Money Back chapter)
- **Sell staying and paying before the trial begins.** "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?" A trial without commitment is just free stuff. ($100M Money Models)
- **The Crazy Guarantee principle:** "Personal satisfaction guarantee removes all risk. Best case you change their life; worst case they worked out for free." (Gym Launch Secrets, Irresistible Offer Checklist)

## Common Mistakes

- **Avoiding guarantees out of refund fear.** The math almost always favors stronger guarantees. Run the numbers before deciding. The conversion lift outweighs the refund increase in nearly every case.
- **Using a generic "money-back guarantee" without naming it.** An unnamed guarantee has a fraction of the persuasive power of a named, specific, outcome-based guarantee.
- **Setting conditions that feel punitive rather than supportive.** Conditions should be the actions the prospect would want to do anyway to get results. They should feel like a helpful guide, not a legal trap.
- **Offering trials without selling staying and paying first.** Before any trial or guarantee, ask: "If this program got you the result, will you stay long-term?" Without this, a trial is just free stuff.
- **Fighting refund requests.** Hormozi's personal rule: give the money back and move on. The energy spent fighting refunds is better spent acquiring the next customer.
- **Using the anti-guarantee without a compelling reason why.** "All sales are final" without a frame is hostile. With a frame ("our methods are so powerful that once revealed they cannot be unseen"), it becomes a signal of extreme value.
- **Ignoring the service guarantee for service businesses.** Service businesses default to money-back guarantees when they could offer service guarantees with zero refund risk and higher persuasive power.
- **Discounting instead of strengthening the guarantee.** When price resistance appears, the answer is a better guarantee or more bonuses, never a lower price. "Never discount the main offer. It teaches your customers that your prices are negotiable."
- **Missing the Win Your Money Back opportunity.** For goal-oriented offers, letting customers earn their money back creates a guarantee that drives retention and upsells rather than costing you revenue.
